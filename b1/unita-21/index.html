    Unità 21 – Present Perfect (Real Use)

    Unità 21 – Present Perfect (Real Use)

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità saprai:

    • usare il Present Perfect in modo semplice ma corretto
    • collegare un’azione passata al presente
    • parlare di esperienze, risultati e cambiamenti
    • distinguere tra Present Perfect e Past Simple nei casi più comuni

    🧠 Grammar in context

    Struttura

    👉 subject + have / has + past participle

    • I have worked
    • She has studied
    • They have travelled

    Forme brevi molto comuni:

    • I’ve finished
    • She’s gone out
    • We’ve seen it

    🔑 Usi principali

    1. Esperienze di vita

    Usa il Present Perfect quando parli di un’esperienza e non dici quando.

    • I have been to Spain.
    • She has eaten sushi.
    • We have seen that film.

    2. Risultati nel presente

    L’azione è passata, ma il risultato è importante adesso.

    • I have lost my keys.
    • He has broken his glasses.
    • We have finished our homework.

    3. Cambiamenti nel tempo

    • My English has improved.
    • The city has changed a lot.
    • She has become more confident.

    ⚖️ Present Perfect vs Past Simple

    Present Perfect Past Simple
    esperienza o risultato azione finita nel passato
    non dici quando dici quando
    legame con il presente tempo passato concluso

    Examples

    • I have been to Rome. ✅
    • I went to Rome last year. ✅
    • I have been to Rome last year. ❌

    📚 Core vocabulary

    Past participles frequenti

    • be → been
    • go → gone
    • do → done
    • eat → eaten
    • see → seen
    • write → written
    • make → made
    • take → taken
    • speak → spoken
    • buy → bought

    Signal words

    • already = già
    • just = appena
    • yet = ancora / già (in domande e negative)
    • ever = mai
    • never = mai

    Examples

    • I have already finished.
    • She has just arrived.
    • We haven’t started yet.
    • Have you ever tried Indian food?
    • I have never flown business class.

    🎧 Reading / Dialogue

    A: Have you finished your report yet? B: Yes, I’ve just sent it.

    A: Have you ever worked with international clients? B: Yes, I have. I’ve worked with clients from Germany and Spain.

    A: Really? When did you start? B: I started two years ago.

    🗣️ Speaking

    Ripeti:

    • I’ve just finished.
    • We haven’t decided yet.
    • She has already called.
    • Have you ever tried it?

    Ora completa con idee tue:

    • I have already ...
    • I have just ...
    • I haven’t ... yet.
    • I have never ...

    Fai domande:

    • Have you ever travelled alone?
    • Have you finished your work yet?
    • Have you ever met a famous person?

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi 8 frasi usando il Present Perfect.

    Usa almeno queste parole:

    • already
    • just
    • yet
    • ever
    • never

    Esempio:

    I’ve already done my homework.
    I haven’t called my friend yet.
    Have you ever visited London?

    🔊 Pronunciation

    Nel parlato naturale spesso si usano le contrazioni:

    • I have → I’ve
    • you have → you’ve
    • she has → she’s
    • we have → we’ve

    Attenzione anche a:

    • finished → /ˈfɪnɪʃt/
    • worked → /wɜːrkt/
    • lived → /lɪvd/

    ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

    ❌ I have went to London.
    ✅ I have gone to London.

    ❌ I have done it yesterday.
    ✅ I did it yesterday.

    ❌ I am finished.
    ✅ I have finished.

    ❌ Have you ever been in New York?
    ✅ Have you ever been to New York?

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa con il participio corretto:

    1. I have ___ (eat) lunch.
    2. She has ___ (go) home.
    3. We have ___ (see) that film.
    4. He hasn’t ___ (finish) yet.
    5. They have ___ (write) the email.

    Scegli il tempo corretto:

    1. I have seen / saw him yesterday.
    2. Have you ever been / did you go to Ireland?
    3. She has just arrived / just arrived five minutes ago.
    4. We didn’t finish / haven’t finished yet.

    🧠 Think & Speak

    Parla di:

    • 3 cose che hai già fatto oggi
    • 2 cose che non hai ancora fatto
    • 2 esperienze che hai fatto nella tua vita

    🚀 Mini task

    Parla per 1–2 minuti:

    👉 What have you done this week?

    Prova a usare:

    • already
    • just
    • yet
    • ever / never

    English becomes more natural when you connect the past to the present.

    Unità 21 – Present Perfect (Real Use)

    🎯 Lesson Goals

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • usare il Present Perfect in modo naturale per parlare di esperienze, risultati, cambiamenti e periodi non conclusi
    • distinguere con sicurezza tra Present Perfect e Past Simple in base al significato, non solo alla regola meccanica
    • usare in modo corretto e fluente already, just, yet, ever, never, for, since
    • sostenere una conversazione B1 con follow-up naturali, domande di approfondimento e riferimenti temporali coerenti
    • evitare gli errori più comuni degli italofoni, soprattutto con participi irregolari, traduzione letterale e scelta sbagliata del tempo verbale

    Questa è una delle unità centrali del passaggio da A2 a B1. Il punto non è soltanto “sapere la regola”, ma capire quando il presente è ancora coinvolto e quando invece l’evento appartiene a un passato chiuso.

    🧠 Core concept: past event, present relevance

    Il Present Perfect collega un fatto passato a una situazione presente. In altre parole, l’azione è avvenuta prima di adesso, ma è ancora rilevante adesso.

    👉 Formula di base:

    subject + have / has + past participle

    • I have finished the report.
    • She has changed jobs.
    • We have never tried that app.

    Nel parlato reale userai spesso le contrazioni:

    • I’ve finished.
    • She’s changed jobs.
    • We’ve never tried it.

    1. Main uses of the Present Perfect

    A. Experiences

    Parli di cose che hai fatto nella vita senza specificare un momento preciso.

    • I’ve been to Japan twice.
    • Have you ever worked abroad?
    • She has never driven on the left.

    Qui il focus non è quando, ma se l’esperienza esiste nel tuo bagaglio personale.

    B. Results visible now

    L’azione è passata, ma il risultato è presente.

    • I’ve lost my keys. (so I can’t open the door now)
    • We’ve finished the presentation. (it is ready now)
    • He’s broken his phone. (it doesn’t work now)

    C. Change over time

    Parli di evoluzione, sviluppo o trasformazione.

    • My pronunciation has improved a lot.
    • The company has grown بسرعة.
    • This area has become more expensive.

    D. Unfinished time periods

    Il periodo di riferimento non è concluso.

    • I’ve had three meetings today.
    • She hasn’t called me this week.
    • We’ve sold a lot this month.

    👉 today / this week / this month / this year possono richiedere il Present Perfect se il periodo è ancora in corso.

    E. States or actions continuing until now

    Con for e since puoi parlare di situazioni iniziate nel passato e ancora vere adesso.

    • I’ve lived here for six years.
    • She has worked here since 2021.
    • We’ve known each other for a long time.

    2. Present Perfect vs Past Simple

    Questa è la distinzione più importante di tutta l’unità.

    Usa il Present Perfect quando:

    • il tempo non è specificato
    • il periodo non è finito
    • il risultato è importante adesso
    • l’esperienza conta più del momento preciso

    Usa il Past Simple quando:

    • il tempo è definito o implicito come concluso
    • parli di un evento “storico” della tua narrazione
    • vuoi dire quando è successo qualcosa

    Compare

    • I’ve seen that film. (esperienza generale)

    • I saw that film last weekend. (momento definito)

    • She has changed a lot. (cambiamento fino a oggi)

    • She changed schools in 2022. (evento chiuso)

    • We’ve had two meetings today. (oggi non è finito)

    • We had two meetings yesterday. (ieri è finito)

    Quick test

    Chiediti:

    1. Sto dicendo quando?
    2. Il periodo è finito?
    3. Mi interessa il risultato presente?
    4. Sto parlando dell’esperienza in generale?

    Se la risposta 1 è sì, di solito vai verso Past Simple. Se prevalgono 2, 3 o 4, spesso serve il Present Perfect.

    3. Key markers and discourse patterns

    already

    Indica che qualcosa è successo prima del previsto o prima di quanto necessario.

    • I’ve already sent the email.
    • She has already eaten.

    just

    Indica un’azione appena conclusa.

    • We’ve just arrived.
    • I’ve just spoken to Marta.

    yet

    Si usa soprattutto in negative e domande.

    • I haven’t finished yet.
    • Have you booked the tickets yet?

    ever / never

    Parlano di esperienza di vita.

    • Have you ever tried scuba diving?
    • I’ve never been to Iceland.

    for / since

    • for = durata

    • since = punto iniziale

    • I’ve studied English for ten years.

    • I’ve studied English since primary school.

    4. Been vs Gone

    Questa differenza crea molti errori.

    been

    La persona è andata e poi è tornata.

    • I’ve been to Berlin three times.
    • She has been to that restaurant before.

    gone

    La persona è andata ed è ancora lì, oppure non è qui nel momento in cui parli.

    • Tom has gone to the bank. (he isn’t here now)
    • My parents have gone on holiday.

    5. Frequent past participles you really need

    Irregular verbs

    • be → been
    • begin → begun
    • break → broken
    • do → done
    • eat → eaten
    • go → gone
    • know → known
    • see → seen
    • speak → spoken
    • take → taken
    • write → written
    • drive → driven
    • choose → chosen
    • give → given
    • make → made

    High-frequency chunks

    • I’ve already finished.
    • I haven’t decided yet.
    • Have you ever heard of it?
    • We’ve just arrived.
    • She’s worked here since May.

    🎧 Reading: workplace conversation

    Elena: Have you finished the client presentation yet?
    Marco: Yes, I’ve just sent the final version to the team.
    Elena: Great. Have you included the new sales figures?
    Marco: Yes, I have. We’ve had excellent results this quarter.
    Elena: Really?
    Marco: Yes. We’ve signed three new contracts this month.
    Elena: That’s impressive. Have you ever worked on a project like this before?
    Marco: Not exactly. I worked on a similar campaign last year, but this one has been much more international.

    Comprehension

    1. Has Marco finished the presentation?
    2. Why is this month used with the Present Perfect?
    3. Which sentence refers to a finished past time?
    4. Why does Marco say I worked on a similar campaign last year and not I’ve worked?

    🗣️ Guided Speaking

    A. Personal experience

    Rispondi in modo completo:

    • Have you ever done something difficult in English?
    • Have you ever travelled alone?
    • Have you ever changed your routine because of work or study?
    • Have you ever learned a skill online?

    B. Result now

    Completa e spiega la conseguenza presente:

    • I’ve lost ... so now ...
    • We’ve finished ... so now ...
    • She’s changed ... so now ...

    C. for / since

    Parla di te:

    • I’ve lived in ... for ...
    • I’ve known ... since ...
    • I’ve studied English for ...
    • I haven’t ... since ...

    D. Follow-up questions

    Dopo una risposta con il Present Perfect, fai una domanda di approfondimento al Past Simple.

    Example:

    • A: Have you ever worked abroad?
    • B: Yes, I have.
    • A: Where did you work?
    • A: How long did you stay there?

    Questa combinazione è una firma del parlato naturale a livello B1.

    ✍️ Writing Task

    Scrivi un testo di 140–180 parole sul tema:

    How your life has changed in the last few years

    Nel testo prova a usare:

    • almeno 3 Present Perfect per cambiamenti o risultati
    • almeno 2 esempi con for / since
    • almeno 1 Past Simple per un evento con tempo definito
    • almeno 2 segnali tra already, just, yet, ever, never

    Writing frame

    Puoi organizzare il testo così:

    1. Introduzione generale
    2. Cambiamenti nello studio, lavoro o routine
    3. Nuove esperienze fatte o non ancora fatte
    4. Conclusione personale

    🔊 Pronunciation and connected speech

    Nel parlato naturale, have e has sono spesso deboli e poco marcati.

    • I’ve finished it.
    • She’s just called.
    • We’ve already seen it.
    • They’ve never tried it.

    Allenati a pronunciare i blocchi, non le parole isolate.

    Drill

    Ripeti:

    • I’ve just sent it.
    • We’ve already booked it.
    • She hasn’t answered yet.
    • Have you ever seen it?
    • I’ve lived here for years.

    ⚠️ Typical Italian mistakes

    ❌ I have went there.
    ✅ I have gone there.

    ❌ I have seen him yesterday.
    ✅ I saw him yesterday.

    ❌ I am here since 2020.
    ✅ I have been here since 2020.

    ❌ I know her since high school.
    ✅ I have known her since high school.

    ❌ Have you ever been in London?
    ✅ Have you ever been to London?

    ❌ I have finished just now yesterday.
    ✅ I’ve just finished.

    Why these happen

    Molti di questi errori nascono da:

    • traduzione letterale dall’italiano
    • confusione tra participio e past simple
    • mancata distinzione tra tempo definito e rilevanza presente
    • uso scorretto di for / since

    🔄 Transformation Drill

    Trasforma correttamente.

    1. I finished my homework. → risultato presente
    2. She started working here in 2021. → situazione ancora vera
    3. We didn’t decide. → con yet
    4. He went to Paris three times in his life. → esperienza
    5. I know Anna from 2018. → con since

    Possible answers

    1. I’ve finished my homework.
    2. She has worked here since 2021.
    3. We haven’t decided yet.
    4. He has been to Paris three times.
    5. I’ve known Anna since 2018.

    ✅ Mixed Practice

    Scegli la forma corretta.

    1. I’ve lost / I lost my phone, so I can’t call her now.
    2. Did you ever try / Have you ever tried Korean food?
    3. We’ve had / We had two meetings yesterday.
    4. She has worked / worked here since 2022.
    5. I’ve already sent / already sent the file.
    6. Tom has been / has gone to the doctor, so he isn’t here.
    7. I haven’t finished / didn’t finish yet.
    8. They’ve lived / lived here for ten years.

    🧠 Self-check

    Riesci a:

    • spiegare la differenza tra I’ve been to Rome e I went to Rome last year?
    • usare bene already / just / yet senza tradurre parola per parola?
    • costruire frasi corrette con for / since?
    • passare dal Present Perfect al Past Simple in una conversazione naturale?
    • evitare errori come have went, I am here since, have seen yesterday?

    Se la risposta è “quasi sempre sì”, sei sulla strada giusta per il livello B1.

    🚀 Final Speaking Task

    Parla per 2–3 minuti su uno di questi temi:

    1. What has changed in your life recently?
    2. What have you already done this week, and what haven’t you done yet?
    3. What experiences have helped you grow as a person?

    Target language to include

    • Present Perfect for experiences
    • Present Perfect for results
    • one example with for
    • one example with since
    • one Past Simple follow-up with a specific time reference

    At B1 level, grammar becomes useful when you choose it for meaning, not just because you remember the rule.
