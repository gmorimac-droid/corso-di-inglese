    Unità 27 – Modals: must, should, have to

    Unità 27 – Modals: must, should, have to

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità saprai:

    • dare consigli in modo naturale con should / shouldn’t
    • esprimere obblighi, necessità e regole con must, mustn’t e have to
    • distinguere tra un obbligo personale e una regola esterna
    • parlare di ciò che è obbligatorio, consigliato, vietato o non necessario
    • evitare gli errori più comuni con i verbi modali

    🧠 Grammar in context

    I verbi modali aiutano a esprimere atteggiamento, obbligo, consiglio, divieto o necessità. In questa unità ci concentriamo su tre strutture molto utili nel parlato reale:

    • should → consiglio
    • must → obbligo forte / regola percepita come importante
    • have to → necessità pratica o regola esterna

    Forma base

    • You should rest.
    • You must wear a seatbelt.
    • I have to leave early tomorrow.

    Dopo should e must usiamo sempre il verbo base, senza to.

    1. Should = advice

    Usiamo should per dire che qualcosa è una buona idea, un suggerimento o un consiglio.

    Esempi

    • You should drink more water.
    • You should talk to your manager.
    • You should go to bed earlier.
    • We should leave now.

    Negativa

    • You shouldn’t stay up so late.
    • He shouldn’t worry too much.

    Domande

    • Should I call her?
    • What should we do?

    2. Must = strong obligation or rule

    Usiamo must quando l’obbligo è sentito come forte, importante o categorico.

    Esempi

    • You must wear protective equipment.
    • I must finish this report today.
    • You must stop when the light is red.

    Negativa: mustn’t

    Mustn’t significa proibizione.

    • You mustn’t smoke here.
    • Students mustn’t use their phones during the exam.

    3. Have to = external obligation / practical necessity

    Usiamo have to quando c’è una regola esterna, una necessità concreta o un obbligo pratico.

    Esempi

    • I have to work on Saturday.
    • We have to wear ID badges at the office.
    • She has to take two buses to get to work.

    Negativa

    • I don’t have to work tomorrow.
    • You don’t have to pay now.

    Attenzione: don’t have to non significa proibizione. Significa non è necessario.

    4. Key difference

    Struttura Significato principale Esempio
    should consiglio You should rest.
    must obbligo forte / regola importante You must wear a helmet.
    have to necessità pratica / regola esterna I have to be at work at 8.
    mustn’t proibizione You mustn’t park here.
    don’t have to non necessario You don’t have to come early.

    🎧 Reading / Dialogue

    Dialogue 1 – Advice

    A: I’m really tired these days. B: You should take a short break. A: I know. I also sleep very little. B: Then you should go to bed earlier.

    Dialogue 2 – Rules at work

    A: Do we need our badges every day? B: Yes, we have to wear them at all times. A: Can we eat in the meeting room? B: No, we mustn’t eat there.

    Dialogue 3 – Practical necessity

    A: Why are you leaving so early? B: I have to catch a train. A: Do you have to go now? B: Yes, otherwise I’ll miss it.

    🗣️ Speaking

    Ripeti:

    • You should take a break.
    • You shouldn’t worry so much.
    • You must wear a helmet.
    • You mustn’t use your phone here.
    • I have to get up early.
    • We don’t have to finish today.

    Ora completa a voce:

    • You should ____.
    • At work, I have to ____.
    • In my country, drivers must ____.
    • Students shouldn’t ____.
    • Today I have to ____ before 6 p.m.

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi un testo di 90–110 parole su uno di questi temi:

    1. Advice for a healthy routine
    2. Rules in my workplace / school
    3. Things I have to do during the week

    Cerca di includere:

    • almeno 2 frasi con should
    • almeno 2 frasi con have to
    • almeno 1 regola con must o mustn’t

    Example

    During the week I have to wake up early and go to work by bus. I also have to prepare reports for my team. I think I should sleep more and do more exercise because I often feel tired. At work, we must wear our ID badges, and we mustn’t leave confidential documents on our desks. I don’t have to work on Sundays, so I usually relax and spend time with my family.

    🔊 Pronunciation

    Attenzione a queste forme:

    • should → /ʃʊd/
    • must → /mʌst/
    • have to → spesso nel parlato suona vicino a /hæftə/
    • shouldn’t → la l non si sente

    Ripeti:

    • You should call her.
    • We have to leave now.
    • You mustn’t park here.

    ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

    ❌ You should to study more.
    ✅ You should study more.

    ❌ I must to go now.
    ✅ I must go now.

    ❌ I no have to work tomorrow.
    ✅ I don’t have to work tomorrow.

    ❌ You don’t must do that.
    ✅ You mustn’t do that. / You don’t have to do that. (dipende dal significato)

    Ricorda:

    • dopo should e must → verbo base
    • mustn’t = vietato
    • don’t have to = non necessario

    🧠 Pattern fondamentali

    • Subject + should + base verb
    • Subject + must + base verb
    • Subject + have to / has to + base verb
    • Subject + don’t / doesn’t have to + base verb

    Examples

    • You should speak to him.
    • She must be here by nine.
    • I have to finish this task.
    • He doesn’t have to come tomorrow.

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa con should, must, mustn’t, have to, don’t have to.

    1. You ____ drink more water.
    2. We ____ wear a seatbelt in the car.
    3. Employees ____ smoke in this area.
    4. I ____ go to the office tomorrow; it’s optional.
    5. She ____ finish the report before noon.
    6. What ____ I do if I feel stressed?

    🔄 Trasforma

    Riscrivi usando la parola tra parentesi.

    1. It is a good idea to rest more. (should)
    2. It is not necessary to come early. (don’t have to)
    3. It is forbidden to park here. (mustn’t)
    4. It is necessary for me to wear a uniform. (have to)

    🧠 Think & Speak

    Parla per 1–2 minuti su uno di questi temi:

    • three things you should do to improve your English
    • rules at your workplace or school
    • things you have to do every week
    • one thing people must do to stay healthy

    🚀 Mini task

    Immagina di dare consigli a un nuovo collega o a uno studente straniero.

    Scrivi o registra 8 frasi:

    • 3 consigli con should
    • 3 obblighi con have to / must
    • 2 proibizioni con mustn’t

    💬 Extra practice

    Rispondi:

    • What should you do to improve your English quickly?
    • What do you have to do before work or class?
    • What must people do in public places?
    • What mustn’t people do in your office, school or city?

    🎯 Challenge

    Scrivi un piccolo guide sheet intitolato:

    Rules and advice for new students / new employees

    Usa almeno:

    • 3 frasi con should
    • 3 con have to
    • 2 con mustn’t

    English becomes more useful when you can give advice, explain rules and talk about real obligations.

    Unità 27 – Modals: must, should, have to

    🎯 Lesson Goals

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • dare consigli in modo più maturo, preciso e pragmatico
    • distinguere chiaramente tra advice, internal obligation, external obligation, prohibition e lack of necessity
    • usare should, must, have to, mustn’t e don’t have to senza tradurre meccanicamente dall’italiano
    • formulare regole, raccomandazioni, linee guida e priorità in contesti reali: lavoro, salute, studio, sicurezza, servizio al cliente
    • evitare gli errori tipici degli italofoni, soprattutto con mustn’t vs don’t have to, l’uso scorretto di to dopo i modali, e la tendenza a usare must dove l’inglese reale preferisce have to

    Questa unità è importante perché porta l’uso dei modali dal livello “regola scolastica” a un livello operativo e comunicativo: non si tratta solo di costruire la frase corretta, ma di scegliere la forma che esprime il grado giusto di forza.

    🧠 Core idea: modals express stance, not just grammar

    Molti studenti imparano:

    • should = consiglio
    • must = obbligo
    • have to = dovere

    Ma nel parlato reale il punto non è solo il significato base. Il punto è chi impone l’obbligo, quanto è forte, e come suona la frase.

    Confronta:

    • You should check the figures again. (consiglio professionale)
    • You must send this today. (urgenza / obbligo forte, spesso deciso da chi parla)
    • We have to submit the report by 5 p.m. (scadenza esterna / requisito pratico)

    La differenza è sottile ma decisiva.

    1. Should: recommendation, expectation, strategic advice

    Should non significa solo “dovresti”. A livello B1 serve per:

    • dare consigli
    • fare raccomandazioni professionali
    • suggerire una strategia
    • indicare ciò che è ragionevole o opportuno
    • parlare di aspettative moderate

    Examples

    • You should speak to your manager before making a decision.
    • We should review the contract more carefully.
    • You shouldn’t reply when you’re angry.
    • The meeting should end by 4 p.m.

    Why it matters

    Should è molto utile perché è abbastanza forte da guidare una persona, ma non aggressivo come must. In molti contesti professionali è la scelta più naturale.

    Useful professional patterns

    • You should probably...
    • We should consider...
    • I think you should...
    • You shouldn’t ignore...
    • The best thing to do is...

    Example in context

    • If the client is unhappy, you should acknowledge the problem first and then offer a solution.

    2. Must: strong necessity, rule, urgency, speaker authority

    Must esprime un obbligo forte. Spesso comunica che la regola è percepita come importante, urgente o non negoziabile.

    Examples

    • You must wear protective equipment in this area.
    • We must act quickly.
    • I must remember to call her.
    • You must read this before the meeting.

    Important nuance

    Nel parlato quotidiano, soprattutto quando l’obbligo viene da circostanze esterne, l’inglese usa spesso have to più di must. Must resta molto comune per:

    • istruzioni forti
    • safety rules
    • formal notices
    • decisioni personali forti
    • enfasi

    Internal obligation

    • I must remember to back up my files.
    • I must stop wasting time.

    Qui l’obbligo parte dal parlante, non da una regola esterna.

    3. Have to: external obligation, requirement, practical necessity

    A livello B1, have to è spesso la forma più naturale per parlare di doveri quotidiani, scadenze, regole organizzative e necessità pratiche.

    Examples

    • I have to attend a training session tomorrow.
    • We have to wear badges in the office.
    • She has to take medication twice a day.
    • Do you have to work this weekend?

    Why learners overuse must

    Molti italofoni traducono “devo” sempre con must, ma in inglese reale have to è spesso più naturale quando l’obbligo è legato a:

    • orari
    • procedure
    • regolamenti
    • scadenze
    • condizioni esterne

    Confronta:

    • I must finish this. (forte, personale, urgente)
    • I have to finish this by noon. (scadenza concreta)

    4. The crucial contrast: mustn’t vs don’t have to

    Questa è una delle distinzioni più importanti di tutta l’unità.

    Mustn’t = prohibition

    Significa: non devi farlo perché è vietato / proibito / non consentito.

    • You mustn’t enter this area.
    • Staff mustn’t share passwords.
    • You mustn’t use your phone during the exam.

    Don’t have to = no necessity

    Significa: non è necessario.

    • You don’t have to come if you’re busy.
    • We don’t have to print the document.
    • She doesn’t have to wear formal clothes on Fridays.

    Compare

    • You mustn’t be late. = È proibito / inaccettabile fare tardi.
    • You don’t have to be early. = Non serve arrivare in anticipo.

    Questa distinzione va interiorizzata, non solo memorizzata.

    5. Choosing the right modal by communicative force

    A. Gentle advice

    • You should take a break.
    • You should update the client.

    B. Strong recommendation bordering on obligation

    • You really should check the figures again.

    C. Strong obligation / urgency

    • You must send this now.

    D. External requirement

    • We have to follow company policy.

    E. Prohibition

    • You mustn’t disclose confidential information.

    F. Lack of necessity

    • You don’t have to answer immediately.

    Un buon parlante B1 sceglie il modal in base alla forza pragmatica, non solo alla traduzione italiana.

    6. Workplace English: advice, rules and compliance

    Questa unità è molto utile in ambito lavorativo.

    Advice to a colleague

    • You should summarise the key points more clearly.
    • You should confirm the deadline in writing.
    • You shouldn’t promise something you can’t deliver.

    Company rules / procedures

    • Employees have to complete the form before access is granted.
    • Visitors must wear a pass at all times.
    • Staff mustn’t leave confidential material unattended.

    Practical necessity

    • I have to finish this before the weekly meeting.
    • We have to update the database by Friday.

    7. Health, study and everyday life

    Health advice

    • You should cut down on sugar.
    • You should get more sleep.
    • You shouldn’t ignore these symptoms.

    Study advice

    • You should revise a little every day.
    • You shouldn’t leave everything until the night before the exam.
    • You have to practise regularly if you want to improve.

    Everyday obligations

    • I have to renew my passport.
    • We have to leave early to avoid traffic.
    • You mustn’t park in front of the gate.

    8. Form and structure: keep it clean

    Positive

    • should + base verb
    • must + base verb
    • have to + base verb

    Negative

    • shouldn’t + base verb
    • mustn’t + base verb
    • don’t / doesn’t have to + base verb

    Questions

    • Should I send it now?
    • Must we sign this today?
    • Do you have to attend the meeting?

    Past / future note

    Must non ha tutte le forme verbali in uso quotidiano come have to. Per passato e futuro si usa spesso had to / will have to.

    • I had to cancel the appointment.
    • We’ll have to leave earlier next time.

    Questo è utile per rendere l’inglese più realistico e meno scolastico.

    🎧 Reading / Dialogue

    Dialogue 1 – Team leader giving advice

    A: I’m not sure how to handle the client’s complaint. B: You should reply quickly, but you shouldn’t sound defensive. A: Should I call them or send an email? B: You should probably email first and then offer a call.

    Dialogue 2 – Office policy

    A: Do we have to wear badges even on Fridays? B: Yes, we have to wear them every day. A: And can we share login details if someone is absent? B: No, you mustn’t share passwords under any circumstances.

    Dialogue 3 – Personal obligation vs external rule

    A: Why are you still at the office? B: I have to finish the monthly report. A: You look exhausted. B: I know. I should manage my time better.

    🗣️ Speaking Drills

    Ripeti con attenzione al tono:

    • You should double-check the figures.
    • We have to follow the procedure.
    • You mustn’t interrupt the patient.
    • I don’t have to be there in person.
    • We’ll have to discuss this again tomorrow.

    Controlled practice

    Completa a voce:

    • If a colleague is stressed, you should ____.
    • In my job, we have to ____.
    • Visitors mustn’t ____.
    • Students don’t have to _, but they should _.
    • To improve your English, you should never ____.

    Contrast drill

    Scegli la forma giusta e spiega perché:

    1. You ____ wear protective glasses in the lab.
    2. You ____ come tomorrow if you’re busy.
    3. I ____ remember to send that invoice.
    4. You ____ leave confidential files on your desk.

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi un testo di 140–180 parole su uno di questi temi:

    1. Advice for new employees in my company
    2. Rules and expectations in a professional environment
    3. What people should do to work or study more effectively

    Cerca di includere:

    • almeno 3 frasi con should / shouldn’t
    • almeno 3 con have to / don’t have to
    • almeno 2 con must / mustn’t
    • almeno una distinzione chiara tra prohibition e lack of necessity

    Model opening

    New employees should ask questions when they are unsure, and they shouldn’t try to solve every problem alone. In our company, staff have to follow security procedures and update their tasks regularly. They mustn’t share confidential information, but they don’t have to work late every day. In my opinion, people should organise their priorities better and communicate clearly when deadlines are unrealistic.

    🔊 Pronunciation and delivery

    Focus points

    • should → vocale breve /ʃʊd/
    • mustn’t → spesso parlato rapidamente, attenzione alla t
    • have to → in connected speech spesso suona come /hæftə/
    • should have / must have / have to non si leggono parola per parola come in italiano

    Practice lines

    • You should tell them as soon as possible.
    • We have to finish this by five.
    • You mustn’t discuss this outside the team.

    L’obiettivo non è la perfezione fonetica, ma una delivery chiara, sicura e fluida.

    ⚠️ Error Clinic for Italian Speakers

    1. Adding “to” after modals

    ❌ You should to call him.
    ✅ You should call him.

    ❌ I must to leave now.
    ✅ I must leave now.

    2. Confusing prohibition with no necessity

    ❌ You don’t have to smoke here.
    ✅ You mustn’t smoke here.

    ❌ You mustn’t come tomorrow if you’re busy.
    ✅ You don’t have to come tomorrow if you’re busy.

    3. Overusing must for everything

    ❌ I must go to work at 8 every day. (possible, but often too strong)
    ✅ I have to go to work at 8 every day.

    4. Direct translation from Italian “devo”

    In inglese non tutti i “devo” sono uguali. Chiediti sempre:

    • è un consiglio?
    • è una regola?
    • è un obbligo esterno?
    • è vietato?
    • non è necessario?

    Questo ti aiuta a scegliere la forma giusta.

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Scegli la forma migliore: should, must, have to, mustn’t, don’t have to.

    1. If you want to improve, you ____ practise consistently.
    2. Visitors ____ enter the building without a pass.
    3. We ____ wear formal clothes on Fridays; it’s optional.
    4. I ____ finish this today because the deadline is tomorrow.
    5. You really ____ speak to HR before making a complaint.
    6. Staff ____ store passwords in shared documents.
    7. Do we ____ attend the training session, or is it optional?
    8. You ____ ignore repeated mistakes in a safety procedure.

    🔄 Transformation Drill

    Riscrivi con il modal richiesto.

    1. It is a good idea to take notes during meetings. (should)
    2. It is forbidden to use personal USB devices. (mustn’t)
    3. It is not necessary to reply tonight. (don’t have to)
    4. It is necessary for all staff to sign the form. (have to)
    5. I feel a strong personal need to change my routine. (must)

    🧠 Final Speaking Task

    Parla per 2–3 minuti su uno di questi temi:

    • advice for someone starting a new job
    • rules people have to follow in your workplace or country
    • what students should do to learn more effectively
    • the difference between something that is forbidden and something that is simply optional

    Per fare una buona performance, cerca di:

    • usare almeno 6 modals diversi in totale
    • includere esempi reali
    • spiegare il motivo delle tue scelte
    • non limitarti a frasi isolate: costruisci un discorso coerente

    🚀 Performance Task

    Scrivi una mini guida intitolata:

    Advice and Rules for New Team Members

    Lunghezza consigliata: 150–180 parole.

    La guida deve includere:

    • consigli pratici
    • regole di comportamento
    • obblighi organizzativi
    • almeno 2 proibizioni
    • almeno 1 esempio di cosa non è necessario fare

    A strong B1 speaker does not just know modal verbs. They know how to use them to guide, warn, advise and manage real situations.
