    Unità 08 – Places in Town

    Modalità Base attiva. Questa versione ti aiuta a parlare dei luoghi della città e a fare domande semplici con Is there...? / Are there...?. Per vedere la versione estesa usa il toggle BASE / PRO nella barra in alto.

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • nominare luoghi comuni in una città
    • dire se un posto esiste oppure no
    • fare domande semplici con Is there...? / Are there...?
    • descrivere in modo essenziale la tua città o il tuo quartiere

    🧠 Grammar in context

    Domande con there is / there are

    Usiamo Is there...? per chiedere se c’è un solo luogo.

    • Is there a bank?
    • Is there a supermarket near here?
    • Is there a park in your town?

    Usiamo Are there...? per chiedere se ci sono più luoghi.

    • Are there any shops?
    • Are there restaurants in the centre?
    • Are there any hotels near the station?

    Risposte brevi

    • Yes, there is.
    • No, there isn’t.
    • Yes, there are.
    • No, there aren’t.

    Esempi:

    • Is there a hospital? → Yes, there is.
    • Are there any cafés? → No, there aren’t.

    Frasi affermative

    • There is a bank near my house.
    • There is a park in my town.
    • There are two cafés in my street.
    • There are many shops in the centre.

    📚 Core vocabulary

    Places in town

    • restaurant
    • café
    • bar
    • supermarket
    • bank
    • school
    • hospital
    • pharmacy
    • park
    • hotel
    • station
    • shop
    • cinema
    • museum

    Useful words

    • near
    • far
    • here
    • there
    • in the centre
    • in my town
    • on my street

    🎧 Listening / Reading

    Dialogue:

    A: Excuse me, is there a bank near here?
    B: Yes, there is. It’s next to the supermarket.
    A: Are there any cafés in this area?
    B: Yes, there are two cafés on this street.
    A: Is there a pharmacy?
    B: No, there isn’t.

    🗣️ Speaking

    Ripeti:

    • Is there a restaurant near here?
    • Is there a bank in your town?
    • Are there any shops on this street?
    • There is a park near my house.
    • There are two cafés in the centre.

    Ora completa con informazioni vere:

    • There is a ______ in my town.
    • There are ______ near my house.
    • There isn’t a ______ in my area.
    • Are there any ______ in your town?

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi 6–8 frasi sulla tua città o sul tuo quartiere.

    Guida:

    1. un luogo vicino a casa tua
    2. due luoghi nel centro
    3. una cosa che non c’è
    4. una domanda che puoi fare a un turista

    Esempio:

    There is a supermarket near my house.
    There is a park in my town.
    There are two cafés in the centre.
    There are many shops on the main street.
    There isn’t a museum in my area.
    Is there a hotel near the station?

    🔊 Pronunciation

    Attenzione a queste parole:

    • restaurant
    • pharmacy
    • station
    • museum

    Tip

    In inglese la pronuncia non segue sempre le regole italiane. Ascolta e ripeti più volte intere frasi, non solo parole isolate.

    ⚠️ Errori comuni

    ❌ Is there restaurants?
    ✅ Are there restaurants?

    ❌ There is many shops.
    ✅ There are many shops.

    ❌ Is there a cafés?
    ✅ Are there cafés?

    ❌ In my town have a park.
    ✅ In my town, there is a park.

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa

    1. ___ there a hotel near here?
    2. ___ there any parks in your town?
    3. There ___ a station in the centre.
    4. There ___ two cafés on this street.
    5. There ___ not a hospital in my area.

    Trasforma in domanda

    1. There is a bank near here. → ______?
    2. There are shops in the centre. → ______?

    🚀 Mini task

    Immagina di parlare con un turista.

    Fai 4 domande:

    • Is there a hotel near here?
    • Is there a restaurant in the centre?
    • Are there any cafés nearby?
    • Is there a pharmacy on this street?

    Poi rispondi come abitante della città.

    Modalità Professional attiva. Questa versione amplia la lezione su places in town con lessico più ricco, descrizione di quartieri e servizi, uso naturale di there is / there are, domande utili per orientarsi, collocazioni frequenti, mini-dialoghi realistici, errori tipici degli italofoni, speaking drills, writing guidato e self-check finale.

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • parlare della tua città o del tuo quartiere in modo più organizzato e realistico
    • usare there is / there are per descrivere servizi e luoghi pubblici
    • fare domande naturali per chiedere se un luogo esiste e dove si trova
    • usare un lessico più preciso per orientamento, servizi, centro città e zona residenziale
    • evitare errori frequenti quando descrivi una città in inglese

    🧭 Focus della lezione

    Questa unità non serve solo a memorizzare parole come bank o park. Serve a fare tre cose molto utili nella comunicazione reale:

    1. descrivere un luogo dove vivi o che conosci
    2. aiutare qualcuno a orientarsi
    3. fare domande pratiche da turista o da residente

    In inglese A1, saper parlare della propria città è una competenza molto importante perché unisce:

    • grammatica di base
    • lessico quotidiano
    • micro-interazione reale
    • descrizione guidata di uno spazio pubblico

    L’obiettivo finale non è fare frasi isolate, ma costruire una mini-presentazione credibile della propria zona.

    🧠 Grammar in context

    Talking about places with there is / there are

    Quando descrivi una città o un quartiere, there is / there are ti serve per dire quali servizi o luoghi sono presenti.

    Singular: there is

    Per un solo luogo o servizio:

    • There is a pharmacy near my house.
    • There is a small park in my neighbourhood.
    • There is a railway station in the city centre.
    • There is a museum near the river.

    Plural: there are

    Per più luoghi:

    • There are two supermarkets in my area.
    • There are many cafés in the centre.
    • There are several shops on this street.
    • There are three bus stops near the station.

    👉 Regola chiave:

    • there is = una cosa
    • there are = più cose

    Questions: Is there...? / Are there...?

    Queste forme servono per chiedere se un luogo esiste.

    Singular

    • Is there a bank near here?
    • Is there a post office in your town?
    • Is there a bus stop near the hotel?
    • Is there a pharmacy open now?

    Plural

    • Are there any restaurants in this area?
    • Are there cafés near the station?
    • Are there any parks in your neighbourhood?
    • Are there many hotels in the city centre?

    Short answers

    • Yes, there is.
    • No, there isn’t.
    • Yes, there are.
    • No, there aren’t.

    Full answers sound more natural

    • Yes, there is a bank next to the supermarket.
    • No, there isn’t a pharmacy on this street.
    • Yes, there are two cafés near the square.

    👉 In una conversazione vera, la short answer è corretta, ma spesso è meglio aggiungere un’informazione in più.

    Some / Any / A lot of

    Quando parli della città, queste parole sono molto frequenti.

    some

    Nelle affermazioni plurali:

    • There are some shops near my house.
    • There are some good restaurants in the centre.

    any

    Nelle domande e nelle negative:

    • Are there any cafés near here?
    • There aren’t any buses after midnight.

    a lot of / many

    Per quantità maggiori:

    • There are a lot of tourists in summer.
    • There are many restaurants in the old town.

    👉 A livello iniziale, a lot of suona spesso più naturale di many nelle frasi spontanee.

    Useful prepositions and place phrases

    Per descrivere una città non basta dire che un posto esiste. Devi anche dire dove si trova.

    Basic location phrases

    • near my house
    • near the station
    • in the centre
    • in the city centre
    • on this street
    • next to the bank
    • opposite the hotel
    • between the supermarket and the café
    • far from my home

    Examples

    • There is a pharmacy near the station.
    • There are two cafés on this street.
    • There is a bank next to the supermarket.
    • There is a hotel opposite the station.
    • There are some restaurants in the city centre.

    📚 Core vocabulary

    Public places and services

    • bank
    • pharmacy
    • hospital
    • school
    • library
    • museum
    • cinema
    • hotel
    • station
    • bus stop
    • post office
    • police station

    Food and social places

    • restaurant
    • café
    • bar
    • bakery
    • market
    • supermarket

    Outdoor places

    • park
    • square
    • street
    • bridge
    • river
    • playground

    Area words

    • town
    • city
    • neighbourhood
    • area
    • centre
    • suburb
    • old town
    • main street

    🧩 Funzioni comunicative

    Con questa unità impari a:

    1. Dire cosa c’è nella tua zona

    • There is a supermarket near my house.
    • There are two parks in my neighbourhood.

    2. Chiedere se un servizio esiste

    • Is there a pharmacy near here?
    • Are there any taxis near the station?

    3. Dare informazioni utili a un turista

    • There is a good restaurant near the square.
    • There are several cafés in the centre.

    4. Descrivere differenze tra centro e periferia

    • In the city centre there are many shops.
    • In my area there aren’t many restaurants.

    5. Esprimere una valutazione semplice

    • My town is small but convenient.
    • My neighbourhood is quiet and there is a nice park nearby.

    🎧 Listening / Reading

    Dialogue 1 – Tourist information

    A: Excuse me, is there a pharmacy near here?
    B: Yes, there is. It’s next to the bank.
    A: And are there any cafés in this area?
    B: Yes, there are two cafés on the main street.
    A: Is there a bus stop nearby?
    B: Yes, there is one opposite the hotel.

    Dialogue 2 – Talking about your town

    A: What is your town like?
    B: It’s quite small, but there are a lot of useful places.
    A: Really? What is there?
    B: There is a library, there is a sports centre, and there are several shops in the centre. There isn’t a big museum, but there is a nice park by the river.

    Notice

    Le descrizioni più naturali spesso seguono questo ordine:

    1. impressione generale della città
    2. luoghi principali
    3. servizi utili
    4. cosa manca
    5. valutazione finale

    🗣️ Speaking

    Drill 1 – Complete the sentence

    Completa oralmente:

    • There is a ______ near my house.
    • There are two ______ in my area.
    • There isn’t a ______ in my neighbourhood.
    • Are there any ______ in the centre?
    • Is there a ______ near the station?

    Drill 2 – Add location

    Espandi le frasi:

    • There is a bank. → There is a bank near the station.
    • There are cafés. → There are cafés on the main street.
    • There is a park. → There is a park next to the school.

    Drill 3 – Ask and answer like a tourist

    Pratica queste coppie:

    • Is there a hotel near here?
    • Is there a pharmacy open now?
    • Are there any restaurants in this area?
    • Are there buses to the city centre?

    Rispondi prima con una short answer, poi con una frase completa.

    Drill 4 – Describe your area in one minute

    Parla per 1 minuto e usa:

    • 3 frasi con there is
    • 3 frasi con there are
    • 1 frase negativa
    • 2 espressioni di luogo
    • 1 frase finale con la tua opinione

    ✍️ Writing

    Task 1 – Guided paragraph

    Scrivi 8–10 frasi sulla tua città, sul tuo paese o sul tuo quartiere.

    Checklist:

    • una frase introduttiva generale
    • almeno 3 luoghi singolari
    • almeno 3 luoghi plurali
    • almeno 2 dettagli con near / next to / opposite / in the centre
    • almeno 1 frase negativa
    • una frase finale con opinione personale

    Modello:

    My town is small but pleasant.
    There is a supermarket near my house.
    There is a pharmacy on the main street.
    There are two cafés in the centre.
    There are some small shops near the square.
    There is a park next to the school.
    There isn’t a train station, but there is a bus stop near the church.
    I like my town because it is quiet and practical.

    Task 2 – Compare two places

    Scrivi 4 frasi sul centro città e 4 frasi sulla tua zona.

    Obiettivo: imparare a confrontare due contesti usando le stesse strutture.

    🔊 Pronunciation

    Focus words

    • restaurant
    • pharmacy
    • museum
    • station
    • neighbourhood

    Rhythm tip

    Nelle domande, le parole più importanti sono spesso il luogo e la posizione:

    • Is there a bank near here?
    • Are there any cafés in the centre?

    Fluency tip

    Non leggere parola per parola. Prova a pronunciare interi blocchi:

    • near my house
    • in the city centre
    • next to the station
    • opposite the hotel

    ⚠️ Errori comuni degli italiani

    1. Confondere singolare e plurale

    ❌ Is there restaurants near here?
    ✅ Are there restaurants near here?

    ❌ There is many cafés in the centre.
    ✅ There are many cafés in the centre.

    2. Dimenticare there

    ❌ In my town is a park.
    ✅ In my town, there is a park.

    3. Tradurre dall’italiano con have

    ❌ My town has a lot of shops near here.
    ✅ There are a lot of shops in my town.

    Nota: My town has... non è sempre sbagliato, ma a questo livello, quando descrivi cosa esiste in un luogo, there is / there are è la scelta più sicura e centrale.

    4. Usare male any

    ❌ There aren’t some parks in my area.
    ✅ There aren’t any parks in my area.

    5. Omettere l’articolo con i luoghi singolari

    ❌ Is there bank near here?
    ✅ Is there a bank near here?

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa

    1. ___ there a pharmacy near the station?
    2. ___ there any cafés in the centre?
    3. There ___ a hotel opposite the square.
    4. There ___ two supermarkets in my area.
    5. There ___ not any museums in this town.
    6. There are ___ lot of restaurants near the river.

    Correggi gli errori

    1. Is there restaurants in the centre?
    2. There is many shops on this street.
    3. In my town is a library.
    4. There aren’t some cafés near the station.
    5. Are there a hotel near here?

    Trasforma

    1. There is a bank near the museum. → domanda
    2. There are some cafés on this street. → negativa
    3. There isn’t a hospital in my area. → affermativa
    4. Are there any parks near your house? → risposta completa positiva

    🧠 Think & Speak

    Rispondi oralmente:

    • Is there a supermarket near your house?
    • Are there any restaurants in your neighbourhood?
    • Is there a park in your town?
    • Are there many shops in the centre?
    • What places do tourists usually visit in your city?

    Poi unisci le risposte in una mini descrizione continua di 6–8 frasi.

    🚀 Mini task

    Immagina di aiutare un turista appena arrivato nella tua città.

    Parla o scrivi una risposta di 60–90 secondi includendo:

    • una breve descrizione generale della città
    • almeno 4 luoghi utili
    • 2 indicazioni di posizione
    • 1 luogo che manca
    • 1 consiglio finale

    Esempio finale:

    My town is quite small, but it is comfortable and easy to visit. There is a pharmacy near the station and there is a hotel in the city centre. There are several cafés on the main street and there are some good restaurants near the square. There is a park next to the museum, but there isn’t a train station. I think it is a good place for a short visit because everything is close.
