    Professional Upgrade – Argumentation, Positioning and High-Level Debate Control

    La versione base ti insegna a costruire un'argomentazione chiara. La versione professional lavora su ciò che distingue davvero un parlante C2:

    • costruzione strategica della posizione
    • gestione della forza delle affermazioni
    • anticipazione delle obiezioni
    • uso di concessione, qualificazione e contrasto in modo sofisticato
    • capacità di persuadere senza risultare rigido, aggressivo o schematico

    A questo livello, argomentare bene non significa parlare tanto. Significa guidare il pensiero dell'ascoltatore.

    1. Argument is architecture, not opinion stacking

    Molti studenti avanzati producono discorsi lunghi ma deboli perché accumulano idee senza gerarchia.

    Un'argomentazione forte richiede:

    • una central claim riconoscibile
    • un ordine logico
    • una progressione controllata
    • segnalazione delle relazioni tra le idee
    • una chiusura che mostri perché il ragionamento conta

    Confronta:

    Weak

    • I think remote work is good because people like it and it saves time and companies also change and there are many advantages.

    Stronger

    • I would argue that remote work should remain a permanent option. The strongest reason is not convenience but efficiency: when implemented well, it reduces commuting time and often improves concentration. That said, the model only works if teams also develop strong communication practices.

    La seconda versione ha:

    • tesi chiara
    • priorità argomentativa
    • qualificazione
    • controllo del focus

    2. Claim strength: how hard should you commit?

    A livello C2, la vera differenza non è sapere una frase “forte”, ma sapere quanto forte deve essere.

    Full commitment

    • This policy is ineffective.
    • There is no justification for this decision.

    Controlled commitment

    • This policy appears to be ineffective.
    • It is difficult to see a convincing justification for this decision.

    Analytical distance

    • The policy raises serious questions about effectiveness.
    • The decision is not easy to justify on practical grounds.

    Un parlante C2 sa quando usare:

    • assertion
    • qualified assertion
    • analytical distancing

    Questo evita due estremi opposti:

    • linguaggio debole e vago
    • linguaggio troppo assoluto e facilmente attaccabile

    3. Core moves in advanced argumentation

    A. State a position

    • I would argue that...
    • My position is that...
    • The most convincing view, in my opinion, is that...

    B. Narrow the claim

    • More specifically,...
    • What I mean by this is...
    • To put it more precisely,...

    C. Prioritise

    • The key issue here is...
    • The more important point, however, is...
    • What matters most is not X but Y.

    D. Concede strategically

    • Admittedly,...
    • That said,...
    • While that concern is valid,...

    E. Reframe

    • The issue is not whether X exists, but how significant it is.
    • The real question is not X, but whether Y can be achieved without it.

    F. Close with force

    • For that reason, the proposal is difficult to defend.
    • Taken together, these factors suggest that...
    • On balance, the argument in favour of X remains stronger.

    4. Concession is one of the strongest C2 tools

    I parlanti molto forti non evitano il punto opposto. Lo integrano.

    Basic debate move

    • I disagree.

    Better

    • I can see why that view is attractive, but I don't think it addresses the central issue.

    Strong C2 move

    • While that argument has intuitive appeal, it underestimates the long-term consequences of the policy.

    La concessione strategica ti permette di:

    • sembrare equilibrato
    • dimostrare controllo del dibattito
    • neutralizzare obiezioni in anticipo
    • aumentare credibilità e autorevolezza

    5. Counterargument and rebuttal

    Una vera argomentazione avanzata non presenta solo il proprio lato. Mostra di aver pensato anche al lato opposto.

    Formula utile

    1. present the opposing view
    2. recognise its strength
    3. limit it
    4. replace it with a stronger frame

    Example

    Some people argue that remote work weakens collaboration. While that concern is understandable, it assumes that collaboration depends primarily on physical presence. In reality, what often matters more is how communication is structured. Without strong systems, even office-based teams can function badly.

    Questa struttura è eccellente per speaking C2, essays, debates e presentazioni.

    6. Argument types: not all reasoning works the same way

    A livello molto alto è utile riconoscere il tipo di argomento che stai usando.

    Cause–effect

    • If students are assessed only through exams, they may focus on memorisation rather than understanding.

    Comparison

    • A hybrid model is more realistic than a fully remote or fully office-based one.

    Principle-based argument

    • Public policy should be judged not only by efficiency but also by fairness.

    Practical feasibility

    • The proposal may sound attractive, but it would be difficult to implement consistently.

    Risk-based argument

    • Even if the short-term benefits are clear, the long-term risks remain significant.

    Usare tipi diversi di reasoning rende il discorso più ricco e meno ripetitivo.

    7. Debate language: agreement, challenge and pressure management

    Strong agreement

    • I agree entirely.
    • That's a compelling point.
    • I think that's difficult to dispute.

    Partial alignment

    • I agree with the principle, though not necessarily with the conclusion.
    • I accept part of that argument, but I think it overlooks...

    Challenge without aggression

    • I'm not persuaded by that line of reasoning.
    • That conclusion doesn't seem to follow from the evidence.
    • I think the argument depends on a questionable assumption.

    Pressure-response language

    • Let me address that directly.
    • I think the issue is slightly different.
    • I'd frame the problem another way.
    • That sounds persuasive at first, but...

    Queste formule aiutano a restare lucido anche quando la conversazione diventa tesa.

    8. Fallacies and weak moves to avoid

    Un livello C2 richiede anche consapevolezza di ciò che indebolisce il ragionamento.

    A. Overgeneralisation

    • Everyone knows...
    • This always happens...

    B. False dichotomy

    • Either we do this or everything fails.

    C. Unsupported intensity

    • This is a disaster.
    • It's completely unacceptable.

    D. Circular reasoning

    • It's wrong because it's not right.

    Better alternatives

    • In many cases...
    • There is a risk that...
    • The argument becomes weaker when...
    • This position is difficult to defend because...

    9. Rhetorical control: pacing and emphasis

    Un buon debater C2 non riversa tutto subito. Distribuisce il peso delle idee.

    Useful sequencing

    • First, it is important to define the issue clearly.
    • Second, we need to distinguish principle from practice.
    • Finally, even if the policy looks attractive in theory, the implementation problems remain substantial.

    Emphatic framing

    • What is often overlooked is...
    • The more serious problem is...
    • The strongest objection, in my view, is...

    Narrowing language

    • To be more precise,...
    • At a practical level,...
    • From a long-term perspective,...

    Questa gestione del ritmo crea autorevolezza.

    10. Mini debate models

    Topic 1 – Should university education be more practical?

    I would argue that universities should place greater emphasis on practical skills, though not at the expense of intellectual depth. The central issue is not whether theory matters—it clearly does—but whether students are being adequately prepared for real professional environments. A more practice-oriented model would not weaken academic standards; if designed well, it could strengthen them by making knowledge more usable.

    Topic 2 – Is social media doing more harm than good?

    While social media has undoubtedly expanded access to information, I would argue that its long-term social effects are increasingly difficult to ignore. The problem is not simply that people spend too much time online, but that many platforms reward speed, outrage and oversimplification. That said, the solution is unlikely to be rejection; more realistic responses would involve education, design reform and stronger critical literacy.

    11. Advanced speaking drills

    Parla per 2–3 minuti su ciascun tema. Usa la struttura:

    • opening claim
    • reason 1
    • reason 2
    • concession
    • rebuttal
    • conclusion

    Temi:

    1. Should governments regulate artificial intelligence more strictly?
    2. Is remote work sustainable in the long term?
    3. Should universities charge lower tuition fees?
    4. Is public debate becoming less intelligent?

    Obblighi linguistici:

    • almeno 2 concessioni
    • almeno 3 discourse markers avanzati
    • almeno 1 reframing move
    • almeno 1 rebuttal esplicito

    12. Writing task – C2 argumentative response

    Scrivi 260–320 parole su uno dei temi seguenti:

    • Is higher education still the best route to professional success?
    • Should governments prioritise freedom of speech over protection from harmful content?
    • Does technology improve decision-making, or does it weaken human judgement?

    Il testo deve includere:

    • una tesi precisa
    • sviluppo logico chiaro
    • un controargomento credibile
    • una concessione strategica
    • una conclusione non banale

    13. Final self-evaluation

    Controlla se sai:

    • scegliere quanto forte deve essere una posizione
    • costruire un discorso con gerarchia logica
    • concedere qualcosa senza perdere forza argomentativa
    • smontare un'obiezione senza diventare aggressivo
    • usare language of stance, framing and rebuttal
    • sembrare lucido, credibile e flessibile mentre difendi un punto complesso

    Se riesci a fare tutto questo con naturalezza, controllo e precisione, stai usando l'inglese in modo autenticamente C2.
