    Unità 37 – Relative Clauses (who, which, that)

    🎯 Obiettivo

    In questa unità imparerai a unire frasi in modo più naturale usando le relative clauses.

    Alla fine della lezione saprai:

    • descrivere persone, cose e luoghi con più precisione
    • usare who, which e that
    • evitare ripetizioni inutili
    • scrivere e parlare in modo più fluido

    🧠 Perché questa unità è importante

    Molti studenti producono frasi corrette ma separate:

    I have a colleague. She works in Berlin. She speaks three languages.

    Queste frasi sono chiare, ma poco naturali.

    Con le relative clauses puoi dire:

    I have a colleague who works in Berlin and speaks three languages.

    Il risultato è più fluido, più maturo e più vicino all’inglese reale.

    🧩 Che cos’è una relative clause

    Una relative clause aggiunge informazioni su una persona, una cosa o un luogo.

    Di solito segue un nome.

    Schema base:

    noun + who / which / that + extra information

    Esempi:

    • I know a woman who works in finance.
    • This is the laptop that I bought last month.
    • We visited a town which was surprisingly quiet.

    👤 WHO = persone

    Usa who per parlare di persone.

    Esempi:

    • The woman who called you is my manager.
    • I have a friend who lives in Dublin.
    • He’s the teacher who helped me the most.

    📦 WHICH = cose / animali / idee

    Usa which per cose, oggetti, animali o idee.

    Esempi:

    • This is the book which changed my mind.
    • I bought a phone which has a very good camera.
    • The film which we watched yesterday was excellent.

    🔄 THAT = persone o cose

    That è molto comune nelle frasi defining e spesso suona naturale nel parlato.

    Esempi:

    • She’s the colleague that works with me.
    • This is the app that everyone uses.
    • The restaurant that we tried was great.

    ✂️ Perché servono: evitare ripetizioni

    Confronta:

    ❌ I met a man. The man works for an international company.
    ✅ I met a man who works for an international company.

    ❌ I bought a bag. The bag was very expensive.
    ✅ I bought a bag that was very expensive.

    🧱 Subject relative clauses

    Quando il pronome relativo è il soggetto della relative clause, non puoi ometterlo.

    • The woman who lives next door is a doctor.
    • The book that explains everything is on the table.

    Qui who/that fanno l’azione.

    🧱 Object relative clauses

    Quando il pronome relativo è l’oggetto, in inglese informale spesso si può omettere.

    • This is the book that I told you about.

    • This is the book I told you about.

    • She’s the person who I met at the conference.

    • She’s the person I met at the conference.

    Per adesso è importante riconoscere entrambe le forme.

    📍 WHERE = luoghi

    Per parlare di luoghi, puoi usare anche where.

    • This is the city where I was born.
    • We need a place where we can talk quietly.
    • That’s the café where we first met.

    👪 WHOSE = possesso

    Whose indica possesso.

    • I know someone whose brother works in Canada.
    • She’s the student whose project won the prize.

    A questo livello basta riconoscerlo e iniziare a usarlo in esempi semplici.

    📚 Esempi reali

    • I work with people who come from different countries.
    • The software that we use every day is quite reliable.
    • We stayed in a hotel which was close to the station.
    • I’d like a job where I can keep learning.
    • I know a designer whose work is really original.

    🎧 Reading

    Leggi il testo e nota le relative clauses.

    I work for a company that develops educational software. One of my colleagues, who is based in Madrid, often leads international meetings. We also have a platform which helps teachers create online lessons. It’s the kind of job where you learn something new every week.

    Domande di comprensione

    1. What does the company develop?
    2. Where is one colleague based?
    3. What does the platform help teachers do?
    4. Why is it the kind of job where you learn a lot?

    🗣️ Speaking practice

    Completa oralmente le frasi.

    • I know a person who...
    • I use an app that...
    • I enjoy places where...
    • I admire people whose...

    Ora prova a espandere ogni frase con un esempio.

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi 8 frasi:

    • 3 con who
    • 2 con which
    • 2 con that
    • 1 con where oppure whose

    Poi controlla se hai evitato ripetizioni inutili.

    🔊 Pronunciation note

    • who → /huː/
    • which → /wɪtʃ/
    • whose → /huːz/

    Nel parlato veloce, that spesso è debole e poco accentato.

    ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

    ❌ I have a friend he works in Milan.
    ✅ I have a friend who works in Milan.

    ❌ The person which called me is my boss.
    ✅ The person who called me is my boss.

    ❌ The thing what I need is time.
    ✅ The thing that I need is time.

    ❌ This is the place where I work in.
    ✅ This is the place where I work.

    🔁 Trasforma

    Unisci le frasi.

    1. I know a woman. She teaches physics.
    2. We bought a car. It uses very little fuel.
    3. This is the office. I had my first interview there.
    4. I met a student. Her project was excellent.
    5. We watched a documentary. It explained the problem well.

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa con who, which, that, where, whose.

    1. The man ___ works here is very helpful.
    2. This is the phone ___ I told you about.
    3. That’s the town ___ my grandparents live.
    4. She’s the employee ___ ideas are always interesting.
    5. The report ___ we read this morning was clear and useful.

    💬 Mini task

    Descrivi:

    • una persona che ammiri
    • un oggetto che usi spesso
    • un luogo importante per te

    Prova a usare almeno tre relative clauses.

    🎯 Challenge

    Scrivi un breve paragrafo di 100–120 parole su:

    A person, a place, and a tool that have influenced your life

    Usa almeno:

    • 2 frasi con who
    • 2 con that/which
    • 1 con where oppure whose

    Good English becomes more natural when ideas are connected, not separated.
