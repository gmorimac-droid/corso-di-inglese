    Unità 38 – Nuance & Tone (Formal vs Informal English)

    Obiettivo della lezione

    In questa unità porti il tuo inglese a un livello più maturo imparando a gestire:

    • register (informal, neutral, formal);
    • distance and politeness;
    • tone softening;
    • differenza tra messaggio naturale e messaggio troppo diretto;
    • riscrittura di email e richieste in modo più appropriato.

    L’obiettivo non è solo “parlare correttamente”, ma parlare in modo adatto al contesto.

    Perché il tono conta davvero

    A livello B2, molte frasi non suonano sbagliate grammaticalmente, ma possono risultare:

    • troppo brusche;
    • troppo informali;
    • eccessivamente rigide;
    • poco naturali nel contesto professionale o accademico.

    Confronta:

    Send me the report today.

    Questa frase è grammaticalmente corretta, ma in molti contesti suona dura.

    Versioni più appropriate:

    Could you send me the report today, please?

    Would it be possible to send me the report today?

    Could you please send me the report by the end of the day?

    Il contenuto non cambia molto. Cambia la relazione che costruisci con l’altra persona.

    Register: informal, neutral, formal

    Informal

    Tipico di:

    • amici;
    • chat rapide;
    • colleghi molto vicini;
    • ambienti con bassa distanza sociale.

    Esempi:

    • Hi, can you send it?
    • Thanks a lot!
    • No problem.
    • Let’s do it tomorrow.

    Neutral professional

    È il registro più utile nella vita reale. Va bene per:

    • email di lavoro ordinarie;
    • richieste semplici;
    • coordinamento tra colleghi;
    • messaggi chiari ma cortesi.

    Esempi:

    • Hello, could you send it when you have a moment?
    • Thanks for your help.
    • Please let me know if you need anything else.
    • I’m writing to confirm our meeting.

    Formal

    Tipico di:

    • contesti istituzionali;
    • candidature;
    • richieste delicate;
    • situazioni in cui vuoi aumentare deferenza e distanza.

    Esempi:

    • Dear Ms Turner,
    • I would like to enquire about...
    • I would be grateful if you could...
    • Please accept my apologies for...

    Le principali strategie di cortesia

    1. Indirectness

    Più una richiesta è indiretta, più tende a suonare cortese.

    Direct Softer More formal
    Send me the file. Could you send me the file? I would be grateful if you could send me the file.
    Tell me the price. Could you tell me the price? I was wondering if you could let me know the price.
    Change the meeting. Could we change the meeting? Would it be possible to reschedule the meeting?

    2. Softening expressions

    Espressioni molto utili:

    • please
    • when you have time
    • if possible
    • a bit
    • I think
    • perhaps / maybe
    • I was wondering if...
    • Would it be possible to...?

    Esempi:

    • Could you check this?
    • Could you check this when you have time?
    • Would it be possible to check this today?

    3. Depersonalising the request

    In certi contesti, rendere la frase meno personale la fa suonare più professionale.

    • I need you to send the invoice.

    • The invoice needs to be sent today.

    • You must complete this form.

    • This form needs to be completed by Friday.

    Tone shift: stessa idea, tono diverso

    Situation: asking for information

    Informal

    Hi, I want info about the training course.

    Neutral professional

    Hello, I would like some information about the training course.

    More formal

    Dear Sir or Madam, I am writing to enquire about the training course and would appreciate any further information you could provide.

    Situation: asking for a meeting change

    Informal

    I can’t make it. Let’s change the meeting.

    Neutral professional

    I’m sorry, but I can’t make the meeting. Could we move it to another time?

    More formal

    I’m afraid I won’t be able to attend the meeting as scheduled. Would it be possible to reschedule it?

    Situation: following up

    Informal

    Did you see my message?

    Neutral professional

    I just wanted to check whether you had seen my previous message.

    More formal

    I am writing to follow up on my previous email regarding the proposal.

    Formality is not only vocabulary

    Molti studenti pensano che il tono dipenda solo da parole come would like o please. In realtà dipende anche da:

    • struttura della frase;
    • lunghezza e densità informativa;
    • grado di esplicitazione;
    • presenza di mitigazione;
    • opening e closing appropriati.

    Confronta:

    Send me the updated version today.

    Could you send me the updated version today, please?

    Could you please send me the updated version by the end of the day, if possible?

    I’d appreciate it if you could send me the updated version by the end of the day.

    Ogni frase aumenta la cortesia e modifica il rapporto interpersonale.

    Common tone problems for Italian speakers

    1. Overdirect translation

    ❌ I ask you to send me...

    In inglese spesso suona duro o artificiale.

    Meglio:

    ✅ Could you send me...?

    ✅ I’d like to ask whether you could send me...

    2. Using “I want” in professional contexts

    ❌ I want to know...

    Meglio:

    ✅ I’d like to know...

    ✅ I was wondering whether...

    3. Missing politeness framing

    ❌ Answer me today.

    ✅ Could you get back to me today, please?

    ✅ I’d appreciate a reply today if possible.

    4. Being too formal in casual contexts

    Anche l’eccesso di formalità può essere innaturale.

    A un collega con cui lavori ogni giorno, questa frase può risultare rigida:

    I would be grateful if you could provide the document at your earliest convenience.

    In molti casi è meglio:

    Could you send me the document when you have a moment? Thanks.

    Register map: common rewrites

    Too direct / basic Better neutral More formal
    I want information. I’d like some information. I am writing to enquire about...
    Can you help me? Could you help me? I would appreciate your assistance with...
    Send me the file. Could you send me the file? Would it be possible to send me the file?
    I can’t come. I won’t be able to attend. I’m afraid I won’t be able to attend.
    Did you read it? Have you had a chance to read it? I am writing to ask whether you have had a chance to review it.

    Dialogue analysis

    Informal chat

    A: Hey, did you finish the slides?

    B: Not yet. I’ll send them later.

    A: OK, thanks.

    Per una chat tra colleghi può andare bene.

    Professional email tone

    A: Hello,

    I just wanted to check whether the slides are ready.

    Could you please send them later today if possible?

    Thanks in advance.

    B: Of course. I’m still making a few changes, but I’ll send the final version this afternoon.

    Qui il contenuto è simile, ma il tono è molto più adatto a un contesto professionale.

    Transformation lab

    Trasforma le frasi in una versione più appropriata per un’email professionale.

    1. Hi, I want the details now.
    2. Can you send me the contract today?
    3. I need your answer fast.
    4. Did you read my last email?
    5. I can’t come. Change the meeting.

    Possible upgraded versions

    1. Hello, I’d like to receive the details as soon as possible.
    2. Could you send me the contract today, please?
    3. I’d appreciate a prompt reply.
    4. I just wanted to check whether you had the chance to read my previous email.
    5. I’m afraid I won’t be able to attend. Would it be possible to reschedule the meeting?

    Writing task

    Task 1

    Scrivi un messaggio informale a un collega con cui hai molta confidenza per chiedere un file.

    Task 2

    Scrivi una email neutra-professionale a un collega per chiedere di spostare una riunione.

    Task 3

    Scrivi una email più formale a un centro di formazione per chiedere informazioni su:

    • prezzo;
    • date;
    • livello richiesto;
    • modalità del corso.

    Lunghezza consigliata:

    • Task 1: 40–60 parole
    • Task 2: 80–100 parole
    • Task 3: 120–160 parole

    Checklist di revisione

    Controlla se il tuo testo:

    • ha un tono coerente con il contesto;
    • evita frasi troppo dirette;
    • usa forme di richiesta adeguate;
    • contiene opening e closing appropriati;
    • non suona né troppo brusco né eccessivamente rigido.

    Self-check finale

    Sai:

    • distinguere informal, neutral e formal English?
    • mitigare una richiesta?
    • rendere più professionale una frase troppo diretta?
    • scegliere il tono giusto per email, chat e richieste delicate?
    • riscrivere lo stesso messaggio in registri diversi?

    Se sì, stai usando l’inglese in modo molto più vicino a un vero livello B2.
