    Unità 28 – Emails and Messages

    🎯 Lesson Goals

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • scrivere email e messaggi con un tono adeguato al contesto, al rapporto con il destinatario e all’obiettivo comunicativo
    • distinguere con sicurezza tra informal, neutral professional e more formal English
    • organizzare una email in modo credibile: subject, opening, context, request / update, closing action, sign-off
    • formulare richieste, solleciti, conferme, aggiornamenti, scuse e follow-up in modo naturale
    • evitare gli errori tipici degli italofoni, soprattutto su register, eccessiva direttività, calchi dall’italiano e formule poco idiomatiche

    Questa unità è fondamentale perché a livello B1 non basta più “scrivere corretto”: bisogna scrivere in modo chiaro, efficiente e socialmente appropriato.

    🧠 Core idea: good written English is about register and purpose

    Molti studenti si concentrano solo sulla grammatica della frase singola. In realtà, una buona email o un buon messaggio dipendono da tre domande:

    1. Who am I writing to?
    2. Why am I writing?
    3. How direct should I be?

    Confronta:

    • Send me the updated file today.
    • Could you send me the updated file today, please?
    • Could you please send me the updated file by the end of the day?

    Tutte e tre le frasi sono grammaticalmente corrette, ma non comunicano la stessa cosa. La differenza è di tono, relazione e professionalità.

    A livello B1 l’obiettivo è imparare a scegliere la formula che suona naturale nel contesto giusto.

    1. The three main registers

    A. Informal

    Si usa con amici, persone con cui hai confidenza, chat veloci, messaggi personali.

    Typical openings

    • Hi,
    • Hi Tom,
    • Hey,
    • Just a quick message...
    • Sorry, I’ve only just seen your message.

    Typical closings

    • See you soon,
    • Talk soon,
    • Let me know,
    • Thanks,
    • Bye for now,

    B. Neutral professional

    È il registro più utile per la maggior parte delle email di lavoro o studio. Suona educato ma non rigido.

    Typical openings

    • Hello Anna,
    • Hello Mr Walker,
    • Thank you for your email.
    • I’m writing to confirm...
    • I’m writing to ask about...

    Typical closings

    • Best regards,
    • Kind regards,
    • Thank you,
    • Please let me know if you need anything else.

    C. More formal

    Si usa con aziende, istituzioni, persone senior, contesti amministrativi o situazioni delicate.

    Typical openings

    • Dear Ms Green,
    • Dear Sir or Madam,
    • I am writing to enquire about...
    • I would like to request...
    • I would be grateful if you could...

    Typical closings

    • Kind regards,
    • Yours sincerely, (se sai il nome)
    • Yours faithfully, (se usi Dear Sir or Madam in British English)

    A livello B1 non serve essere eccessivamente cerimoniosi, ma è utile riconoscere il grado di formalità.

    2. Email architecture: write in blocks, not in one long paragraph

    Una email professionale chiara segue spesso questa struttura:

    1. Subject line

    L’oggetto deve essere chiaro e specifico.

    Good examples

    • Meeting confirmation – Thursday 14 May
    • Request for information about the course
    • Updated report attached
    • Follow-up on yesterday’s call

    Weak examples

    • Information
    • Hello
    • Important
    • Question

    2. Opening

    L’apertura serve per stabilire il tono e introdurre il motivo del messaggio.

    • Hello Sara,
    • Dear Mr Hall,
    • Thanks for your message.
    • I’m writing to confirm the details of...
    • I’m writing to ask whether...

    3. Context

    Se necessario, dai un minimo di contesto.

    • Following our meeting yesterday...
    • As discussed this morning...
    • Further to your email...
    • I’m contacting you regarding...

    4. Main purpose

    Qui vai al punto:

    • Could you confirm the final schedule?
    • I’d like to request an extension.
    • I’m sending you the updated version.
    • We need to postpone the meeting.

    5. Action / next step

    Una buona email dice chiaramente cosa succede adesso.

    • Please let me know by Friday.
    • Could you reply when you have a moment?
    • I look forward to hearing from you.
    • Let me know if Tuesday works for you.

    6. Sign-off

    • Best regards,
    • Kind regards,
    • Many thanks,
    • See you soon, (solo informale / semi-informale)

    3. Common email functions and natural language

    A. Asking for information

    Neutral professional

    • I’m writing to ask for some information about...
    • Could you tell me whether...?
    • I’d like to know if...
    • Could you confirm...?

    More formal

    • I would like to enquire about...
    • I would be grateful if you could provide...

    B. Making a request

    • Could you send me the latest version?
    • Could you please review the attached document?
    • Would it be possible to reschedule the meeting?
    • Please let me know if this is possible.

    C. Giving an update

    • I’m writing to let you know that...
    • I’ve completed the first draft.
    • We’re still waiting for confirmation.
    • The project is currently on schedule.

    D. Confirming

    • I’m happy to confirm that...
    • This is to confirm our meeting at 3 p.m.
    • Thursday works well for me.
    • I can confirm that the payment has been received.

    E. Apologising / explaining delay

    • I’m sorry for the delay in replying.
    • Apologies for the late response.
    • I’m afraid I won’t be able to attend.
    • Sorry, but I need to move the meeting to next week.

    F. Following up

    • I’m just following up on my previous email.
    • I wanted to check whether you had a chance to review the document.
    • Just a quick reminder that...

    Queste funzioni sono più utili della pura grammatica, perché riflettono situazioni reali.

    4. Directness: polite does not mean weak

    Molti italofoni oscillano tra due estremi:

    • troppo diretti: Send me the contract today.
    • troppo lunghi o vaghi: I wanted to write maybe to ask if perhaps...

    L’inglese professionale efficace è spesso:

    • chiaro
    • breve
    • cortese
    • orientato all’azione

    Compare

    Too direct

    • Send me the slides today.

    Better

    • Could you send me the slides today, please?

    More efficient and professional

    • Could you please send me the slides by 4 p.m. today?

    L’ultima frase è la migliore perché unisce cortesia e chiarezza operativa.

    5. Emails vs messages: different channels, different expectations

    Messages

    I messaggi, specialmente su chat o app di lavoro, tendono a essere:

    • più brevi
    • meno strutturati
    • meno formali
    • più immediati

    Examples

    • Hi, I’m running 10 minutes late.
    • Are we still on for 4?
    • I’ve sent the file.
    • Can I call you later?

    Emails

    Le email sono preferibili quando:

    • serve traccia scritta più stabile
    • devi spiegare qualcosa con ordine
    • stai scrivendo a un cliente, docente, azienda o ufficio
    • devi allegare documenti
    • devi formulare una richiesta più ufficiale

    Examples

    • I’m writing to confirm the revised timeline.
    • Please find the updated budget attached.
    • Could you let me know if you approve the proposal?

    Un buon parlante B1 sa scegliere non solo le parole giuste, ma anche il canale giusto.

    6. Tone markers that make your writing sound natural

    Softer requests

    • Could you...?
    • Could you please...?
    • Would it be possible to...?
    • When you have a moment, could you...?

    Clear but polite action points

    • Please let me know by Thursday.
    • I’d appreciate your feedback.
    • Let me know if you’d prefer a different time.

    Friendly but professional warmth

    • I hope you’re well.
    • Thanks for your help.
    • Thanks again for your time.
    • It was great speaking with you yesterday.

    Closing signals

    • I look forward to your reply.
    • I look forward to hearing from you.
    • Please don’t hesitate to contact me if you have any questions.

    Queste formule aiutano molto a costruire un tono credibile senza risultare rigido.

    7. High-frequency professional patterns

    Asking

    • I’m writing to ask whether...
    • Could you confirm if...?
    • I’d like to know when...

    Sending / attaching

    • I’ve attached the file for your review.
    • Please find the document attached.
    • I’m sending you the latest version.

    Scheduling

    • Are you available on Tuesday afternoon?
    • Could we move the meeting to Friday?
    • Thursday works for me.
    • I’m afraid I need to reschedule.

    Clarifying

    • Just to clarify,
    • For reference,
    • As mentioned above,
    • To avoid confusion,

    Closing with next step

    • Please let me know what you think.
    • I look forward to your feedback.
    • Thanks in advance for your help.

    8. Reading: model texts with analysis

    A. Informal message

    Hi Laura,
    Just a quick message to check if you’re still free this evening.
    I’m finishing work a bit later than expected, so I may arrive around 7.30.
    Let me know if that still works for you.
    See you later!

    Why it works

    • apertura naturale
    • tono personale
    • informazione chiara
    • richiesta semplice
    • chiusura coerente con il rapporto

    B. Neutral professional email

    Hello Mr Reed,
    I’m writing to confirm our meeting tomorrow at 10 a.m.
    I’ve attached the updated agenda and a short summary of the main points we’ll discuss.
    Please let me know if you would like me to add anything before the meeting.
    Best regards,
    Martina Bianchi

    Why it works

    • motivo del messaggio immediato
    • informazione operativa chiara
    • allegato segnalato bene
    • invito all’azione finale
    • tono professionale, non rigido

    C. Follow-up email

    Hello Daniel,
    I’m just following up on my previous email about the training schedule.
    Could you let me know whether the final dates have been confirmed?
    This will help us organise the internal calendar for next month.
    Many thanks,
    Elisa

    Why it works

    • follow-up gentile
    • richiesta precisa
    • spiega perché la risposta serve
    • tono professionale e collaborativo

    🗣️ Speaking Drills

    Leggi ad alta voce queste formule come blocchi pronti, non parola per parola:

    • I’m writing to ask about...
    • I’m writing to confirm that...
    • Could you please send me...?
    • Please let me know if this works for you.
    • I’m sorry for the delay in replying.
    • I’m just following up on my previous email.
    • I’ve attached the updated document for your review.

    Controlled practice

    Completa a voce:

    • I’m writing to confirm ____.
    • Could you please send me ____ by Friday?
    • I’m sorry for the delay in ____.
    • I’d like to know whether ____.
    • Please let me know if ____ works for you.

    Register drill

    Trasforma a voce da troppo diretto a professionale:

    1. Send me the file.
    2. Change the meeting.
    3. Answer me today.
    4. I want information about the course.

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi due testi.

    Task 1 – Professional email

    Scrivi una email di 120–150 parole per una di queste situazioni:

    1. chiedere informazioni su un corso o servizio
    2. confermare e organizzare un meeting
    3. inviare un file e spiegare cosa contiene
    4. fare follow-up su una richiesta precedente

    Il testo deve includere:

    • subject line
    • opening appropriata
    • motivo del messaggio
    • richiesta o aggiornamento chiaro
    • closing action
    • sign-off adeguato

    Task 2 – Short work message

    Scrivi un messaggio di 40–60 parole a un collega per:

    • avvisare di un ritardo
    • cambiare rapidamente un orario
    • confermare l’invio di un file

    Model opening

    Subject: Request for information about the evening course
    Hello,
    I’m writing to ask for some information about your evening English course.
    I’d like to know the start date, the weekly timetable and whether the lessons are available online.

    🔊 Pronunciation and delivery

    Anche nella scrittura professionale, leggere ad alta voce aiuta a sentire se una frase è naturale.

    Focus words

    • reply → /rɪˈplaɪ/
    • regards → /rɪˈɡɑːrdz/
    • attached → /əˈtætʃt/
    • forward → /ˈfɔːwəd/ o /ˈfɔrwərd/
    • schedule → BrE /ˈʃedjuːl/ – AmE /ˈskedʒuːl/
    • available → /əˈveɪləbəl/

    Practice lines

    • Please find the updated file attached.
    • I look forward to hearing from you.
    • Could you confirm whether you’re available next Tuesday?

    Se la frase è difficile da leggere ad alta voce, spesso è troppo lunga o poco naturale anche da scrivere.

    ⚠️ Error Clinic for Italian Speakers

    1. Translating directly from Italian openings

    ❌ I write you for ask information.
    ✅ I’m writing to ask for information.

    ❌ I contact you for the course.
    ✅ I’m contacting you about the course.

    2. Using “I want” in professional requests

    ❌ I want to know the price.
    ✅ I’d like to know the price.

    ❌ I want an answer today.
    ✅ Could you please reply today?

    3. Being too direct with imperatives

    ❌ Send me the document.
    ✅ Could you send me the document, please?

    ❌ Confirm the date.
    ✅ Could you confirm the date?

    4. Wrong or unnatural closings

    ❌ Dear friend
    ✅ Hi Marco / Hello Marco

    ❌ Kisses / Hug in a professional email
    ✅ Best regards / Kind regards

    5. Confusing “reply” and “answer”

    In molti contesti scritti, reply è più naturale di answer.

    • Thank you for your reply.
    • I look forward to your reply.

    6. Overusing long formal phrases in normal emails

    A volte gli studenti cercano di suonare professionali usando formule troppo pesanti.

    ❌ I would be extremely grateful if you could kindly provide me with... (possibile, ma spesso troppo)
    ✅ Could you please send me...? (più naturale nella maggior parte dei casi)

    Il buon inglese professionale è spesso semplice ma preciso.

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa con una parola o espressione adatta.

    1. I’m writing to __ whether the meeting has been confirmed.
    2. Please find the updated file __.
    3. I’m sorry for the delay in __.
    4. Could you __ the final price, please?
    5. I look forward to __ from you.
    6. Best __,
    7. Just a quick message to let you __ that I’m running late.
    8. Thanks for getting back __ me.

    🔄 Transformation Drill

    Rendi più naturale e professionale.

    1. Send me the new version today.
    2. I want information about your course.
    3. Answer me soon.
    4. I write to you for confirm the meeting.
    5. Hi friend, I wait your answer.

    Poi riscrivi le frasi in due registri diversi:

    • semi-formal email
    • short informal message

    🧠 Final Speaking Task

    Parla per 2–3 minuti su uno di questi temi:

    • la differenza tra scrivere a un amico e scrivere a un’azienda
    • come rendere una richiesta chiara ma cortese
    • quando usare un messaggio e quando usare una email
    • gli errori più comuni degli italiani nella scrittura professionale in inglese

    Per una buona performance:

    • usa esempi concreti
    • confronta almeno due registri diversi
    • includi almeno 6 formule utili della lezione
    • spiega non solo cosa scrivere, ma anche perché

    🚀 Performance Task

    Scrivi una email professionale completa di 140–180 parole in una di queste situazioni:

    Option A – Request

    Vuoi informazioni su un corso, un servizio o una collaborazione.

    Option B – Follow-up

    Hai già scritto una email e vuoi fare un sollecito educato.

    Option C – Confirmation + attachment

    Confermi un meeting e invii un documento allegato.

    La tua email deve includere:

    • un subject line chiaro
    • apertura appropriata
    • tono coerente
    • un motivo del messaggio espresso con chiarezza
    • almeno una richiesta formulata in modo cortese
    • una closing line con prossimo passo
    • un sign-off credibile

    A strong B1 writer does not just know useful phrases. They know how to sound clear, polite and appropriate in real written communication.
