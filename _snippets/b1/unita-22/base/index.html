    Unità 22 – Present Perfect vs Past Simple

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità saprai:

    • distinguere tra Present Perfect e Past Simple
    • capire se il focus è su quando è successa un’azione o sul suo legame con il presente
    • usare correttamente parole chiave come already, just, yet, ever, never, yesterday, last week
    • evitare gli errori più comuni nella scelta del tempo verbale

    🧠 La differenza fondamentale

    Usa il Present Perfect quando:

    • non dici quando è successo qualcosa
    • parli di un’esperienza di vita
    • il risultato è importante adesso
    • il periodo di tempo non è finito

    Examples

    • I have seen that film.
    • She has just arrived.
    • We have finished the project.
    • I have had three meetings today.

    Usa il Past Simple quando:

    • dici quando è successo qualcosa
    • parli di un momento passato finito
    • racconti un evento concluso

    Examples

    • I saw that film last weekend.
    • She arrived ten minutes ago.
    • We finished the project yesterday.
    • I had three meetings yesterday.

    ⚖️ Confronto diretto

    Present Perfect Past Simple
    I have been to London. I went to London in 2022.
    She has finished her work. She finished her work at 6.
    We have seen this series. We saw this series last month.
    I have spoken to him today. I spoke to him yesterday.

    📚 Signal words

    Present Perfect

    • already
    • just
    • yet
    • ever
    • never
    • today
    • this week
    • this month

    Past Simple

    • yesterday
    • last week
    • last month
    • in 2021
    • two days ago
    • when I was a child

    🎧 Dialogue

    A: Have you finished the report yet?
    B: Yes, I have. I’ve just sent it.

    A: Great. When did you send it?
    B: I sent it five minutes ago.

    A: Have you ever written a report in English before?
    B: Yes, I have, but I wrote my first one only last year.

    🗣️ Speaking

    Completa le frasi:

    • I have already ...
    • I haven’t ... yet.
    • I saw ... yesterday.
    • I went to ... last ...
    • Have you ever ...?

    Fai domande a un compagno:

    • Have you ever travelled abroad alone?
    • Have you finished your homework yet?
    • When did you last watch a film in English?
    • Did you study English yesterday?

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi 8 frasi:

    • 4 con il Present Perfect
    • 4 con il Past Simple

    Usa almeno:

    • one sentence with already
    • one with yet
    • one with ever
    • one with yesterday
    • one with last week

    Esempio:

    I have already done my homework.
    I haven’t called my friend yet.
    I saw my teacher yesterday.
    We visited Rome last week.

    🔊 Pronunciation

    Fai attenzione alla differenza tra:

    • I’ve finished
    • I finished

    Nel parlato veloce, I’ve è molto breve. Allenati a sentire e produrre la differenza.

    ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

    ❌ I have done it yesterday.
    ✅ I did it yesterday.

    ❌ I have seen him last week.
    ✅ I saw him last week.

    ❌ Have you ever went there?
    ✅ Have you ever been there?

    ❌ I went to London many times.
    ✅ I have been to London many times.

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Scegli la forma corretta:

    1. I have seen / saw that film yesterday.
    2. She has just arrived / just arrived.
    3. We finished / have finished the meeting ten minutes ago.
    4. Have you ever been / Did you ever go to Scotland?
    5. I have had / had two coffees today.
    6. He went / has gone to Paris in 2023.

    🔄 Trasforma

    Trasforma usando il tempo corretto:

    1. I saw that film. → I have ______ that film.
    2. She finished the task. → She has ______ the task.
    3. We went to Spain last year. → We have ______ to Spain.

    🧠 Think & Speak

    Parla per 1–2 minuti di:

    • 3 esperienze che hai fatto nella vita
    • 3 azioni con un tempo preciso nel passato
    • una cosa che hai già fatto oggi
    • una cosa che non hai ancora fatto

    The best way to choose the tense is to ask: When? or Relevant now?
