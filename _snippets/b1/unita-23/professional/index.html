    Unità 23 – Future Forms (Will / Going to / Present Continuous)

    🎯 Lesson Goals

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • scegliere tra will, be going to e Present Continuous in base al significato comunicativo, non solo alla regola formale
    • parlare di decisioni spontanee, intenzioni già formulate, appuntamenti organizzati e previsioni con maggiore precisione
    • riconoscere le sfumature tra futuro “pensato”, futuro “organizzato” e futuro “deciso sul momento”
    • usare strutture molto frequenti nel parlato reale, come I’ll, I’m going to, I’m meeting..., I think... will..., It’s going to...
    • evitare gli errori tipici degli italofoni, specialmente la tendenza a usare una sola forma per tutti i contesti

    Questa unità è fondamentale a livello B1 perché il parlante deve iniziare a usare il futuro in modo flessibile e naturale. Il punto non è memorizzare tre etichette, ma capire che ogni forma trasmette una prospettiva diversa.

    🧠 Core idea: future meaning depends on viewpoint

    In inglese il futuro non si esprime con un solo “tempo verbale”. Si sceglie una forma in base a come il parlante vede l’evento.

    Chiediti sempre:

    1. È una decisione che sto prendendo adesso?
    2. È un piano già deciso?
    3. È un appuntamento o un programma già organizzato?
    4. È una previsione basata sulla mia opinione o su un’evidenza visibile?

    Le tre forme di questa unità coprono proprio questi significati.

    1. Will: decision now, promise, offer, prediction

    Formula

    subject + will + base form

    • I will call you later.
    • We’ll send the file this afternoon.
    • She won’t agree.
    • Will they come?

    Main uses

    A. Spontaneous decisions

    La decisione nasce nel momento in cui parli.

    • The phone is ringing. I’ll get it.
    • You look tired. I’ll make some coffee.
    • We don’t have any bread. I’ll go and buy some.

    B. Offers and promises

    • I’ll help you with that report.
    • I won’t tell anyone.
    • I’ll call you as soon as I arrive.

    C. Personal predictions

    Parli di ciò che pensi o immagini.

    • I think the meeting will be useful.
    • I’m sure she’ll enjoy the course.
    • They probably won’t finish on time.

    Common discourse markers

    • I think...
    • I’m sure...
    • probably
    • maybe
    • I promise...
    • don’t worry, I’ll...

    2. Be going to: prior intention and visible evidence

    Formula

    subject + be + going to + base form

    • I’m going to apply for the job.
    • They’re going to move in June.
    • It isn’t going to work.
    • Are you going to join us?

    Main uses

    A. Prior plan or intention

    La decisione è stata presa prima del momento in cui parli.

    • I’m going to learn Spanish this year.
    • We’re going to redesign the website.
    • She’s going to look for a new flat.

    Qui non è necessario che tutto sia già prenotato: basta che ci sia un’intenzione chiara.

    B. Prediction based on present evidence

    • Look at those clouds. It’s going to rain.
    • He’s driving too fast. He’s going to have an accident.
    • This project is going to be expensive.

    La differenza con will è che qui il parlante si basa su un segnale osservabile o su una situazione già evidente.

    3. Present Continuous for future arrangements

    Formula

    subject + be + verb-ing

    • I’m meeting the supplier tomorrow.
    • We’re having dinner with Clara on Friday.
    • She isn’t working next Monday.
    • Are you seeing the doctor this week?

    Main use

    Il Present Continuous si usa per programmi organizzati, spesso con elementi concreti già fissati: orario, luogo, persone coinvolte, prenotazione, agenda.

    • I’m flying to Amsterdam on Tuesday morning.
    • We’re meeting the client at 11:30.
    • They’re staying at a hotel near the station.

    Key nuance

    going to = piano / intenzione
    Present Continuous = appuntamento / arrangiamento già impostato

    Compare:

    • I’m going to see the dentist soon. (intenzione / piano)
    • I’m seeing the dentist at 3 on Thursday. (appuntamento fissato)

    4. The real contrast: same future, different angle

    A. Plan vs arrangement

    • I’m going to visit Paris this summer. (intenzione o piano generale)
    • I’m flying to Paris on 12 July. (organizzazione concreta)

    B. Opinion vs evidence

    • I think sales will improve next quarter. (opinione)
    • Sales are going to improve. We’ve already signed three major contracts. (evidenza / base concreta)

    C. Decision now vs decision before

    • We need someone to take notes. I’ll do it. (decisione spontanea)
    • I’m going to take notes during the meeting. (decisione già presa prima)

    D. Arrangement vs promise

    • I’m meeting Julia after work. (programma fissato)
    • I’ll meet Julia after work if she’s free. (decisione o proposta che emerge ora / non ancora organizzata)

    5. Time expressions that help the choice

    Spesso il futuro si accompagna a espressioni come:

    • tonight
    • tomorrow
    • this evening
    • next week
    • on Friday
    • in two days
    • later
    • soon
    • at 8:30

    Attenzione: la presenza di una time expression non basta da sola a scegliere la forma.

    • I’ll call you tomorrow. ✅
    • I’m going to call you tomorrow. ✅
    • I’m calling you tomorrow. ✅

    Sono tutte possibili, ma il significato cambia:

    • I’ll call you tomorrow → decisione / promessa
    • I’m going to call you tomorrow → intenzione
    • I’m calling you tomorrow → piano già inserito nella tua agenda mentale o reale

    📚 High-frequency patterns you really need

    With will

    • I’ll do it.
    • I’ll let you know.
    • I’ll be there.
    • I won’t forget.
    • Do you think it’ll work?

    With going to

    • I’m going to start next month.
    • We’re going to change the plan.
    • She isn’t going to accept that.
    • What are you going to do after the course?

    With future Present Continuous

    • I’m meeting the team at 9.
    • We’re leaving early tomorrow.
    • Are you coming with us tonight?
    • They’re staying with friends.

    🎧 Reading: planning a work trip

    Nadia: Have you booked your trip to Berlin yet?
    Leo: Yes, I have. I’m flying on Wednesday morning and I’m meeting the client in the afternoon.
    Nadia: Great. Where are you staying?
    Leo: I’m staying near Alexanderplatz.
    Nadia: Nice. What are you going to discuss with the client?
    Leo: We’re going to talk about the new contract and the delivery schedule.
    Nadia: Sounds important. Do you think they’ll accept the proposal?
    Leo: I think they will, but they’re probably going to ask for a few changes.
    Nadia: That makes sense. Don’t worry, I’ll help you prepare before you leave.

    Comprehension

    1. Which future form is used for Leo’s travel arrangements?
    2. Which sentence expresses Nadia’s offer?
    3. Which form is used for Leo’s opinion about the proposal?
    4. Why is going to used in they’re probably going to ask for a few changes?

    🗣️ Guided Speaking

    A. Decision now

    React immediately to these situations:

    • Your colleague forgot the slides.
    • Your friend looks stressed.
    • There is no one at reception.
    • A customer is waiting for an answer.

    Use I’ll... in complete sentences.

    B. Prior intentions

    Answer with going to:

    • What are you going to improve in your English this month?
    • What are you going to do this weekend?
    • Are you going to change anything in your routine?
    • What are you going to study next?

    C. Arrangements

    Answer with Present Continuous:

    • Who are you meeting this week?
    • What are you doing on Friday evening?
    • Are you travelling anywhere soon?
    • When are you seeing your teacher / manager / client next?

    D. Predictions

    Make one sentence with will and one with going to for each topic:

    • the weather
    • your work or studies
    • technology
    • your English progress

    Then explain why you chose each form.

    ✍️ Writing task

    Write 140–180 words about your plans for the next two weeks.

    Include:

    • at least 2 sentences with will
    • at least 3 sentences with going to
    • at least 3 sentences with Present Continuous for future arrangements
    • one prediction based on opinion
    • one prediction based on evidence

    Suggested opening:

    The next two weeks are going to be quite busy for me. I’m meeting...

    🔊 Pronunciation and connected speech

    Common contractions

    • I will → I’ll
    • you will → you’ll
    • we will → we’ll
    • is going to → ’s going to
    • are going to → ’re going to

    Natural spoken rhythm

    • I’ll call you later.
    • We’re going to start at nine.
    • I’m meeting them after lunch.

    Nel parlato informale potresti sentire gonna, ma a questo livello è più utile riconoscerla e capire quando compare, senza forzarne l’uso in modo artificiale.

    ⚠️ Error clinic: typical Italian mistakes

    ❌ I will to send the email.
    ✅ I will send the email.

    ❌ I going to change job.
    ✅ I’m going to change jobs. / I’m going to change my job situation.

    ❌ Tomorrow I am go to Milan.
    ✅ Tomorrow I’m going to Milan. / Tomorrow I’m going to go to Milan.

    ❌ I will meeting the customer at 4.
    ✅ I’m meeting the customer at 4.

    ❌ It will rain, look at the sky.
    ✅ It’s going to rain. Look at the sky.

    ❌ I think it’s going to be a good idea. (possible, but often Italians overuse going to for personal opinion)
    ✅ I think it will be a good idea.

    Why these mistakes happen

    Molti italiani cercano un equivalente unico di “futuro semplice”, ma l’inglese ragiona in modo più sfumato. Non devi tradurre il tempo verbale italiano: devi esprimere l’intenzione comunicativa.

    🔁 Transformation drill

    Riformula la frase scegliendo la forma più naturale.

    1. I have already decided: visit my aunt this weekend.
    2. The phone is ringing: answer it.
    3. Appointment with the accountant at 11 tomorrow.
    4. Look at the score: our team lose.
    5. My opinion: the new system be useful.
    6. We have tickets and a hotel reservation: stay in Rome for three nights.

    ✅ Mixed Review

    Choose the best option.

    1. I think the presentation will / is going to / is presenting be successful.
    2. We will meet / are going to meet / are meeting the supplier at 2 p.m. tomorrow.
    3. Don’t worry, I will / am going to / am help you with the report.
    4. Look at that child on the bike! He will fall / is going to fall / is falling.
    5. I will start / am going to start / am starting a new online course next month; I’ve already enrolled.
    6. What will you do / are you going to do / are you doing after class tonight? (more than one answer may be possible — explain the nuance)

    🧠 Think & Speak

    Parla per 2–3 minuti di:

    • un progetto personale che hai intenzione di iniziare
    • tre appuntamenti o programmi già organizzati
    • due decisioni che potresti prendere sul momento in una situazione reale
    • due previsioni: una basata su opinione e una basata su evidenza

    Obiettivo: usare tutte e tre le forme in modo consapevole e naturale.

    🚀 Final task

    Prepare a short spoken presentation titled:

    My plans, arrangements and predictions for the next month

    Include:

    • one opening sentence with overview
    • two examples with will
    • two with going to
    • two with Present Continuous
    • one contrast sentence that shows you know the difference

    Example frame:

    I’m going to focus more on my English next month. I’m meeting my teacher twice a week, and I’m starting a new reading routine. I think this will help me become more confident...

    At B1 level, speaking naturally about the future means choosing the form that matches your intention, your evidence and your degree of organisation.
