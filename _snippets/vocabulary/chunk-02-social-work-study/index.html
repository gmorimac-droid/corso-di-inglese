    Chunk 02 social work study

    Chunk 2 — Social, Study, Work, and Communication Vocabulary

    Questo secondo chunk raccoglie il lessico funzionale e relazionale che diventa centrale da B1 a B2, ma che parte da bisogni molto concreti già presenti ad A2.
    Qui il focus non è solo “sapere più parole”, ma imparare a gestire situazioni reali: studiare, lavorare, collaborare, comunicare, spiegare problemi, prendere decisioni, discutere idee e muoversi in contesti contemporanei.

    Il lessico è organizzato per aree d’uso, con una progressione interna: - core vocabulary per la base operativa - useful combinations per evitare traduzioni letterali - typical sentence patterns per lo speaking e il writing - common mistakes for Italian speakers - blocchi Professional per collocations, register, nuance, tone e lexical precision

    In questa versione rigenerata, il Core vocabulary è stato reso più leggibile e più utile per lo studio attivo tramite tabelle con: - word / expression - significato in italiano - example

    L’obiettivo non è solo capire il lessico, ma usarlo meglio in contesti reali.

    1. Study, Learning, and Academic Life

    Core vocabulary

    Word / Expression Significato Example
    study studiare I study English every day.
    learn imparare Children learn quickly.
    lesson lezione Today's lesson was useful.
    class classe / lezione I have class at 6 p.m.
    course corso I'm taking an online course.
    unit unità We're revising Unit 3.
    level livello My level is B1.
    teacher insegnante The teacher explained it clearly.
    student studente Every student needs a notebook.
    classmate compagno di classe My classmate helped me.
    homework compiti I finished my homework.
    exercise esercizio This grammar exercise is difficult.
    task compito / consegna We have a listening task today.
    test test / verifica We had a vocabulary test yesterday.
    exam esame I'm studying for an exam.
    mark / grade voto She got a good grade.
    mistake errore I made a mistake in exercise 2.
    correction correzione The teacher gave us corrections.
    answer risposta Check your answers carefully.
    question domanda Can I ask a question?
    note appunto / nota I wrote a note in the margin.
    notebook quaderno I always bring a notebook.
    topic argomento Today's topic is travel.
    subject materia English is my favourite subject.
    skill abilità Listening is an important skill.
    progress progresso I'm making progress.
    method metodo I need a better study method.
    revision ripasso I usually do revision on Sundays.
    deadline scadenza The deadline is Friday.
    result risultato I'm happy with my result.

    Useful combinations

    • attend a class
    • take a course
    • study for an exam
    • revise for a test
    • hand in homework
    • do an exercise
    • complete a task
    • make progress
    • improve your skills
    • take notes
    • check your answers
    • correct a mistake
    • miss a lesson
    • fall behind
    • catch up
    • pass an exam
    • fail a test
    • get a good mark
    • develop a study method

    Typical sentence patterns

    • I'm studying for an exam next week.
    • I need to revise this unit again.
    • We had a vocabulary test yesterday.
    • I'm making good progress, but I still make mistakes with tenses.
    • Please hand in your homework by Friday.
    • I missed the lesson, so I need to catch up.

    Common mistakes for Italian speakers

    • I give an exam → I take an exam / sit an exam
    • I do a mistake → I make a mistake
    • I lost the lesson → I missed the lesson
    • I read at university → I study at university
    • I have done an exam and I have taken 28 → I took an exam and got 28
    • I prepare an exam can work in some contexts, but usually study for an exam is more natural

    Mini examples

    • I usually revise in the evening because I'm more focused then.
    • She passed the exam, but she wasn't happy with her grade.
    • I'm trying to improve my listening skills and expand my vocabulary.

    Professional upgrade

    At higher levels, study vocabulary becomes more precise: - assignment - assessment - submission - feedback - learning outcome - academic performance - self-study - study load - revision strategy - time management - concentration span - retention - academic writing - critical thinking - source - reference

    Higher-value expressions

    • I'm working on improving my academic writing.
    • The feedback was useful, especially on structure and clarity.
    • I need a more effective revision strategy.
    • My main challenge is balancing study load and work.
    • The course focuses on both language accuracy and communicative effectiveness.

    Nuance and lexical precision

    • study = general learning effort
    • learn = acquire knowledge or skill
    • revise = review what you studied before an exam
    • review = more general, often neutral or professional
    • mark = common in British English
    • grade = common in American English
    • feedback = comments on performance, not just correction

    Upgrade lab

    Basic: I study English.
    More natural: I'm studying English at an intermediate level.
    More developed: I'm focusing on speaking, vocabulary, and listening comprehension.
    More professional: I'm following a structured course and working on accuracy, fluency, and vocabulary range.

    duty mansione / dovere One of my duties is checking orders.
    responsibility responsabilità I'm responsible for weekly reports.

    sector settore I work in the finance sector.
    industry industria / settore The tech industry changes quickly.

    Useful combinations

    • work as a designer
    • work in marketing
    • work for a company
    • work with clients
    • have responsibility for something
    • manage a project
    • attend a meeting
    • write a report
    • meet a deadline
    • gain experience
    • change jobs
    • get promoted
    • apply for a position
    • do training
    • work full-time
    • work part-time
    • work remotely
    • solve a problem
    • support a colleague
    • deal with customers

    Typical sentence patterns

    • I work in the finance sector.
    • My role involves customer support and project coordination.
    • We have a meeting every Monday morning.
    • I'm responsible for preparing weekly reports.
    • She's looking for a new job because she wants more flexibility.
    • I work with an international team, so I use English every day.

    Common mistakes for Italian speakers

    • I make experience → I gain experience
    • I do smart working → I work remotely / I work from home
    • I have a work → I have a job
    • My work is stressful but I like my work → often better: My job is stressful, but I like what I do
    • I am responsible to manage → I am responsible for managing
    • I participate to a meeting → I participate in a meeting / attend a meeting

    Mini examples

    • I work for a software company, mainly with international clients.
    • One of my main responsibilities is managing deadlines.
    • I'm thinking about changing jobs to find a better work-life balance.

    Professional upgrade

    Useful vocabulary for more developed work contexts: - workload - deliverable - stakeholder - alignment - objective - target - performance - output - workflow - coordination - supervision - accountability - leadership - initiative - onboarding - career path - role description - promotion cycle - strategic priority

    Higher-value expressions

    • My workload has increased over the last few months.
    • We need better alignment across teams.
    • The project has clear objectives but limited resources.
    • One of my strengths is cross-functional coordination.
    • I'm looking for a role with more responsibility and long-term growth.

    Nuance and lexical precision

    • job = specific employment
    • work = activity in general
    • role = function within an organisation
    • position = often formal or HR-related
    • career = long-term path
    • task = single piece of work
    • responsibility = area you are accountable for
    • duty = obligation, often formal or procedural

    Register notes

    • boss is common but can sound informal.
    • manager is more neutral and professional.
    • staff is a collective noun, not normally pluralised as staffs in this meaning.
    • collaborator is often a false friend. In English, prefer:
    • colleague
    • coworker / co-worker
    • team member
    • collaborator only in specific academic/creative contexts

    Upgrade lab

    Basic: I work in an office.
    More natural: I work in an office and support the sales team.
    More developed: My role involves client communication and internal coordination.
    More professional: I work in operations, where I coordinate internal processes and support client-facing projects.

    3. Office Life, Projects, Meetings, and Workflow

    Core vocabulary

    Word / Expression Significato Example
    office ufficio The office is quiet today.
    desk scrivania My laptop is on my desk.
    laptop portatile I forgot my laptop at home.
    document documento Please review the document.
    file file I shared the file with the team.
    folder cartella Put it in the project folder.
    calendar calendario / agenda Check the calendar before booking.
    agenda ordine del giorno What's on the agenda today?
    meeting riunione We have a team meeting at 10.
    call chiamata / call Let's have a quick call later.
    video call videochiamata Join the video call at 3 p.m.
    presentation presentazione She's preparing the presentation.
    slide slide / diapositiva I've updated the last slide.
    update aggiornamento I'll send you an update later.
    task list lista di attività Check the task list first.
    deadline scadenza The deadline is very tight.
    priority priorità This is our top priority.
    progress avanzamento / progresso We're making steady progress.
    issue problema / questione We need to solve one issue.
    solution soluzione This could be a practical solution.
    delay ritardo There was a delay in approval.
    approval approvazione We need final approval.
    process processo The process is too slow.
    step passaggio / fase What's the next step?
    plan piano We need a clear plan.
    schedule pianificazione / tabella The project is on schedule.
    project progetto The project starts next week.
    team member membro del team Every team member received the update.

    Useful combinations

    • schedule a meeting
    • join a call
    • share a document
    • send an update
    • discuss a project
    • set a deadline
    • change priorities
    • solve an issue
    • ask for approval
    • follow a process
    • keep track of progress
    • postpone a meeting
    • prepare a presentation
    • make a decision
    • review a document
    • assign a task
    • move something to next week
    • stay on schedule
    • fall behind schedule

    Typical sentence patterns

    • Let's schedule a meeting for Thursday afternoon.
    • I'll send you an update by the end of the day.
    • We're slightly behind schedule, but we're still on track overall.
    • Could you share the latest version of the document?
    • We need approval before we move to the next step.
    • The main issue is that the deadline is too tight.

    Common mistakes for Italian speakers

    • make a reunion → have a meeting
    • respect the deadline → meet the deadline
    • actual version → current version / latest version
    • control the progress → monitor the progress / keep track of progress
    • open the camera → turn on the camera
    • I close the call → I end the call / wrap up the call

    Mini examples

    • Can we move the meeting to tomorrow morning?
    • I've updated the slide deck and shared it with the team.
    • We're waiting for final approval before launching the new page.

    Professional upgrade

    More developed project vocabulary: - milestone - blocker - dependency - follow-up - action point - decision-maker - scope - handover - implementation - rollout - ownership - escalation - bottleneck - timeline - resource allocation - status update

    Higher-value expressions

    • We need to clarify ownership before moving forward.
    • The project is at risk because of a dependency on another team.
    • Let's capture the key action points before we wrap up.
    • The rollout has been delayed due to a technical issue.
    • We need to avoid unnecessary escalation and solve this directly.

    Nuance and lexical precision

    • issue is usually more neutral and professional than problem
    • deadline = fixed final date
    • timeline = broader sequence of dates
    • delay = something happens later than planned
    • postpone = move to a later time intentionally
    • reschedule = change the time or date

    Meeting language upgrade

    • Let's align on priorities.
    • Could you walk us through the latest update?
    • Just to clarify, who's responsible for the next step?
    • Before we close, let's confirm deadlines and ownership.

    tablet tablet She reads on her tablet.
    app app / applicazione This app is really useful.
    platform piattaforma We use a different platform now.
    account account I can't access my account.
    password password Reset your password first.
    login accesso / login My login isn't working.

    notification notifica I got a notification.

    Useful combinations

    • log in to your account
    • reset your password
    • open a file
    • attach a document
    • share a link
    • update a page
    • check your settings
    • turn on your microphone
    • lose the connection
    • upload a file
    • download an attachment
    • send a message
    • reply in the chat
    • join an online meeting
    • share your screen
    • report a bug
    • fix an error
    • install an update

    Typical sentence patterns

    • I can't log in to my account.
    • Please attach the file to the email.
    • My connection is unstable, so I may need to turn my camera off.
    • Could you share the link in the chat?
    • The app needs an update before it works properly.
    • I uploaded the wrong file by mistake.

    Common mistakes for Italian speakers

    • I have problem of connection → I have a connection problem
    • Can you send me the invite? works, but often more natural: Can you send me the link / invitation?
    • I have blocked the microphone → My microphone isn't working / My microphone is muted
    • I cancel the meeting when you mean postpone → I postponed / rescheduled the meeting
    • I am in mute → I'm muted
    • Open the camera → Turn on the camera

    Mini examples

    • I'll upload the revised file in a few minutes.
    • Sorry, I was muted and didn't hear the question.
    • The system crashed while I was sharing my screen.

    Professional upgrade

    More advanced digital communication vocabulary: - interface - access rights - permissions - integration - feature - functionality - outage - workaround - user experience - technical issue - troubleshooting - support ticket - sync - backup - data loss - deployment - dashboard

    Higher-value expressions

    • We're experiencing a temporary outage.
    • As a workaround, you can use the browser version.
    • I don't seem to have the right permissions.
    • The new feature improves the user experience.
    • Please open a support ticket if the issue continues.

    Nuance and register

    • bug = software defect
    • issue = general technical or process problem
    • error = can be human or technical
    • outage = service interruption
    • workaround = temporary practical solution
    • crash = system or app stops suddenly

    5. Emails, Messages, Requests, and Follow-ups

    Core vocabulary

    Word / Expression Significato Example
    email email I sent you an email yesterday.
    message messaggio Did you read my message?
    subject line oggetto dell’email The subject line is too vague.
    attachment allegato The attachment is missing.
    reply risposta / rispondere Thanks for your reply.
    response risposta We need a quick response.
    request richiesta I received a formal request.
    reminder promemoria This is a quick reminder.
    confirmation conferma Please send confirmation by Friday.
    update aggiornamento I'll send you an update soon.
    invitation invito Thanks for the invitation.
    availability disponibilità Please confirm your availability.
    schedule agenda / calendario My schedule is full today.
    detail dettaglio Let me know the details.
    information informazioni I'm writing to ask for information.
    apology scusa / messaggio di scuse We sent an apology to the client.
    thanks ringraziamenti Many thanks for your help.
    greeting saluto iniziale Use a polite greeting.
    closing formula di chiusura The closing sounds too informal.
    signature firma Add your full signature.
    tone tono The tone is clear and polite.
    formal formale This email is quite formal.
    informal informale That's too informal for a client.
    polite educato / cortese Try to sound polite.
    urgent urgente This is an urgent request.
    follow-up sollecito / follow-up I'll send a follow-up tomorrow.
    clarification chiarimento We need some clarification.

    Useful combinations

    • send an email
    • reply to a message
    • ask for information
    • confirm your availability
    • apologise for the delay
    • follow up on something
    • send a reminder
    • attach a file
    • clarify a point
    • thank someone for their help
    • ask for confirmation
    • check whether something is possible
    • get back to someone
    • keep someone updated
    • write a formal email
    • make a polite request

    Typical sentence patterns

    • I'm writing to ask for some information.
    • Could you please confirm your availability for Friday?
    • Sorry for the late reply.
    • I'm following up on my previous message.
    • Please let me know if you need any further details.
    • Thank you in advance for your help.

    Common mistakes for Italian speakers

    • I wait your reply → I look forward to your reply / I await your reply (formal)
    • I ask you kindly → better: Could you please...?
    • I write you for... → I'm writing to...
    • Thanks in advance for your answer → Thank you in advance for your reply
    • Dear Mr. Marco → use correct name format; often Dear Mr Rossi
    • Kind regards is fine, but avoid mixing very casual and very formal tone in the same email

    Mini examples

    • I'm writing to follow up on our conversation last week.
    • Could you send me the updated document when you have a moment?
    • Thank you for your patience and sorry again for the inconvenience.

    Professional upgrade

    More advanced email and messaging vocabulary: - acknowledgment - availability window - rescheduling - clarification request - turnaround time - next steps - action required - escalation - courtesy copy - point of contact - misunderstanding - alignment - pending item - request for approval

    Higher-value expressions

    • Just a quick follow-up on the email below.
    • Could you clarify what you mean by “final version”?
    • Please find attached the updated file.
    • Let me know how you'd like to proceed.
    • I'd appreciate it if you could confirm by tomorrow.
    • At this stage, no action is required from your side.

    Tone and register

    • Can you send it today? = direct, neutral
    • Could you send it today, if possible? = softer
    • Would you mind sending it today? = polite, but context-sensitive
    • I'd appreciate it if you could send it today. = more formal / professional

    Upgrade lab

    Basic: Send me the file.
    Better: Could you send me the file, please?
    Professional: Could you please send me the latest version of the file when you have a moment?
    More developed: I'd appreciate it if you could send me the latest version today, as I need it for tomorrow's meeting.

    6. Opinions, Agreement, Disagreement, and Discussion Language

    Core vocabulary

    Word / Expression Significato Example
    opinion opinione In my opinion, it's a good idea.
    idea idea That's an interesting idea.
    point punto / osservazione That's a good point.
    argument argomento / tesi The argument is convincing.
    reason motivo / ragione What's the main reason?
    example esempio Let me give an example.
    discussion discussione / confronto We had a useful discussion.
    debate dibattito The debate was lively.
    agreement accordo We finally reached an agreement.
    disagreement disaccordo There was some disagreement.
    advantage vantaggio One advantage is the price.
    disadvantage svantaggio The main disadvantage is time.
    solution soluzione We need a better solution.
    perspective prospettiva From my perspective, it's risky.
    view punto di vista I understand your view.
    evidence prova / evidenza We need more evidence.
    fact fatto That's an important fact.
    claim affermazione / tesi The claim needs evidence.
    position posizione What's your position on this issue?
    topic argomento Let's change the topic.
    issue questione / problema This is a complex issue.
    point of view punto di vista From my point of view, it works.

    Useful combinations

    • express an opinion
    • agree with someone
    • disagree with a point
    • support an argument
    • make a claim
    • give an example
    • raise an issue
    • discuss a topic
    • change your mind
    • see someone's point
    • make a distinction
    • compare two ideas
    • reach an agreement
    • solve a disagreement
    • challenge an assumption

    Typical sentence patterns

    • In my opinion, this is the best option.
    • I agree with you up to a point.
    • I can see your point, but I have a different view.
    • One advantage is that it's cheaper.
    • The main issue is that we don't have enough time.
    • That's a good point, but I'm not fully convinced.

    Common mistakes for Italian speakers

    • I am agree → I agree
    • I am not agree → I don't agree
    • According to me → In my opinion / From my point of view
    • I think differently to you → I think differently / I have a different opinion
    • I am contrary → I disagree / I'm against it
    • We discuss about this → We discuss this / talk about this

    Mini examples

    • From my point of view, the plan is realistic.
    • I agree with the general idea, but not with the timing.

    Professional upgrade

    More advanced discussion vocabulary: - stance - assumption - implication - counterargument - rebuttal - trade-off - nuance - framework - perspective shift - strong claim - weak point - evidence-based argument - balanced view - line of reasoning

    Higher-value expressions

    • There's a trade-off between speed and quality.

    Nuance and register

    • I'm not sure I agree = softer
    • I see where you're coming from, but... = natural, diplomatic
    • That may be true, however... = more formal / structured
    • I'm not convinced that... = analytical, controlled

    Chunk 2 Review — From Functional Vocabulary to Social and Professional Use

    This chunk is designed to help learners move from general communication to functional, purposeful English.
    At this stage, vocabulary growth is not just about learning more words; it is about learning: - which words naturally go together - how different situations require different tones - how the same idea can sound casual, neutral, or professional - how to avoid literal translations from Italian

    Core learning goals

    By working through this chunk, the learner should become more confident in: - talking about study and work - describing roles and responsibilities - managing meetings, tasks, and digital tools - writing clearer emails and messages - expressing opinions and disagreeing diplomatically - discussing problems, decisions, and solutions - talking about media, society, and current issues - handling social interaction more naturally

    High-value vocabulary habits

    To make this chunk truly useful, the learner should: 1. study words in chunks, not in isolation 2. review collocations regularly 3. rewrite simple sentences in more natural English 4. note down recurring false friends 5. practise moving from basic to professional versions of the same message

    Professional upgrade checklist

    At the higher end of this chunk, learners should start noticing: - issue vs problem - role vs job - feedback vs correction - follow up vs simple ask again - current vs false-friend actual - raise awareness vs literal translations - meet a deadline vs respect a deadline - gain experience vs make experience

    Final upgrade challenge

    Take one simple sentence from each area and upgrade it: - study - work - project communication - email writing - discussion - social behaviour

    Example:
    Basic: I work in an office and send emails.
    Professional: I work in an office-based role and regularly communicate with clients by email.
    More advanced: My role is mainly office-based and involves frequent written communication with clients and internal teams.
