    Unità 47 – Collocations (Advanced Natural English)

    🎯 Lesson Goals

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • trattare le collocations come unità di significato e non come semplici parole accostate
    • distinguere tra inglese corretto e inglese idiomaticamente naturale
    • riconoscere e usare collocations ad alta frequenza in contesti professionali, accademici e argomentativi
    • evitare translation traps tipici degli italofoni, soprattutto nei pattern verb + noun, adjective + noun, adverb + adjective e verb + preposition
    • migliorare la qualità del tuo output orale e scritto attraverso lexical chunking e retrieval by pattern
    • costruire frasi più credibili, dense e fluide, coerenti con un vero livello C1

    Questa unità non riguarda il “vocabolario avanzato” nel senso tradizionale. Riguarda il fatto che l’inglese naturale si costruisce per combinazioni preferenziali, non per parole isolate.

    🧠 Beyond vocabulary: why collocations are a C1 marker

    Molti studenti pensano che il salto verso C1 dipenda dall’imparare parole più difficili. In realtà, una parte decisiva del salto riguarda come le parole si combinano.

    Confronta:

    • The company did progress after the new strategy.
    • The company made significant progress after the new strategy.

    La prima frase è comprensibile. La seconda, però, mostra:

    • maggiore naturalezza
    • migliore controllo lessicale
    • maggiore probabilità di essere prodotta da un parlante competente

    Le collocations sono quindi un indicatore forte di:

    1. fluency
    2. processing speed
    3. idiomaticity
    4. register awareness
    5. writer/speaker credibility

    A livello C1 non basta “sapere una parola”. Devi sapere:

    • con quali verbi compare
    • con quali aggettivi compare
    • in quali registri è tipica
    • quali combinazioni suonano neutre, forti, formali o colloquiali

    🧩 A practical taxonomy of collocations

    1. Verb + noun

    Questa è una delle famiglie più importanti perché influenza moltissimo la naturalezza percepita.

    High-frequency academic / professional patterns

    • reach a conclusion
    • draw attention to
    • meet expectations
    • raise awareness
    • address an issue
    • pose a threat
    • play a role
    • gain insight
    • take into account
    • launch an initiative

    Compare

    • We need to address the issue immediately.
    • The report draws attention to several structural weaknesses.
    • The results did not meet expectations.
    • The campaign helped raise awareness.

    2. Adjective + noun

    Qui la differenza tra “corretto” e “naturale” è spesso sottilissima, ma molto visibile.

    • strong argument
    • significant improvement
    • substantial evidence
    • key factor
    • major concern
    • serious risk
    • heavy workload
    • mutual understanding
    • widespread belief
    • growing pressure

    Compare

    • a strong argument
    • substantial evidence
    • a major concern

    Molti studenti direbbero big concern o important evidence. Non sempre è grammaticalmente impossibile, ma spesso non è la scelta più naturale.

    3. Adverb + adjective / participle

    Questi pattern sono fondamentali per il parlato e lo scritto maturi.

    • highly effective
    • widely accepted
    • deeply concerned
    • strongly opposed
    • closely linked
    • entirely reasonable
    • perfectly understandable
    • largely based on
    • deeply rooted
    • firmly established

    Compare

    • This approach is highly effective.
    • The two issues are closely linked.
    • Their concerns are perfectly understandable.

    4. Verb + preposition / particle patterns

    Qui l’errore tipico non è solo scegliere il verbo sbagliato, ma rompere un pattern fissato dall’uso.

    • depend on
    • result in
    • lead to
    • focus on
    • cope with
    • contribute to
    • stem from
    • engage in
    • deal with
    • account for

    Compare

    • Several factors contributed to the decline.
    • The delay resulted in further complications.
    • The problem appears to stem from poor planning.

    ⚠️ Translation traps for Italian speakers

    Molti errori collocazionali nascono perché l’italiano usa accoppiamenti diversi.

    Trap Natural English
    make a photo take a photo
    do a decision make a decision
    do progress make progress
    say an opinion express an opinion
    do experience gain experience
    make a question ask a question
    strong rain heavy rain
    high possibility strong likelihood / high probability
    big difference significant / major difference
    arrive to a conclusion reach / draw a conclusion

    Il problema non è solo “ricordare la forma giusta”. Il problema è allenare il cervello a recuperare il chunk corretto invece di tradurre parola per parola.

    🧠 Collocations and register

    Le collocations cambiano anche in base al contesto.

    More neutral / everyday

    • make a choice
    • have a problem
    • give advice
    • take a break

    More formal / academic / professional

    • exercise judgment
    • encounter difficulties
    • provide guidance
    • conduct an analysis

    Compare

    • We had a problem with the schedule.
    • We encountered scheduling difficulties during implementation.

    Entrambe sono corrette. Ma non appartengono allo stesso registro.

    A livello C1 devi poter scegliere in modo consapevole.

    🔬 Collocation depth: not just one correct answer

    Una stessa idea può essere espressa con collocations diverse a seconda di intensità, formalità e focus.

    Example: “there was criticism”

    • There was some criticism.
    • The decision attracted heavy criticism.
    • The proposal came under considerable criticism.
    • The measure was met with widespread criticism.

    Example: “improve”

    • make some progress
    • make steady progress
    • make significant progress
    • show marked improvement

    Questo è un punto davvero C1: non esiste solo “la risposta corretta”, ma una gamma di opzioni con effetti diversi.

    🛠️ Upgrade lab: from acceptable to convincing

    1. Basic

    We did a big effort and arrived to a good result.

    Better

    We made a major effort and achieved a good result.

    Stronger C1 version

    We made a substantial effort and achieved a highly positive outcome.

    2. Basic

    The meeting had many problems and created confusion.

    Better

    The meeting involved several problems and caused confusion.

    Stronger C1 version

    The meeting was marked by several issues and led to considerable confusion.

    3. Basic

    This report says important things about the situation.

    Better

    This report highlights important aspects of the situation.

    Stronger C1 version

    This report draws attention to several key aspects of the situation.

    📘 Reading analysis

    Leggi il testo e individua le collocations.

    The investigation drew attention to a number of structural weaknesses that had previously gone unnoticed. Although the organisation had made some progress, it had failed to address several key issues. As a result, growing pressure from stakeholders led to a strategic review. The final report raised serious concerns but also offered practical recommendations that could help restore public confidence.

    Individua nel testo:

    1. una collocation con attention
    2. una collocation con key
    3. una collocation con pressure
    4. una collocation con raise
    5. una collocation con restore

    Reflection

    Quali di queste useresti in:

    • una presentazione professionale?
    • un essay?
    • una riunione?

    🗣️ Speaking drills

    Drill 1 – Personal experience

    Parla per 90 secondi su una situazione in cui hai:

    • made progress
    • faced a challenge
    • took responsibility
    • reached a decision

    Drill 2 – Work / study discussion

    Rispondi a queste domande usando collocations naturali:

    1. What factors contribute to successful teamwork?
    2. How can a company build public trust?
    3. What usually leads to poor communication in teams?
    4. Why do some projects fail to meet expectations?

    Drill 3 – Opinion and argument

    Sostieni questa tesi per 2 minuti:

    Strong communication skills play a key role in career development.

    Usa almeno 6 collocations della lezione.

    ✍️ Writing tasks

    Task 1 – Transformation

    Riscrivi le frasi usando una collocation più naturale.

    1. The company did progress after the change.
    2. She said her opinion very clearly.
    3. The article gave attention to a serious issue.
    4. We arrived to a conclusion after the discussion.
    5. The reform created a big criticism.

    Task 2 – Controlled writing

    Scrivi un testo di 170–220 parole su uno dei temi seguenti:

    • a difficult decision with long-term consequences
    • a project that failed to meet expectations
    • a problem that required immediate action
    • an issue that deserves greater public awareness

    Vincoli:

    • usa almeno 10 collocations della lezione
    • includi almeno 3 adjective + noun patterns
    • includi almeno 2 verb + preposition patterns
    • evita formulazioni da traduzione letterale

    🧪 Error clinic

    Correggi gli errori.

    1. We did an important decision last month.
    2. The campaign made awareness about the issue.
    3. The plan received strong criticism because it didn’t meet the expectations.
    4. This problem depends from poor communication.
    5. The report says attention to several important points.
    6. She made a research before taking the decision.
    7. The pressure became always more strong.
    8. We need to do progress quickly.

    ✅ Self-assessment

    Controlla se sai:

    • distinguere chiaramente tra parola corretta e combinazione naturale
    • riconoscere collocations tipiche di contesto professionale e accademico
    • evitare almeno 10 translation traps frequenti
    • migliorare una frase “accettabile” rendendola più idiomatica
    • usare collocations in modo spontaneo nel parlato e nello scritto
    • scegliere collocations coerenti con il registro

    Se riesci a farlo con continuità, questa unità ti sta spostando davvero verso un inglese di livello C1.
