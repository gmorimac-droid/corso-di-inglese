    Unità 44 – Passive Voice (Advanced & Natural Use)

    🎯 Lesson Goals

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • usare il passive voice come scelta di focus, stance e register, non come semplice trasformazione grammaticale
    • distinguere quando il passive rende il testo più efficace, più neutro, più istituzionale, più diplomatico o più impersonale
    • controllare diverse strutture avanzate: perfect passive, continuous passive, modal passive, impersonal passive, get passive e costruzioni con reporting verbs
    • riconoscere quando il passive aiuta a gestire la responsabilità, la distanza, l’evidenza o la prominenza informativa
    • evitare passive artificiosi, gonfi o poco idiomatici che spesso derivano da una falsa idea di “inglese avanzato”
    • produrre speaking e writing più maturi, con maggiore controllo tra directness, neutrality, diplomacy e formality

    Questa unità non serve a “fare il passive meglio”. Serve a capire what the passive does to discourse.

    🧠 Beyond transformation: passive as discourse management

    Molti studenti associano il passive a un esercizio scolastico:

    • They approved the proposal.
    • The proposal was approved.

    Ma a livello C1, la vera domanda è:

    • Why is the agent being backgrounded?
    • What is being foregrounded instead?
    • Does the passive sound objective, evasive, formal, or appropriately impersonal?
    • Would an active sentence be clearer, stronger, or more accountable?

    Il passive, infatti, modifica almeno quattro dimensioni del discorso:

    1. information structure → cosa va in posizione iniziale
    2. agency visibility → quanto è chiaro chi agisce
    3. register → quanto il testo suona formale, tecnico o burocratico
    4. stance → se il parlante si avvicina, si distanzia o si protegge pragmaticamente

    ⚖️ Active vs Passive: same facts, different discourse effect

    Confronta:

    • We made a mistake in the final version.
    • A mistake was made in the final version.

    La seconda frase:

    • riduce la visibilità dell’agente
    • suona più distaccata
    • può essere più diplomatica
    • può anche sembrare meno trasparente

    Questo significa che il passive non è mai neutro al 100%: crea sempre un effetto.

    Compare

    • The board rejected the proposal.
    • The proposal was rejected.

    Nel primo caso l’attenzione è sulla responsabilità del board. Nel secondo caso il focus è sull’esito.

    Practical implication

    Un buon utente C1 sa scegliere:

    • active quando servono chiarezza, responsabilità, energia, precisione
    • passive quando servono oggettività, proceduralità, diplomazia, coesione tematica o depersonalizzazione

    🧩 The core grammar, quickly but precisely

    Canonical passive

    be + past participle

    • The system is monitored continuously.
    • The issue was discussed yesterday.
    • The proposal has been revised.
    • The final version will be published soon.

    Modal passive

    modal + be + past participle

    • The matter should be addressed immediately.
    • The figures can be checked later.
    • A final decision must be taken today.

    Perfect passive

    • The project has been completed.
    • The documents had been prepared in advance.

    Continuous passive

    • The website is being updated.
    • The terms were being negotiated when the meeting ended.

    A livello avanzato, il punto non è solo saperle formare. È capire which one best matches the viewpoint you want.

    🧠 Focus management: what goes first matters

    In inglese, la posizione iniziale della frase è molto importante.

    Con il passive puoi mettere in posizione tematica un elemento che vuoi mantenere come topic del discorso.

    Example sequence

    • The report was submitted on Monday.
    • It was then reviewed by the compliance team.
    • Several issues were identified, and a revised version was requested.

    Qui il passive aiuta a mantenere il discorso centrato sul report e sul processo, non sulle persone.

    Questo è molto comune in:

    • report professionali
    • documentazione tecnica
    • presentazioni istituzionali
    • sintesi accademiche

    🧠 When the passive is useful

    1. The agent is unknown, irrelevant, or obvious

    • My bike was stolen last night.
    • The patient was taken to hospital.
    • The building was designed in the 1990s.

    2. The result matters more than the actor

    • The data were analysed using three different models.
    • The contract has been signed.
    • The new policy was introduced in January.

    3. You want a more formal or institutional tone

    • The application has been received and is currently being processed.
    • All requests will be handled within five working days.

    4. You want diplomatic distance

    • Some deadlines were missed.
    • Certain assumptions were made too quickly.

    Qui il passive può attenuare l’impatto accusatorio. Però va usato con attenzione: a volte suona professionale, altre volte evasivo.

    ⚠️ When the passive is a bad choice

    Uno studente avanzato deve anche saper non usare il passive.

    Avoid passive when:

    • l’agente è il vero centro informativo
    • il testo diventa rigido o burocratico
    • la frase perde energia o chiarezza
    • vuoi mostrare responsabilità esplicita

    Compare

    • We made the wrong assumption.
    • The wrong assumption was made.

    La seconda frase è utile in alcuni contesti, ma in altri sembra una fuga dalla responsabilità.

    Better active examples

    • Our team identified the issue quickly.
    • The researchers collected the data over six months.
    • The company changed the policy last year.

    Qui l’active è più vivo, più diretto e spesso più credibile.

    🧠 Impersonal passive and reporting structures

    Questa è una delle aree più interessanti del passive a livello C1.

    Common patterns

    • It is believed that...
    • It is thought that...
    • It is assumed that...
    • It has been reported that...
    • It was claimed that...
    • It is expected that...

    Queste strutture servono a:

    • introdurre informazione senza attribuirla in modo forte
    • creare distanza epistemica
    • mantenere un tono oggettivo o semi-oggettivo
    • evitare commitment troppo forte

    Examples

    • It is believed that the market will recover gradually.
    • It has been suggested that the current model is no longer sustainable.
    • It was later discovered that several files had been deleted.

    Alternative advanced pattern

    • The market is believed to be recovering gradually.
    • Several employees are thought to have left the company.
    • The policy is expected to be revised next quarter.

    Queste strutture sono particolarmente utili in:

    • academic summaries
    • formal presentations
    • analytical writing
    • news-style reporting

    🧩 Get passive: not just informal, but experiential

    Molti manuali dicono solo:

    • be passive = formale
    • get passive = informale

    È vero solo in parte.

    Il get passive spesso aggiunge:

    • maggiore senso di evento
    • coinvolgimento del soggetto
    • idea di cambiamento o impatto vissuto
    • sfumatura più colloquiale o narrativa

    Compare

    • He was fired last week. → neutro, reportivo

    • He got fired last week. → più narrativo, più personale

    • She was promoted in June. → neutro/professionale

    • She got promoted in June. → più colloquiale, più vicino all’esperienza della persona

    Typical get-passive examples

    • get hired
    • get promoted
    • get paid
    • get hurt
    • get stuck
    • get invited
    • get caught

    A livello C1 è utile saper distinguere non solo la grammatica, ma il tone profile di queste scelte.

    🧠 Passive, adjectives, and resultative states

    Una zona di confine importante è la differenza tra:

    • true passive
    • resultative adjective / state

    Compare

    • The door was closed by security at 10 p.m. → evento, passive chiaro

    • The door is closed. → stato risultante / descrizione

    • The window was broken during the storm. → evento

    • The window is broken. → stato attuale

    Questo conta molto nella comprensione e nella precisione descrittiva.

    ⚠️ Advanced learner problems

    1. Overpassivisation

    Alcuni studenti usano il passive troppo spesso per “suonare avanzati”. Il risultato può essere innaturale.

    ❌ Important decisions were taken by us in order to improve the strategy.

    Meglio:

    ✅ We made several important decisions to improve the strategy.

    2. Unnecessary by-phrases

    ❌ The report was written by the manager

    Se il manager è già noto o irrilevante, il complemento d’agente è superfluo.

    3. Wrong register assumptions

    Il passive non rende automaticamente una frase elegante. A volte la rende solo più pesante.

    4. Confusion with intransitive verbs

    Non tutti i verbi possono andare al passive.

    ❌ He was arrived late. ✅ He arrived late.

    📘 Analysis Task

    Leggi il testo e osserva cosa viene foregrounded e cosa viene backgrounded.

    The final report was submitted on Friday and was circulated among senior staff later that day. Several concerns were raised during the review process, and a revised version is expected to be produced by the end of the month. It has also been suggested that additional data should be collected before any final decision is made.

    Analysis questions

    1. Why is passive preferred in this text?
    2. Which agents are hidden or backgrounded?
    3. Which sentence sounds most institutional?
    4. Where would an active version be more effective?
    5. What effect does It has also been suggested that... create?

    🗣️ Speaking Practice

    Task 1 – Process explanation

    Spiega oralmente uno di questi processi usando il passive quando è naturale:

    • how a proposal is approved
    • how a product is tested
    • how complaints are handled
    • how university applications are processed

    Task 2 – Diplomatic reporting

    Riformula queste frasi in modo più istituzionale o diplomatico:

    • We made a few mistakes.
    • The team missed two deadlines.
    • They changed the schedule at the last minute.
    • Someone leaked the information.

    Task 3 – Active or passive?

    Per ogni caso, spiega quale forma sceglieresti e perché:

    • formal report
    • apology email
    • news summary
    • meeting recap
    • storytelling in conversation

    ✍️ Writing Practice

    Scrivi 180–220 parole scegliendo uno dei seguenti compiti.

    Option A – Formal process description

    Describe how an internal review process is carried out in a company or institution.

    Option B – Analytical summary

    Write a short formal summary of a project in which the focus is on outcomes, actions taken, and decisions made.

    Option C – Style comparison

    Write two short versions of the same event:

    1. one mostly in the active voice
    2. one using the passive voice strategically

    Poi spiega brevemente quale versione suona più adatta a un professional context e perché.

    Writing checklist

    Assicurati di includere:

    • almeno 6 passive structures
    • almeno 3 tempi diversi nel passive
    • almeno 1 impersonal passive
    • almeno 1 modal passive
    • almeno 1 scelta consapevole tra active e passive spiegabile in termini di focus o register

    🧪 Error Clinic

    Correggi o migliora queste frasi.

    1. The company was deciding to reduce costs.
    2. The report has finished yesterday.
    3. Important changes were made by the management, and they were announced by them later.
    4. He was arrived late, so the meeting was delayed.
    5. The product got developed in our lab.

    Possibili correzioni

    1. The company decided to reduce costs.
    2. The report was finished yesterday. / The report has been completed.
    3. Important changes were made by management and announced later. oppure una versione attiva più chiara
    4. He arrived late, so the meeting was delayed.
    5. The product was developed in our lab.

    ✅ Final Self-check

    Verifica se sai davvero fare queste cose:

    • scegliere tra active e passive per ragioni di focus e non solo di grammatica
    • usare il passive in tempi semplici, perfetti, continui e con modali
    • riconoscere quando il passive aumenta neutrality, diplomacy, formality o distance
    • usare con naturalezza strutture come It is believed that... e X is thought to...
    • distinguere be passive, get passive e resultative state
    • evitare passive rigidi, sovraccarichi o poco idiomatici

    Se riesci a fare tutto questo con buona naturalezza, stai usando il passive in modo molto vicino a un vero livello C1.
