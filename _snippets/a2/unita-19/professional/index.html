    Professional

    Modalità Professional attiva. In questa unità lavori sul Present Perfect of experience in modo molto più preciso e naturale. Non ti limiti a dire yes / no: impari a chiedere, approfondire, raccontare esperienze, collegare il Present Perfect al Past Simple, usare ever / never / before, scegliere i participi corretti e sostenere mini-conversazioni autentiche.

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • usare il Present Perfect per parlare di esperienze di vita con sicurezza
    • costruire domande naturali con Have you ever...?
    • rispondere in modo più ricco con dettagli, follow-up e reazioni
    • distinguere bene experience e finished past event
    • usare in modo corretto been, gone, ever, never, before e i participi irregolari più frequenti

    🧭 Language focus – Present Perfect for life experience

    1. What the tense really does

    Quando usiamo il Present Perfect per le esperienze, non stiamo raccontando una storia in ordine cronologico. Stiamo dicendo che una certa esperienza fa parte della nostra vita fino ad ora.

    • I've been to Berlin.
    • She's tried Turkish food.
    • Have you ever spoken English with native speakers?

    Il punto centrale non è quando, ma se è successo almeno una volta nella tua vita.

    👉 Per questo il Present Perfect risponde bene a domande come:

    • Have you ever...?
    • Have you tried...?
    • Have you been to...?
    • Have you done that before?

    2. The basic structure

    Affermativa

    • I have visited Dublin.
    • She has travelled alone.
    • We have tried that restaurant.

    Negativa

    • I haven't been there.
    • He hasn't tried sushi.
    • They haven't travelled abroad.

    Interrogativa

    • Have you ever visited Dublin?
    • Has she ever flown business class?
    • Have they tried Moroccan food?

    Short answers

    • Yes, I have.
    • No, I haven't.
    • Yes, she has.
    • No, they haven't.

    3. Ever, never, before

    Ever

    Usiamo ever soprattutto nelle domande:

    • Have you ever been to Greece?
    • Have you ever eaten snails?
    • Have you ever done anything like this?

    Può comparire anche in frasi come:

    • It's the best film I've ever seen.

    Never

    Usiamo never per dire “mai nella vita fino ad ora”.

    • I've never tried horse riding.
    • She's never flown alone.
    • We've never stayed in a five-star hotel.

    Before

    Before è utile quando vogliamo dire che qualcosa è già successo prima di questo momento.

    • I've seen this film before.
    • Have you met him before?
    • We haven't been here before.

    👉 never è più assoluto; before dice semplicemente che l'esperienza è o non è già successa in precedenza.

    🧠 Been vs gone

    Questa distinzione merita attenzione.

    Have been to

    Significa: la persona è stata in un posto e poi è tornata.

    • I've been to Madrid twice.
    • Have you ever been to Amsterdam?
    • She's been to Japan, but only once.

    Have gone to

    Significa: la persona è andata lì e, nel momento in cui parliamo, è ancora là oppure è in viaggio.

    • Tom has gone to the bank. (= non è qui adesso)
    • My parents have gone to Sicily for the weekend.

    👉 Per parlare di esperienze di vita, nella maggior parte dei casi userai have been to, non have gone to.

    🔄 Present Perfect vs Past Simple – the real contrast

    Present Perfect: experience without time reference

    • Have you ever visited London?
    • I've tried Korean food.
    • She's never seen snow.

    Past Simple: specific finished moment

    • When did you visit London?
    • I tried Korean food last month.
    • She saw snow when she was a child.

    👉 Nelle conversazioni reali spesso succede questo:

    1. prima chiedi l'esperienza con Present Perfect
    2. poi chiedi il dettaglio con Past Simple

    Esempio naturale:

    • Have you ever been to Ireland?
    • Yes, I have.
    • Really? When did you go?
    • I went there in 2023.
    • Did you like it?
    • Yes, I loved it.

    Questa sequenza è fondamentale.

    🧱 High-frequency past participles

    Ecco i participi che servono davvero in questa unità:

    Base form Past participle
    be been
    go gone
    see seen
    eat eaten
    do done
    try tried
    visit visited
    travel travelled / traveled
    fly flown
    ride ridden
    speak spoken
    write written
    take taken
    make made
    meet met

    👉 Per chi studia dall'italiano, gli errori più frequenti sono:

    • I have went
    • I have saw
    • I have wrote
    • Have you ever did...?

    📚 Vocabulary expansion – experiences worth talking about

    Travel and movement

    • travel abroad
    • go on a road trip
    • miss a flight
    • stay in a hostel
    • take a night train
    • visit a capital city
    • cross a border
    • get lost in a new city

    Food and culture

    • try street food
    • eat something spicy
    • cook a foreign dish
    • go to a food market
    • visit a museum
    • watch a show in English
    • attend a concert
    • see a famous landmark

    Personal development

    • speak in public
    • take an exam in English
    • make a speech
    • work with international clients
    • study abroad
    • move to another city
    • meet someone from another culture

    Useful follow-up expressions:

    • once / twice / several times
    • a few years ago
    • when I was younger
    • the first time
    • actually
    • to be honest
    • not yet

    🎧 Reading – natural conversation model

    Dialogue 1 – from experience to detail

    Emma: Have you ever travelled on your own?
    Leo: Yes, I have. I went to Lisbon by myself two years ago.
    Emma: Really? Were you nervous?
    Leo: A little, yes, but it was a great experience.
    Emma: Have you ever stayed in a hostel?
    Leo: No, I haven't. I've always stayed in small hotels.

    Dialogue 2 – food and reactions

    Marta: Have you ever tried Ethiopian food?
    Chris: No, I haven't, but I've heard it's excellent.
    Marta: I've had it twice. It's really good.
    Chris: Where did you try it?
    Marta: In London, last autumn.

    Dialogue 3 – work and study

    A: Have you ever had to use English at work?
    B: Yes, I have. I've spoken to clients from Germany and the Netherlands.
    A: Was it difficult at first?
    B: Yes, it was, but it got easier with practice.

    🗣️ Speaking drills

    Drill 1 – question formation

    Transform the prompts into full questions:

    • you / ever / be / abroad
    • you / ever / fly / alone
    • you / ever / try / Indian food
    • you / ever / speak / English with a stranger
    • you / ever / lose / your passport

    Drill 2 – short answer + extension

    Answer using this pattern:

    • Yes, I have. / No, I haven't. + one extra detail

    Examples:

    • Have you ever travelled by plane?

    • Yes, I have. I've flown to Barcelona twice.

    • Have you ever tried surfing?

    • No, I haven't, but I'd like to.

    Drill 3 – experience interview

    Ask and answer at least 8 questions about:

    • travel
    • food
    • films
    • English
    • work
    • sport
    • unusual experiences
    • future wishes connected to experiences

    ✍️ Writing

    Task 1 – Experience profile

    Scrivi un testo di 120–150 parole su di te.

    Devi includere:

    • 4 esperienze che hai già fatto
    • 3 cose che non hai mai fatto
    • 2 dettagli al Past Simple su una delle esperienze
    • almeno 5 participi passati diversi

    Possible opening:

    I have had several interesting experiences in my life. I have travelled abroad, tried different kinds of food and spoken English with foreign visitors. However, there are still many things I haven't done yet...

    Task 2 – Interview report

    Immagina di aver intervistato un amico con domande Have you ever...?. Scrivi un report di 90–120 parole.

    Model:

    Giulia has travelled abroad several times, but she has never flown outside Europe. She has tried Japanese food and she has been to Spain, but she hasn't visited Portugal yet...

    🔊 Pronunciation and connected speech

    Weak forms and rhythm

    Nella lingua parlata, have spesso non è forte.

    • Have you ever...? → suona spesso come /həv juː ˈevə/
    • I've never... → ritmo compatto e veloce

    Sound focus

    • been → /biːn/ o /bɪn/
    • ever → stress sulla prima sillaba
    • written / ridden / eaten / spoken → attenzione alla sillaba accentata e alla consonante finale

    Repeat in chunks:

    • Have you ever been there?
    • I've never done that before.
    • Have you ever spoken English at work?
    • It's the best meal I've ever had.

    ⚠️ Error clinic – typical Italian mistakes

    ❌ Have you ever went to London?
    ✅ Have you ever been to London? / Have you ever gone to London? (grammatical, ma meno naturale per esperienza)

    ❌ I have saw that film.
    ✅ I have seen that film.

    ❌ I have wrote an email in English.
    ✅ I have written an email in English.

    ❌ I am never been to Berlin.
    ✅ I have never been to Berlin.

    ❌ Did you ever try sushi?
    ✅ Have you ever tried sushi?

    ❌ I have been in Paris.
    ✅ I have been to Paris.

    ❌ I have gone to Rome last year.
    ✅ I went to Rome last year.

    👉 Regola d'oro:

    • Present Perfect → esperienza / collegamento con il presente
    • Past Simple → momento finito e specifico nel passato

    ✅ Review & Practice

    A. Complete with the correct participle

    1. Have you ever ___ (ride) a horse?
    2. I have never ___ (write) a formal email in English.
    3. She has ___ (meet) several international clients.
    4. We have ___ (take) a night train.
    5. Have they ever ___ (fly) abroad?

    B. Choose the best option

    1. Have you ever been / went to Scotland?
    2. I have never seen / saw snow.
    3. We went / have gone there in 2022.
    4. She has tried / tried sushi last week.
    5. Have you ever did / done anything like that?

    C. Correct the mistakes

    1. I have went to Spain.
    2. Have you ever saw this film?
    3. I have never be there.
    4. She has gone to Paris last year.
    5. Did you ever travelled alone?

    D. Build a mini-conversation

    Start with Present Perfect, continue with Past Simple:

    • Have you ever ______?
    • Yes, I have. / No, I haven't.
    • When did you ______?
    • I __ in ____.
    • Did you enjoy it?
    • Yes, I did / No, I didn't.

    🧠 Think, react, extend

    Answer these naturally, not with one-word replies:

    • Have you ever done something that scared you?
    • Have you ever eaten something you didn't like at all?
    • Have you ever had to speak English unexpectedly?
    • Have you ever missed a train or a flight?
    • Have you ever changed your opinion after travelling somewhere?

    Useful reaction language:

    • Actually, yes, I have.
    • Not yet, but I'd love to.
    • No, never.
    • Yes, once, and it was...
    • Yes, a couple of times.
    • No, but I'm curious to try.

    🚀 Final task – interview + story

    Part 1

    Crea una mini intervista di 10 domande con Have you ever...?

    Part 2

    Scegli 3 risposte positive e trasformale in una breve storia con dettagli al Past Simple.

    Example:

    Have you ever travelled alone? Yes, I have. I went to Lisbon in 2024. I stayed in a small hotel near the centre and I met people from different countries. It was challenging at first, but I really enjoyed it.

    🎯 Self-assessment

    Sai:

    • usare Have you ever...? con buona fluidità
    • scegliere il participio corretto dei verbi più frequenti
    • passare da Present Perfect a Past Simple per chiedere dettagli
    • evitare gli errori classici su been / gone, ever / never, gone / went
    • sostenere una breve conversazione autentica sulle esperienze

    Se sì, sei pronto per la review finale di A2.

    Interesting conversations often begin with real experiences.
