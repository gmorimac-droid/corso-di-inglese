    Professional

    Modalità Professional attiva. In questa unità lavori sul futuro in modo più realistico e preciso. Non ti limiti a distinguere tra will e be going to: impari a parlare di intenzioni, decisioni spontanee, previsioni basate su evidenze, promesse, offerte e piani già organizzati, con lessico naturale e maggiore controllo dell'uso reale.

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • usare be going to per piani, intenzioni e previsioni con evidenza presente
    • usare will per decisioni spontanee, offerte, promesse e previsioni generali
    • fare domande e frasi negative con buona sicurezza e naturalezza
    • distinguere casi in cui entrambe le forme sono possibili ma con sfumature diverse
    • parlare dei tuoi progetti futuri in modo più fluido, preciso e realistico

    🧭 Language focus – talking about the future naturally

    1. Be going to: piani, intenzioni, segnali evidenti

    Usiamo be going to quando:

    • abbiamo già un'intenzione o un piano
    • parliamo di qualcosa che abbiamo deciso prima del momento in cui parliamo
    • facciamo una previsione basata su qualcosa che vediamo o sappiamo già

    Esempi:

    • I'm going to study after dinner.
    • We're going to move to a bigger flat next year.
    • She's going to apply for a new job.
    • Look at those clouds. It's going to rain.

    👉 Qui la previsione non è “solo un'opinione”: c'è un segnale concreto.

    2. Will: decisione del momento, offerta, promessa, previsione generale

    Usiamo will quando:

    • decidiamo qualcosa mentre stiamo parlando
    • facciamo un'offerta
    • promettiamo qualcosa
    • esprimiamo una previsione, spesso come opinione o valutazione personale

    Esempi:

    • The phone is ringing. I'll answer it.
    • I'll help you with your bags.
    • I won't tell anyone.
    • I think our team will win.
    • I'm sure you'll enjoy the course.

    👉 will suona molto naturale con espressioni come:

    • I think...
    • I guess...
    • probably
    • maybe
    • I'm sure...

    3. The core contrast: prior plan vs decision now

    Confronta:

    • I'm going to cook tonight. → l'ho già deciso

    • OK, I'll cook tonight. → lo decido adesso

    • We're going to visit Laura on Sunday. → visita già programmata

    • Fine, we'll visit Laura on Sunday. → decisione presa nel momento della conversazione

    Questa differenza è centrale. In tanti casi entrambe le forme sono grammaticalmente possibili, ma il significato cambia.

    4. Predictions: opinion vs visible evidence

    Will for opinion / personal prediction

    • I think it will be a good year.
    • She'll probably pass the exam.
    • I don't think they'll arrive on time.

    Going to for visible evidence / strong signs

    • Look at the traffic. We're going to be late.
    • He's driving too fast. He's going to have an accident.
    • The glass is full. It's going to fall.

    👉 Regola utile:

    • will = previsione mentale / opinione
    • going to = previsione con base osservabile o già evidente

    🧠 Forms and structure

    Be going to

    Affermativa

    • I am going to leave early.
    • She is going to start a new project.
    • They are going to travel in June.

    Negativa

    • I'm not going to stay long.
    • He isn't going to buy it.
    • We aren't going to change the date.

    Interrogativa

    • Are you going to call her?
    • Is she going to join us?
    • Are they going to book the hotel?

    Will

    Affermativa

    • I will call you later.
    • She will help us.
    • They will arrive soon.

    Negativa

    • I won't forget.
    • He won't agree.
    • We won't be late.

    Interrogativa

    • Will you send me the file?
    • Will she be there?
    • Will they understand?

    📚 Vocabulary expansion – future plans and decisions

    Planning and organisation

    • make a plan
    • arrange a meeting
    • book a hotel
    • buy a ticket
    • cancel a trip
    • change the date
    • start a course
    • move house
    • apply for a job
    • save money

    Promises and offers

    • help you
    • call you back
    • send the document
    • wait here
    • carry your bag
    • explain everything
    • check it later
    • remind you tomorrow

    Future time expressions

    • tomorrow morning
    • tomorrow evening
    • later today
    • next weekend
    • next month
    • in a few days
    • in the summer
    • soon
    • one day
    • in the future

    🎧 Reading – future in real contexts

    Text 1 – plans and intentions

    I'm going to have a busy weekend. On Saturday morning I'm going to clean the house and then I'm going to meet a friend for lunch. In the afternoon we're going to look at a flat because we may move later this year. On Sunday I'm going to visit my parents. I already told them, so it's a real plan, not just an idea.

    Text 2 – spontaneous decisions and predictions

    The weather looks terrible. It's going to rain soon, so I'm going to take an umbrella. I think the buses will be late because traffic is already heavy. If that happens, I'll call the office and I'll tell them I'm running late. Don't worry, everything will be fine.

    🔍 Notice the language

    Osserva queste scelte:

    • I'm going to have a busy weekend → intenzione / piano
    • we're going to look at a flat → attività organizzata
    • It's going to rain → previsione con evidenza
    • I think the buses will be late → opinione personale
    • I'll call the office → decisione del momento / reazione
    • everything will be fine → previsione rassicurante / promessa implicita

    🗣️ Speaking drills

    Drill 1 – Choose the best form

    Scegli tra will e going to e spiega perché.

    1. I think she ___ enjoy the film.
    2. Look at those black clouds. It ___ rain.
    3. We ___ visit our cousins next weekend. We already bought the train tickets.
    4. The phone is ringing. I ___ answer it.
    5. Don't worry. I ___ help you with the report.

    Drill 2 – Rephrase with a different meaning

    Confronta le due versioni e spiega la differenza.

    • I'm going to cook dinner tonight.

    • I'll cook dinner tonight.

    • We're going to leave early tomorrow.

    • We'll leave early tomorrow.

    • She's going to call him later.

    • She'll call him later.

    👉 L'obiettivo non è solo “scegliere il tempo giusto”, ma capire la prospettiva del parlante.

    Drill 3 – Short role-plays

    Role-play A – at home

    A: We don't have any bread for breakfast.
    B: OK, I'll go to the bakery now.

    Role-play B – planning the weekend

    A: What are you going to do on Saturday?
    B: I'm going to visit my grandparents and then I'm going to meet some friends.

    Role-play C – prediction

    A: Do you think the exam will be difficult?
    B: I think it will be challenging, but manageable.

    Role-play D – office situation

    A: The client just emailed us.
    B: I'll reply now.
    A: Good. I'm going to prepare the updated file.

    ✍️ Writing

    Task 1 – Plans, decisions, predictions

    Scrivi un testo di 120–150 parole intitolato:

    My plans for the next few weeks

    Nel testo prova a includere:

    • almeno 4 frasi con going to
    • almeno 4 frasi con will
    • una previsione personale con I think... will...
    • una previsione con evidenza con going to
    • almeno 5 espressioni di tempo futuro

    Task 2 – Real-life mini scenarios

    Completa queste situazioni con una o due frasi naturali:

    1. Your friend looks tired.
    2. You already bought a ticket for Friday.
    3. Someone asks for help with a heavy bag.
    4. The sky is very dark.
    5. You decide to send an email right now.

    🔊 Pronunciation and spoken English

    Contractions

    Nel parlato quotidiano le contrazioni sono frequentissime:

    • I will → I'll
    • you will → you'll
    • he will → he'll
    • we will → we'll
    • will not → won't

    Going to in connected speech

    Nel parlato informale going to spesso si riduce a un suono simile a gonna.

    • I'm going to call her. → informally: I'm gonna call her.

    👉 In un corso professionale è utile riconoscerlo quando ascolti, ma nello scritto standard continuiamo a usare going to.

    ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

    ❌ I will to call you later
    ✅ I will call you later

    ❌ I going to buy a new phone
    ✅ I am going to buy a new phone

    ❌ She will goes there tomorrow
    ✅ She will go there tomorrow

    ❌ We are going to going out
    ✅ We are going to go out

    ❌ I think it is going to be fine, because that's my opinion only
    ✅ I think it will be fine

    ❌ Look at the sky! It will rain.
    ✅ Look at the sky! It is going to rain.

    👉 L'ultima coppia non è “sempre sbagliata” in assoluto, ma going to è la scelta più naturale quando la previsione nasce da un segnale evidente.

    🧠 Pattern fondamentali

    • be going to + verbo base → piano, intenzione, previsione con evidenza
    • will + verbo base → decisione spontanea, offerta, promessa, previsione/opinione
    • dopo will non usiamo mai to
    • con going to il verbo be è obbligatorio e cambia con il soggetto

    Mini-schema:

    • I'm going to study.
    • She's going to start a new job.
    • We'll help you.
    • I think they'll arrive soon.
    • Look out! You're going to drop it.

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa con will o be going to nella forma corretta.

    1. I think our team ___ win.
    2. Look at that child! He ___ fall.
    3. We already booked everything. We ___ travel in July.
    4. The doorbell is ringing. I ___ open the door.
    5. Don't worry, I ___ keep your secret.
    6. She bought the ingredients. She ___ cook tonight.
    7. I don't think they ___ agree.
    8. Are you ___ apply for that job?

    🔄 Transformation work

    Trasforma o completa le frasi mantenendo il significato.

    1. I decided yesterday to study more.
      → I ___ study more.

    2. The phone is ringing. I answer it now.
      → I ___ answer it.

    3. There are dark clouds in the sky. Rain is imminent.
      → It ___ rain.

    4. I promise I keep this information private.
      → I ___ keep this information private.

    5. We already arranged the meeting for Monday.
      → We ___ meet on Monday.

    🧠 Think & Speak

    Parla per 90 secondi su questi punti:

    • tre cose che hai già deciso di fare nelle prossime settimane
    • una decisione che prenderesti subito in una situazione di emergenza
    • due previsioni sul tuo lavoro, sui tuoi studi o sulla tua vita personale
    • una promessa che puoi fare a te stesso o a un'altra persona

    Prova a usare sia will sia going to in modo consapevole.

    🚀 Final task

    Speaking or writing task

    Prepara una presentazione breve intitolata:

    My future plans and what I think will happen

    Deve includere:

    • piani già decisi
    • intenzioni personali
    • almeno due decisioni spontanee immaginate
    • almeno due previsioni
    • un'offerta o una promessa

    Obiettivo: parlare o scrivere in modo più naturale, non solo “applicare una regola”.
