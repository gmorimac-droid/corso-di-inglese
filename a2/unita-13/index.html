    Unità 13 – Past Simple

    Modalità Base attiva. Questa unità ti aiuta a usare il Past Simple per parlare di azioni concluse nel passato. Imparerai a raccontare cosa hai fatto ieri o la settimana scorsa, a usare alcuni verbi irregolari molto frequenti e a fare domande semplici sul passato. Per la versione estesa usa il toggle BASE / PRO nella barra in alto.

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • parlare di azioni concluse nel passato
    • usare il Past Simple con verbi regolari e irregolari comuni
    • fare domande e risposte brevi con did
    • raccontare in modo semplice cosa hai fatto ieri o nel weekend

    🧠 Grammar in context

    Forma affermativa

    Per parlare di un'azione finita nel passato usiamo il Past Simple.

    Subject + past form of the verb

    • I worked yesterday.
    • She visited her grandmother.
    • We watched a film.
    • They played football.

    Con i verbi regolari, di solito aggiungiamo -ed:

    • work → worked
    • watch → watched
    • play → played
    • clean → cleaned

    Verbi irregolari comuni

    Alcuni verbi non prendono -ed e cambiano forma:

    • go → went
    • eat → ate
    • drink → drank
    • see → saw
    • have → had
    • do → did
    • buy → bought
    • come → came

    Esempi:

    • I went to work.
    • We had dinner at home.
    • She bought a book.
    • They came late.

    Forma negativa

    Per la forma negativa usiamo did not / didn't + verbo base.

    Subject + didn't + base verb

    • I didn't work yesterday.
    • She didn't go out.
    • We didn't watch TV.

    👉 Dopo didn't il verbo torna alla forma base.

    Forma interrogativa

    Per fare domande usiamo Did + subject + base verb?

    • Did you work yesterday?
    • Did she go to school?
    • Did they eat at home?

    Risposte brevi:

    • Yes, I did.
    • No, I didn't.
    • Yes, she did.
    • No, they didn't.

    📚 Core vocabulary

    Time expressions

    • yesterday
    • yesterday morning
    • last night
    • last week
    • last month
    • last year
    • two days ago
    • an hour ago
    • on Saturday
    • in 2024

    Useful verbs

    • work
    • study
    • play
    • watch
    • visit
    • travel
    • cook
    • clean
    • call
    • start
    • finish
    • go
    • eat
    • drink
    • see
    • have

    🔁 Regular vs irregular

    Confronta:

    • work → worked
    • play → played
    • visit → visited

    ma:

    • go → went
    • eat → ate
    • see → saw

    👉 I verbi irregolari vanno memorizzati poco alla volta, ma sono molto frequenti.

    🎧 Listening / Reading

    Text:

    Yesterday was busy. I woke up at 7:00, had breakfast and went to work. I worked until 5:30. In the evening, I met a friend, ate at a small restaurant and came home at 10:00. I didn't watch TV because I was tired.

    Comprensione

    1. What time did the person wake up?
    2. Where did the person go in the morning?
    3. Who did the person meet in the evening?
    4. Did the person watch TV?

    🗣️ Speaking

    Ripeti:

    • I worked yesterday.
    • I went out last night.
    • She ate at home.
    • We didn't study.
    • Did you call your friend?

    Ora completa oralmente:

    • Yesterday I ___
    • Last weekend I ___
    • I didn't ___ yesterday.
    • Did you ___ last night?

    Mini speaking task:

    Parla per 30–45 secondi su what you did yesterday.

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi 8–10 frasi su quello che hai fatto ieri o lo scorso weekend.

    Puoi includere:

    • quando ti sei svegliato
    • dove sei andato
    • cosa hai mangiato
    • chi hai visto
    • cosa non hai fatto

    Modello utile

    Yesterday I woke up at 7:30. I had coffee and went to work. I worked all morning. In the afternoon I visited my parents. In the evening I didn't go out.

    🔊 Pronunciation

    Attenzione alla pronuncia di -ed:

    • worked → /t/
    • played → /d/
    • wanted → /ɪd/

    👉 -ed non si pronuncia sempre allo stesso modo.

    ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

    ❌ I go yesterday.
    ✅ I went yesterday.

    ❌ I didn't went.
    ✅ I didn't go.

    ❌ Did you went out?
    ✅ Did you go out?

    ❌ I have lunch with my friend yesterday.
    ✅ I had lunch with my friend yesterday.

    👉 Con did / didn't usi il verbo base, non il passato.

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa con il Past Simple corretto.

    1. I ___ (work) yesterday.
    2. She ___ (go) to the supermarket.
    3. We ___ (not watch) TV.
    4. ___ you ___ (study) last night?
    5. They ___ (have) lunch at 1:00.
    6. He ___ (see) a good film.

    🔄 Trasforma

    Trasforma al passato.

    1. I work every day. → ________
    2. She goes to school. → ________
    3. We don't study on Sundays. → ________
    4. Do they eat at home? → ________

    🎯 Mini task

    Parla per 1 minuto e racconta:

    • cosa hai fatto ieri
    • dove sei andato
    • con chi sei stato
    • cosa non hai fatto

    🧠 Self-check

    Riesci a:

    • usare worked / watched / visited correttamente?
    • ricordare alcuni verbi irregolari come went / ate / had / saw?
    • fare domande con Did you...??
    • evitare errori come didn't went?

    Se sì, sei pronto per continuare.

    Modalità Professional attiva. In questa unità non impari solo a “mettere il verbo al passato”: impari a raccontare eventi conclusi in modo più preciso, a gestire il contrasto tra forma affermativa e strutture con did / didn't, e a costruire piccoli racconti credibili con sequenza temporale, dettagli e verbi irregolari ad alta frequenza.

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • usare il Past Simple per raccontare eventi conclusi in ordine logico
    • distinguere con sicurezza tra forma al passato e base verb dopo did / didn't
    • usare un primo nucleo essenziale di verbi irregolari ad alta frequenza
    • fare domande più naturali su esperienze recenti e attività concluse
    • produrre un breve racconto orale o scritto sul tuo ieri, sul tuo weekend o su una giornata speciale

    🧭 Quando usiamo il Past Simple

    Il Past Simple si usa per azioni e situazioni concluse in un momento finito del passato.

    1. Azioni concluse

    • I finished work at 6.
    • She called her sister yesterday.
    • We visited a museum last weekend.

    2. Sequenza di azioni

    • I got up, had breakfast, took the bus and arrived at work.
    • He opened the door, turned on the light and sat down.

    3. Eventi legati a un tempo passato chiaro

    • They moved to Milan in 2022.
    • I saw him two days ago.
    • We met on Saturday.

    👉 Il punto centrale è questo: l'azione è finita e il riferimento temporale è chiuso.

    🧠 Struttura completa

    Forma affermativa

    Subject + past form

    • I worked late.
    • She arrived early.
    • They went home.
    • We had dinner together.

    Forma negativa

    Subject + did not / didn't + base verb

    • I didn't work late.
    • She didn't arrive early.
    • They didn't go home.
    • We didn't have dinner together.

    Forma interrogativa

    Did + subject + base verb?

    • Did you work late?
    • Did she arrive early?
    • Did they go home?
    • Did you have dinner together?

    Risposte brevi

    • Yes, I did.
    • No, I didn't.
    • Yes, she did.
    • No, they didn't.

    👉 La difficoltà maggiore per molti studenti non è l'affermativa, ma ricordare che con did / didn't il verbo torna sempre alla forma base.

    🔁 Il contrasto decisivo: went vs did go?

    Confronta bene:

    • I went out last night. ✅
    • Did you go out last night? ✅
    • I didn't go out last night. ✅

    Ma:

    • Did you went out...? ❌
    • I didn't went out. ❌

    Perché? Perché il passato è già espresso da did. Il verbo dopo resta base:

    • go
    • eat
    • see
    • have
    • buy
    • come

    Questo vale con tutti i verbi, regolari e irregolari.

    📚 Language Bank

    Time markers tipici del Past Simple

    • yesterday
    • yesterday morning / afternoon / evening
    • last night
    • last week
    • last month
    • last year
    • on Monday
    • on Saturday evening
    • two days ago
    • a few minutes ago
    • in 2023

    High-frequency regular verbs

    • work
    • watch
    • play
    • visit
    • clean
    • start
    • finish
    • phone / call
    • help
    • open

    High-frequency irregular verbs

    • go → went
    • come → came
    • have → had
    • do → did
    • eat → ate
    • drink → drank
    • see → saw
    • buy → bought
    • get → got
    • make → made
    • meet → met
    • take → took

    ✍️ Formazione del passato regolare

    La regola di base è aggiungere -ed, ma ci sono alcune variazioni ortografiche utili.

    Aggiunta semplice di -ed

    • work → worked
    • play → played
    • clean → cleaned

    Verbi che finiscono in -e

    • live → lived
    • arrive → arrived
    • like → liked

    Verbi che finiscono in consonante + y

    • study → studied
    • carry → carried
    • try → tried

    Alcuni verbi brevi raddoppiano la consonante

    • stop → stopped
    • plan → planned

    👉 A questo livello ti basta riconoscere i modelli più frequenti e produrre correttamente i verbi ad alta utilità.

    🎧 Reading – A day that went wrong

    Leggi il testo.

    Yesterday started badly. I woke up late because I didn't hear my alarm. I got dressed quickly and left the house without breakfast. At the bus stop I met a colleague, but our bus didn't arrive on time, so we took a taxi. I reached the office at 9:20 and my manager asked, “What happened?” In the evening I planned to go to the gym, but I felt tired and stayed at home instead. I made dinner, watched a series and went to bed early.

    Comprensione

    1. Why did the speaker wake up late?
    2. Did the bus arrive on time?
    3. How did the speaker get to the office?
    4. What did the manager ask?
    5. Did the speaker go to the gym?
    6. Which verbs in the text are irregular?

    Language focus

    Nel testo trovi:

    • sequenza narrativa: woke up, got dressed, left, met, took, reached
    • negativa al passato: didn't hear, didn't arrive
    • cambio di piano: planned to go, but stayed at home instead

    Questo è il tipo di controllo che ti serve per passare da frase isolata a mini-racconto.

    🗣️ Guided Speaking

    A. Controlled drill

    Rispondi oralmente con una frase completa.

    1. What time did you get up yesterday?
    2. What did you have for breakfast?
    3. Did you work or study yesterday?
    4. Who did you talk to?
    5. Did anything unexpected happen?
    6. What did you do in the evening?

    B. Expand with details

    Aggiungi un dettaglio di tempo, luogo o compagnia.

    Esempio:

    • I went to the supermarket.
    • I went to the supermarket after work with my brother.

    C. One-minute story

    Parla per 60–90 secondi su uno di questi temi:

    • My day yesterday
    • My last weekend
    • A day that went wrong

    Struttura consigliata:

    • First, I...
    • Then, I...
    • After that, I...
    • In the evening, I...
    • Finally, I...

    ✍️ Writing

    Task 1 – Guided paragraph

    Scrivi un testo di 120–150 parole dal titolo:

    What I did yesterday

    Devi includere:

    • almeno 4 verbi regolari al Past Simple
    • almeno 4 verbi irregolari al Past Simple
    • almeno 2 forme negative con didn't
    • almeno 1 domanda che qualcuno ti ha fatto o che hai fatto tu

    Task 2 – Better storytelling

    Rivedi il testo e controlla se hai inserito:

    • una sequenza chiara degli eventi
    • connettivi semplici come first, then, after that, later, finally
    • dettagli concreti di luogo, tempo o persone
    • almeno un piccolo problema o cambiamento di piano

    🔊 Pronunciation

    La finale -ed ha tre pronunce principali.

    /t/

    • worked
    • watched
    • finished

    /d/

    • played
    • cleaned
    • called

    /ɪd/

    • wanted
    • needed
    • visited

    👉 Pronunciare bene -ed migliora molto l'intelligibilità, soprattutto quando racconti eventi in sequenza.

    ⚠️ Error clinic – errori tipici degli italiani

    ❌ I go to the cinema yesterday.
    ✅ I went to the cinema yesterday.

    ❌ Did you went there alone?
    ✅ Did you go there alone?

    ❌ I didn't saw him.
    ✅ I didn't see him.

    ❌ She studied English and then she didn't went out.
    ✅ She studied English and then she didn't go out.

    ❌ Yesterday I was go to work.
    ✅ Yesterday I went to work.

    Perché succedono questi errori?

    Per tre motivi molto comuni:

    1. trasferimento diretto dall'italiano
    2. confusione tra passato nel verbo e passato nell'ausiliare did
    3. scarsa automatizzazione dei verbi irregolari più frequenti

    🔄 Transformation drill

    Trasforma seguendo l'indicazione.

    1. I go to work by bus. → (yesterday)
    2. She doesn't call her parents. → (last night)
    3. Do they eat at home? → (yesterday evening)
    4. We see our friends every weekend. → (last Saturday)
    5. He buys lunch at the station. → (two days ago)
    6. I don't have time for breakfast. → (yesterday morning)

    Poi controlla:

    • hai cambiato il verbo principale al passato solo dove serve?
    • con did / didn't hai lasciato il verbo base?
    • hai aggiunto un riferimento temporale chiaro?

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa con la forma corretta.

    1. We ___ (leave) the office at 6:30.
    2. She ___ (not come) to class yesterday.
    3. ___ you ___ (see) Luca last weekend?
    4. I ___ (have) dinner at home and then I ___ (watch) a film.
    5. They ___ (buy) train tickets online.
    6. He ___ (study) all afternoon but he ___ (not finish) the report.

    🧠 Self-check

    Riesci a:

    • raccontare una breve sequenza di eventi al passato?
    • usare correttamente went / had / saw / bought / met?
    • fare domande con Did you...? senza usare il passato due volte?
    • scrivere un piccolo racconto con ordine logico e dettagli?

    Se sì, la base del racconto al passato è pronta.
