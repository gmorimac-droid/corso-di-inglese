    Unità 17 – Quantifiers (some, any, much, many)

    Modalità Base attiva. Questa unità ti aiuta a parlare di quantità in inglese in modo corretto e naturale. Imparerai a usare some, any, much e many, a distinguere tra nomi numerabili e non numerabili e a evitare errori molto comuni. Per la versione estesa usa il toggle BASE / PRO nella barra in alto.

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • usare some e any nelle frasi affermative, negative e interrogative
    • usare much e many con i nomi giusti
    • parlare di quantità in contesti quotidiani
    • fare domande semplici su quantità e disponibilità
    • evitare errori comuni come much apples o I don't have some money

    🧠 Grammar in context

    Some

    Usiamo some soprattutto nelle frasi affermative.

    • I have some water.
    • She bought some apples.
    • We need some sugar.

    A volte some si usa anche in offerte o richieste gentili.

    • Would you like some coffee?
    • Can I have some bread, please?

    Any

    Usiamo any soprattutto nelle frasi negative e nelle domande.

    • I don't have any money.
    • There isn't any milk.
    • Do you have any questions?
    • Is there any juice in the fridge?

    Much vs Many

    Usiamo many con i nomi countable al plurale.

    • many books
    • many people
    • many shops
    • many emails

    Usiamo much con i nomi uncountable.

    • much water
    • much time
    • much money
    • much food

    Esempi:

    • We don't have much time.
    • There are many students in the class.
    • How many eggs do we need?
    • How much rice is there?

    How much / How many

    • How many + countable plural
    • How much + uncountable

    Esempi:

    • How many chairs are there?
    • How many apples do you want?
    • How much coffee do you drink?
    • How much money do you need?

    📚 Core vocabulary

    Countable nouns

    • apples
    • bottles
    • chairs
    • books
    • bags
    • sandwiches
    • students
    • questions

    Uncountable nouns

    • water
    • coffee
    • money
    • food
    • rice
    • sugar
    • time
    • information

    Useful quantity phrases

    • some water
    • some apples
    • any milk
    • any problems
    • much time
    • many people
    • a lot of food
    • a lot of books

    🎧 Listening / Reading

    Dialogue 1 – at home

    A: Do we have any milk? B: No, we don't have any milk. A: What about coffee? B: We have some coffee, but not much.

    Dialogue 2 – at the supermarket

    A: How many apples do you want? B: Six, please. A: And how much cheese? B: About 200 grams.

    🗣️ Speaking

    Ripeti:

    • I have some water.
    • I don't have any money.
    • Do you have any bread?
    • There aren't many people here.
    • We don't have much time.

    👉 Ora parla di te:

    • What food do you have at home?
    • Do you have any homework today?
    • How much coffee do you drink?
    • How many books do you read in a year?

    👉 Fai domande:

    • Do you have any questions?
    • How much time do we have?
    • How many students are there in your class?

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi 8–10 frasi usando questi elementi:

    1. some
    2. any
    3. much
    4. many
    5. How much...?
    6. How many...?

    Puoi seguire questo modello:

    I have some tea at home, but I don't have any coffee.
    I don't have much free time during the week.
    There are many good cafés near my office.
    How many emails do you write every day?

    🔊 Pronunciation

    Attenzione a queste parole:

    • some → /sʌm/
    • any → /ˈeni/
    • many → /ˈmeni/
    • much → /mʌtʃ/
    • money → /ˈmʌni/

    👉 much e money non si pronunciano come in italiano. Fai attenzione anche alla differenza tra many e money.

    ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

    ❌ I don't have some money
    ✅ I don't have any money

    ❌ I have much apples
    ✅ I have many apples

    ❌ I have many money
    ✅ I have much money / I have a lot of money

    ❌ How much books do you have?
    ✅ How many books do you have?

    ❌ There isn't many water
    ✅ There isn't much water

    🧠 Pattern fondamentali

    • some → di solito in affermativa
    • any → di solito in negativa e interrogativa
    • many → con countable plural
    • much → con uncountable
    • How many...? → countable plural
    • How much...? → uncountable

    Esempi:

    • We need some eggs.
    • We don't need any eggs.
    • How many eggs do we need?
    • How much milk is there?

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa con some, any, much o many:

    1. We need ___ bread.
    2. I don't have ___ time.
    3. Are there ___ students in the room?
    4. Do you have ___ questions?
    5. There are ___ books on the table.
    6. We don't have ___ milk.

    🔄 Trasforma

    Trasforma le frasi:

    1. I have coffee. → I have ___ coffee.
    2. I don't have money. → I don't have ___ money.
    3. apples → many ___
    4. water → much ___
    5. How ___ chairs are there?
    6. How ___ sugar do we need?

    🚀 Mini task

    Sei a casa e stai preparando una lista della spesa. Scrivi 6–8 frasi usando:

    • some
    • any
    • much
    • many
    • How much...?
    • How many...?

    Esempio:

    We need some fruit and some pasta.
    We don't have any milk.
    How many eggs do we need?
    We don't need much sugar.

    💬 Self-check

    Sai:

    • usare some e any correttamente?
    • distinguere much e many?
    • fare domande con How much e How many?
    • riconoscere i nomi countable e uncountable più comuni?

    Se la risposta è sì, sei pronto per la prossima unità.

    Modalità Professional attiva. In questa unità lavori sui quantifiers in modo più realistico e preciso. Non ti limiti a scegliere tra some, any, much e many: impari a usarli in frasi naturali, a distinguere registro e frequenza d'uso, a fare richieste educate, a parlare di disponibilità, scarsità e abbondanza, e a evitare errori tipici degli italiani con i nomi countable e uncountable.

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • usare con sicurezza some, any, much, many e a lot of in contesti reali
    • distinguere bene tra countable e uncountable nouns e scegliere il quantifier corretto
    • fare domande naturali su quantità e disponibilità con How much e How many
    • usare some anche in offerte e richieste, non solo in frasi affermative
    • parlare di quantità in casa, al supermercato, al lavoro e nello studio con più precisione e naturalezza

    🧭 Language focus – quantifiers in real English

    1. Some e any: la regola base è utile, ma non basta

    La regola iniziale è questa:

    • some → soprattutto in frasi affermative
    • any → soprattutto in negative e domande

    Esempi base:

    • We have some bread.
    • We don't have any bread.
    • Do we have any bread?

    Questa regola è corretta, ma in inglese reale some compare anche in richieste e offerte quando il parlante pensa che la risposta sarà positiva o vuole essere cortese.

    • Would you like some tea?
    • Can I have some water, please?
    • Could you give me some information?

    👉 Quindi non pensare a some solo come “affermativa”: pensa anche a quantità presente o desiderata in modo concreto e cortese.

    2. Much e many: corretto grammaticalmente, ma attenzione alla naturalezza

    La regola formale:

    • many + countable plural
    • much + uncountable

    Esempi:

    • many people
    • many emails
    • much time
    • much money

    Ma nel parlato quotidiano, nelle frasi affermative, spesso preferiamo a lot of.

    Confronta:

    • I have many friends in London. ✅
    • I have a lot of friends in London. ✅ più naturale nel parlato
    • We drink much coffee. ⚠️ possibile, ma poco naturale
    • We drink a lot of coffee. ✅ molto più naturale

    👉 Usa much soprattutto:

    • in frasi negative: We don't have much time.
    • in domande: How much money do you need?
    • in contesti più formali o scritti

    3. Countable vs uncountable: la scelta del quantifier dipende dal nome

    Countable nouns

    Si possono contare singolarmente:

    • a chair / two chairs
    • a student / three students
    • a sandwich / four sandwiches
    • an idea / many ideas

    Con questi usiamo:

    • many
    • a few
    • numeri
    • How many...?

    Uncountable nouns

    Non si contano normalmente come unità separate:

    • water
    • milk
    • rice
    • information
    • advice
    • furniture
    • luggage
    • homework

    Con questi usiamo:

    • much
    • a little
    • some
    • How much...?

    👉 Errori tipici da evitare:

    • informations ❌ → information ✅
    • advices ❌ → advice ✅
    • furnitures ❌ → furniture ✅
    • homeworks ❌ → homework ✅

    4. Some, any, much, many in four common situations

    At home

    • We have some pasta, but we don't have any rice.
    • There isn't much milk left.
    • How many eggs are in the fridge?

    At the supermarket

    • Do we need any fruit?
    • We need some tomatoes and a lot of water.
    • How much cheese should I buy?

    At work

    • I don't have much time this morning.
    • We don't have many people available today.
    • Do you have any updates on the project?

    In study situations

    • I don't have much homework tonight.
    • She has a lot of notes, but not many books.
    • Do you have any questions about the exercise?

    🧠 Forms and contrasts

    Some / Any

    • some coffee
    • some emails
    • any sugar
    • any problems

    Much / Many

    • much time
    • much money
    • many students
    • many mistakes

    More natural spoken alternatives

    • a lot of time
    • a lot of money
    • a lot of students
    • a lot of mistakes

    Key question patterns

    • How much time do we have?
    • How much water do you drink?
    • How many lessons do you have this week?
    • How many messages did you get?

    📚 Vocabulary expansion

    Common countable nouns

    • bottles
    • chairs
    • files
    • questions
    • mistakes
    • lessons
    • buses
    • meetings
    • customers
    • coins

    Common uncountable nouns

    • water
    • coffee
    • money
    • time
    • advice
    • information
    • furniture
    • traffic
    • homework
    • luggage

    Useful collocations

    • some free time
    • some useful information
    • any questions
    • any problems
    • much experience
    • much traffic
    • many options
    • many opportunities
    • a lot of work
    • a lot of energy

    🎧 Reading – realistic contexts

    Text 1 – before a dinner party

    We need to check what we have at home before people arrive. There is some bread and there are some bottles of water, but there isn't much juice left. We don't have any ice cream, and there aren't many snacks in the cupboard. How many guests are coming? If there are ten people, we need a lot of food.

    Text 2 – at work

    I'm quite busy this week. I don't have much free time, and I still have a lot of work to finish. There are many emails in my inbox, but there isn't much useful information in some of them. Do you have any time this afternoon to help me?

    🔍 Notice the language

    Osserva queste scelte:

    • there is some bread / there are some bottles → some con uncountable e con plural countable
    • there isn't much juice → much con uncountable in negativa
    • there aren't many snacks → many con plural countable in negativa
    • a lot of food / a lot of work → molto naturale nel parlato
    • any time this afternoon → any in domanda su disponibilità

    🗣️ Speaking drills

    Drill 1 – Choose and justify

    Completa e spiega la scelta:

    1. We don't have ___ chairs for everyone.
    2. Is there ___ milk in the fridge?
    3. I don't have ___ time today.
    4. There are ___ good restaurants in this area.
    5. Could I have ___ information about the course?

    Drill 2 – Make it more natural

    Rendi più naturale la frase.

    • We have much work today.
      → We have a lot of work today.

    • I want some informations.
      → I need some information.

    • How much students are there?
      → How many students are there?

    • I don't have some money.
      → I don't have any money.

    Drill 3 – Short role-plays

    Role-play A – office

    A: Do you have any time after lunch?
    B: I don't have much time, but I can stay for twenty minutes.

    Role-play B – shopping

    A: How many bottles of water do we need?
    B: Let's get four. We don't have any at home.

    Role-play C – study

    A: Do you have any questions about the homework?
    B: Yes, I have a few questions, but not many.

    ✍️ Writing

    Task 1 – What we have and what we need

    Scrivi un testo di 120–150 parole su una di queste situazioni:

    1. preparare una cena a casa
    2. organizzare una piccola riunione di lavoro
    3. preparare una settimana di studio

    Nel testo prova a usare:

    • some almeno 3 volte
    • any almeno 2 volte
    • much almeno 2 volte
    • many almeno 2 volte
    • a lot of almeno 2 volte
    • almeno 4 nomi uncountable corretti

    Task 2 – Quantity questions

    Scrivi 8 domande realistiche con How much o How many, per esempio su:

    • cibo
    • tempo
    • denaro
    • lavoro
    • studio
    • viaggi

    🔊 Pronunciation

    Attenzione a queste parole:

    • some → /sʌm/
    • much → /mʌtʃ/
    • many → /ˈmeni/
    • money → /ˈmʌni/
    • any → /ˈeni/

    👉 Allenati a distinguere:

    • many /ˈmeni/
    • money /ˈmʌni/

    Sono parole molto comuni e spesso gli italiani le confondono nella pronuncia.

    ⚠️ Error clinic – errori tipici degli italiani

    ❌ I don't have some time
    ✅ I don't have any time

    ❌ There are much people here
    ✅ There are many people here

    ❌ How much chairs do we need?
    ✅ How many chairs do we need?

    ❌ I have many homeworks
    ✅ I have a lot of homework / I have a lot of assignments

    ❌ Can you give me some informations?
    ✅ Can you give me some information?

    ❌ We haven't many milk
    ✅ We don't have much milk

    🧩 Transformation practice

    Riscrivi in inglese corretto e naturale:

    1. Non abbiamo molto tempo oggi.
    2. Ci sono molte persone fuori.
    3. Hai qualche domanda?
    4. Quante lezioni hai questa settimana?
    5. Quanta acqua bevi ogni giorno?
    6. Ho molte cose da fare e non molto tempo.
    7. Vorrei qualche informazione sul corso.
    8. Non ci sono molti autobus la sera.

    ✅ Final review

    Completa con la forma migliore:

    1. We need ___ help with this report.
    2. There aren't ___ chairs in this room.
    3. Do you have ___ advice for me?
    4. I don't have ___ free time this month.
    5. There are ___ useful websites for English learners.
    6. How ___ luggage do you usually take on holiday?
    7. How ___ exercises do you do every week?
    8. We bought ___ food for the weekend.

    🧠 Self-check

    Sai:

    • distinguere rapidamente tra countable e uncountable nouns?
    • usare some e any anche in richieste e offerte?
    • scegliere tra much / many / a lot of in modo naturale?
    • fare domande corrette con How much e How many?
    • correggere errori come informations, much people, some money in negativa?

    Se sì, la base grammaticale dei quantifiers è solida e puoi passare alla prossima unità con maggiore controllo e naturalezza.
