# Unità 18 – Future (Will / Going to)

---

## 🎯 Obiettivo

Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

- parlare del futuro
- fare piani
- esprimere decisioni
- usare *will* e *going to* correttamente

---

## 🧠 Grammar in context

### Going to → piani

👉 qualcosa che hai già deciso

- I am going to travel  
- I am going to study  
- I am going to eat pizza  

---

### Esempi

- I am going to visit Rome  
- I am going to work tomorrow  
- I am going to study English  

---

## ⚡ Will → decisioni spontanee

👉 decisione presa ORA

- I will help you  
- I will call you  
- I will go  

---

### Esempi

- I will help you  
- I will open the door  
- I will call later  

---

## ⚖️ Differenza chiave

| Going to | Will |
|----------|------|
| piano | decisione immediata |
| già deciso | deciso ora |

---

### Esempio chiaro

- I am going to travel tomorrow (già deciso)  
- Ok, I will go with you (decido ora)  

---

## 📚 Core vocabulary

### Future expressions

- tomorrow  
- next week  
- tonight  
- later  

---

### Verbi utili

- travel  
- study  
- work  
- call  
- help  
- go  

---

## 🎧 Listening / Reading

**Dialogue:**

A: What are you going to do tomorrow?  
B: I am going to study.  

A: I’m tired.

---

## 🗣️ Speaking

Ripeti:

- I am going to study  
- I am going to travel  
- I will help  
- I will call  

---

👉 Ora parla:

- I am going to ___  
- I will ___  

---

👉 Fai domande:

- What are you going to do?  
- Will you help me?  

---

## ✍️ Writing

Scrivi 6 frasi:

👉 3 con *going to*  
👉 3 con *will*  

Esempio:

> I am going to study tomorrow.  
> I am going to travel next week.  
> I am going to eat pizza.  
>  
> I will help you.  
> I will call you.  
> I will go later.

---

## 🔊 Pronunciation

Forma naturale:

- going to → **gonna** (informale)  
👉 I’m gonna go  

- will → spesso debole  
👉 I’ll help  

---

## ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

❌ I will to go  
✅ I will go  

❌ I going to study  
✅ I am going to study  

❌ I will going  
✅ I will go  

---

## 🧠 Pattern fondamentale

- I am going to + verbo  
- I will + verbo  

👉 mai usare “to” dopo *will*
