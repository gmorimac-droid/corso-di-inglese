# Unità 19 – Experiences (Have you ever...?)

---

## 🎯 Obiettivo

Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

- parlare di esperienze di vita
- fare domande con *Have you ever...?*
- rispondere in modo naturale
- sostenere una conversazione reale

---

## 🧠 Grammar in context

### Struttura base

👉 **Have + subject + past participle**

- Have you ever been to Rome?  
- Have you ever eaten sushi?  

---

### Risposte

- Yes, I have  
- No, I haven’t  

---

### Esempi

- I have been to Spain  
- I have eaten pizza  
- I have seen that film  

---

## 📚 Core vocabulary

### Esperienze

- travel  
- visit  
- eat  
- see  
- try  
- go  

---

### Espressioni utili

- ever  
- never  

---

### Esempi

- I have never been to London  
- I have never tried sushi  

---

## 🎧 Listening / Reading

**Dialogue:**

A: Have you ever been to Paris?  
B: Yes, I have.  

A: Have you ever eaten sushi?  
B: No, I haven’t.  

---

## 🗣️ Speaking

Ripeti:

- Have you ever been to Italy?  
- Have you ever eaten sushi?  

---

👉 Ora prova:

- Have you ever ___?  

---

👉 Rispondi:

- Yes, I have  
- No, I haven’t  

---

## ✍️ Writing

Scrivi 5 frasi:

👉 3 esperienze  
👉 2 cose mai fatte  

Esempio:

> I have been to Rome.  
> I have eaten pizza.  
> I have seen a film.  
> I have never tried sushi.  
> I have never been to London.

---

## 🔊 Pronunciation

Attenzione:

- have → spesso debole: /həv/  
- ever → /ˈevər/  

👉 “Have you ever” → suona fluido: *hav-you-ever*

---

## ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

❌ Have you ever went  
✅ Have you ever gone  

❌ I have see  
✅ I have seen  

❌ I have been in Rome  
✅ I have been to Rome  

---

## 🧠 Pattern fondamentale

- Have you ever + verbo?  
- I have + verbo  

👉 struttura sempre uguale

---

## 🔁 Differenza con Past Simple

👉 molto importante:

- Past Simple → quando (when)  
- Present Perfect → esperienza (life)

---

### Esempio

- I went to Rome last year (quando)  
- I have been to Rome (esperienza)

---
