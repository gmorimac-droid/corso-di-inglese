# Unità 09 – Can / Can't

---

## 🎯 Obiettivo

Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

- parlare di abilità (skills)
- dire cosa puoi o non puoi fare
- fare domande con *can*
- rispondere in modo naturale

---

## 🧠 Grammar in context

### Forma base

👉 **Subject + can + verb**

- I can swim  
- You can speak English  
- She can drive  

---

### Forma negativa

👉 **Subject + cannot / can't + verb**

- I cannot swim  
- I can’t drive  
- He can’t speak English  

---

### Forma interrogativa

👉 **Can + subject + verb?**

- Can you swim?  
- Can she drive?  
- Can they speak English?  

---

### Risposte brevi

- Yes, I can / No, I can’t  
- Yes, she can / No, she can’t  

---

## 📚 Core vocabulary

### Abilità (skills)

- swim  
- drive  
- speak  
- read  
- write  
- cook  
- play (football, piano)  

---

### Espressioni utili

- very well  
- a little  
- not very well  

---

## 🎧 Listening / Reading

**Dialogue:**

A: Can you swim?  
B: Yes, I can.  

A: Can you drive?  
B: No, I can’t.  

A: Can you speak English?  
B: Yes, a little.

---

## ✍️ Writing

Scrivi:

👉 3 cose che puoi fare  
👉 3 cose che non puoi fare  

Esempio:

> I can swim.  
> I can cook.  
> I can speak English.  
> I can’t drive.  
> I can’t play piano.  
> I can’t dance.

---

## 🔊 Pronunciation

Attenzione:

- can → /kæn/  
- can’t → /kɑːnt/ (UK)  

👉 importante la differenza:

- can (debole) → *kən*  
- can’t (forte) → *KANT*

---

## ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

❌ I can to swim  
✅ I can swim  

❌ I can’t to drive  
✅ I can’t drive  

❌ Can you can swim?  
✅ Can you swim?  

👉 Dopo *can* → verbo base (senza “to”)

---

## 🧠 Pattern fondamentale

- I can + verbo  
- I can’t + verbo  

👉 semplicissimo → molto potente
