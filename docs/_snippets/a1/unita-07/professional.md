## 🧠 Grammar in context

## There is / There are

Usiamo **there is / there are** per introdurre l’esistenza di qualcosa in un luogo.

### Singular: **there is**

Per una cosa sola:

- There is a desk in the room.
- There is an old sofa near the wall.
- There is a lamp on the table.

### Plural: **there are**

Per più cose:

- There are two chairs in the office.
- There are some books on the shelf.
- There are three windows in the living room.

Il punto essenziale è questo:

- **there is** → una cosa
- **there are** → più cose

---

## A / An / Some / Any

Quando descrivi un luogo, molto spesso queste parole lavorano insieme a **there is / there are**.

### **a / an**

Per un nome singolare numerabile:

- There is **a** chair.
- There is **a** table.
- There is **an** armchair.
- There is **an** office next door.

### **some**

Molto comune nelle frasi affermative plurali:

- There are **some** books on the desk.
- There are **some** files in the drawer.

### **any**

Molto comune nelle domande e nelle frasi negative:

- Are there **any** chairs in the room?
- There aren’t **any** pictures on the wall.

👉 A questo livello va benissimo ricordare questa regola pratica:

- **some** → spesso affermativa
- **any** → spesso domanda / negativa

---

## Negative forms

### Singular

- There isn’t a TV in the room.
- There isn’t a window in this office.

### Plural

- There aren’t any chairs here.
- There aren’t any books on the shelf.

Quando parli di ciò che manca, queste forme sono essenziali.

---

## Questions

### Singular

- Is there a bathroom?
- Is there a printer in the office?
- Is there a lamp on the desk?

### Plural

- Are there any windows?
- Are there two meeting rooms?
- Are there any people in the office?

### Short answers

- Yes, there is.
- No, there isn’t.
- Yes, there are.
- No, there aren’t.

---

## There is / It is

Questa distinzione è molto importante.

### **There is / There are**

Si usa per **introdurre** qualcosa:

- There is a laptop on the desk.
- There are two windows in the room.

### **It is / They are**

Si usa quando l’oggetto è già noto e vuoi aggiungere informazioni:

- There is a laptop on the desk. **It is** new.
- There are two windows in the room. **They are** very large.

👉 Quindi:

- **there is / there are** = esistenza
- **it is / they are** = descrizione dell’oggetto già introdotto

## Prepositions of place you need now

Per rendere la descrizione più utile, aggiungi semplici preposizioni di luogo:

- **in** the room
- **on** the desk
- **under** the table
- **next to** the bed
- **near** the window
- **behind** the chair
- **in front of** the sofa

Esempi:

- There is a lamp **on** the desk.
- There are two boxes **under** the table.
- There is a chair **next to** the window.

## 🎧 Listening / Reading

### Text 1 – Describing a bedroom

> There is a bed in the middle of the room.  
> There is a small desk near the window.  
> There is a lamp on the desk and there are some books under it.  
> There are two pictures on the wall.  
> There isn’t a TV, but there is a laptop on the chair.

### Text 2 – Describing an office

> In our office, there are six desks and twelve chairs.  
> There is a printer near the door.  
> There are some documents on the main table.  
> There isn’t a meeting room on this floor, but there are two phone booths.  
> There are also a few plants near the windows.

### Notice

Osserva che le descrizioni più naturali non sono una lista casuale. Spesso seguono un ordine:

1. visione generale del luogo
2. oggetti principali
3. oggetti secondari
4. elementi mancanti
5. dettagli finali

## 🗣️ Speaking

### Drill 1 – Singular or plural?

Completa oralmente:

- There is a ______ in the room.
- There are two ______ near the wall.
- There isn’t a ______ on the table.
- There aren’t any ______ under the desk.

### Drill 2 – Add position

Espandi le frasi:

- There is a lamp. → There is a lamp on the desk.
- There are two chairs. → There are two chairs near the window.
- There is a bag. → There is a bag under the table.

### Drill 3 – Ask and answer

Pratica in coppia o da solo:

- Is there a printer in the office?
- Are there any books on the shelf?
- Is there a sofa in the living room?
- Are there two doors in the room?

Rispondi con short answers e poi con una frase completa.

### Drill 4 – One-minute room description

Parla per 1 minuto e prova a usare:

- 3 frasi con **there is**
- 3 frasi con **there are**
- 1 frase negativa
- 2 preposizioni di luogo

## ✍️ Writing

### Task 1 – Guided description

Scrivi 8–10 frasi su una stanza reale o immaginaria.

Checklist:

- una frase introduttiva generale
- almeno 3 oggetti singolari
- almeno 3 oggetti plurali
- almeno 2 dettagli con preposizioni di luogo
- almeno 1 frase negativa

Modello:

> In my room, there is a desk near the window.  
> There is a lamp on the desk.  
> There are some books next to the lamp.  
> There is a chair under the window.  
> There are two pictures on the wall.  
> There isn’t a TV.  
> There is a small plant on the shelf.

### Task 2 – Compare two spaces

Scrivi 4 frasi su una stanza a casa e 4 frasi su un ufficio o aula.

Obiettivo: imparare a riusare la stessa struttura in contesti diversi.

## 🔊 Pronunciation

### **There is** → **There’s**

Nel parlato naturale la forma contratta è molto comune:

- There’s a chair by the door.
- There’s a lamp on the desk.

### **There are**

Non sempre si contrae nel parlato standard lento. All’inizio è meglio pronunciarlo bene:

- There are two books.
- There are three people in the office.

### Rhythm tip

Le parole forti di solito sono i nomi e le posizioni:

- There’s a **lamp** on the **desk**.
- There are two **chairs** near the **window**.

## ⚠️ Errori comuni degli italiani

### 1. Usare **there have**

❌ There have two chairs.  
✅ There are two chairs.

### 2. Confondere singolare e plurale

❌ There is two windows.  
✅ There are two windows.

❌ There are a desk.  
✅ There is a desk.

### 3. Dimenticare **there**

❌ Is a lamp on the table.  
✅ There is a lamp on the table.

### 4. Usare male **any**

❌ There aren’t some books.  
✅ There aren’t any books.

### 5. Usare **is** con liste plurali

❌ There is a desk and two chairs.  
✅ There are two chairs and a desk.  
✅ There is a desk and there are two chairs.

👉 In inglese reale, quando la frase diventa lunga, spesso è più chiaro **dividerla in due**.

## ✅ Review & Practice

### Completa

1. There ___ a chair next to the desk.
2. There ___ three books on the table.
3. There ___ not a printer in this room.
4. Are there ___ windows in the office?
5. There are ___ files in the drawer.
6. Is there ___ lamp near the bed?

### Correggi gli errori

1. There is two computers.
2. There are a chair near the window.
3. There isn’t some books on the shelf.
4. There have a printer in the office.
5. Is there any chairs?

### Trasforma

1. There is a lamp on the desk. → negativa
2. There are some chairs in the room. → domanda
3. There is a window near the bed. → plurale
4. There aren’t any pictures on the wall. → affermativa
