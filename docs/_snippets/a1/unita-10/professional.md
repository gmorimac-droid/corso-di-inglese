## 🎯 Obiettivo

Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

- usare insieme le principali strutture A1 senza trattarle come blocchi isolati
- sostenere una breve interazione personale con presentazione, domande e risposte
- descrivere in modo semplice ma coerente la tua routine, il tuo ambiente e le tue abilità
- individuare i tuoi errori più frequenti e correggerli

---

## 🧭 Focus della review finale

In una review efficace non basta sapere la regola. Devi verificare se sai:

1. **recuperare** la struttura giusta velocemente
2. **scegliere** il lessico adatto al contesto
3. **collegare** le frasi in modo logico
4. **mantenere la correttezza** nelle forme più frequenti
5. **parlare e scrivere** con un minimo di continuità

A livello A1, il vero salto di qualità è questo: passare da frasi singole come *I am Marco* o *There is a table* a una micro-produzione continua come una presentazione personale, una descrizione della propria giornata o una breve interazione con una persona nuova.

## 🧠 A1 Grammar Map

Queste sono le strutture chiave che devi sapere integrare.

### 1. Identity and personal information

- I am ...
- I am from ...
- I am ... years old.
- I am a student / a teacher / an engineer.

### 2. Daily habits and routines

- I wake up at ...
- I go to work by ...
- She works in a bank.
- He studies English every day.

### 3. Questions

- What is your name?
- Where are you from?
- What do you do?
- Where do you work?
- Can you swim?
- Is there a supermarket near your house?

### 4. Objects and reference

- this / that
- these / those
- singular / plural

### 5. Description of places

- There is a ...
- There are ...
- There isn't ...
- Are there any ...?

### 6. Ability

- I can ...
- I can't ...
- She can speak English quite well.

👉 Se sai usare questi sei blocchi nello stesso compito, il tuo A1 è davvero operativo.

## 🧪 Diagnostic Check – Form Recognition

Scegli la forma corretta.

1. She **is / are** from Spain.
2. He **work / works** in a hotel.
3. **Do / Does** she live in Rome?
4. There **is / are** two books on the desk.
5. **Can / Does** you swim?
6. These **is / are** my keys.
7. I **can't / don't can** drive.
8. What time **do / does** he start work?
9. There isn't **some / any** milk.
10. **Is / Are** there a pharmacy near here?

### Chiave

1. is  
2. works  
3. Does  
4. are  
5. Can  
6. are  
7. can't  
8. does  
9. any  
10. Is

## 🧩 Integrated Grammar Practice

Completa il testo con la forma corretta.

> Hello, my name ___ Elena. I ___ 29 years old and I ___ from Italy. I ___ in a small office in the city centre. Every day I ___ up at 7:00 and ___ breakfast at home. Then I ___ to work by bus. In my office there ___ a big desk and there ___ two computers. Near my office there ___ a café and a bank. I ___ cook quite well, but I ___ drive.

### Chiave

> **is / am / am / work / wake / have / go / is / are / is / can / can't**

👉 Qui vedi se riesci a cambiare struttura in pochi secondi: **to be**, **Present Simple**, **there is / there are**, **can / can't**.

## 🔄 Transformation Drill

Trasforma mantenendo il significato.

1. I am a student. → negative  
2. She works in a shop. → question  
3. There is a park near my house. → negative  
4. There are two chairs in the room. → singular  
5. He can swim. → negative  
6. They are from France. → question  
7. This is my bag. → plural  
8. I go to work by bus. → third person singular  

### Possibili soluzioni

1. I am not a student.  
2. Does she work in a shop?  
3. There isn't a park near my house.  
4. There is a chair in the room.  
5. He can't swim.  
6. Are they from France?  
7. These are my bags.  
8. He goes to work by bus.

## 🎧 Reading + Language Notice

Leggi il testo.

> Hi, I'm Daniel. I'm 31 and I'm from Dublin, but now I live in Milan. I work in a hotel near the station. My day starts early: I wake up at 6:15, have coffee and leave home at 7:00. I usually go to work by tram. At work there is a reception desk, a small office and a staff room. There are also two computers and a printer. In my free time I can cook quite well and I can take good photos, but I can't play any musical instrument.

### Comprensione

1. Where is Daniel from?
2. Where does he live now?
3. What time does he leave home?
4. What is in his workplace?
5. What can he do?
6. What can't he do?

### Language notice

Nel testo compaiono insieme:

- **I'm / I'm from** → identità
- **I work / I wake up / I go** → routine
- **there is / there are** → descrizione di luogo
- **I can / I can't** → abilità

Questa è esattamente la competenza che devi consolidare a fine A1.

## 🗣️ Speaking Performance – Step by Step

### Task 1 – 30 seconds

Presentati usando almeno 4 informazioni:

- name
- age
- country/city
- job/studies

### Task 2 – 45 seconds

Descrivi una giornata tipica:

- what time you wake up
- how you go to work or school
- what you do in the evening

### Task 3 – 45 seconds

Descrivi una stanza o il tuo quartiere:

- there is / there are
- at least 4 objects or places
- one opinion sentence

### Task 4 – 30 seconds

Parla delle tue abilità:

- two things you can do
- one thing you can't do
- one thing you can do only a little

### Combined final task – 2 minutes

Simula di incontrare una persona nuova. Devi:

- presentarti
- fare **2 domande**
- parlare della tua routine
- descrivere dove vivi
- parlare di una tua abilità

#### Useful speaking frame

> Hi, my name is ...  
> I'm ... years old and I'm from ...  
> I live in ... and I work / study ...  
> Every day I wake up at ... and I go to ... by ...  
> In my room / in my area there is ... and there are ...  
> I can ..., but I can't ... very well.  
> What about you? Where are you from? What do you do?

## ✍️ Writing Task – Guided Production

Scrivi un testo di **120–150 parole**. Il tema è:

### **About me: my life, my routine and my town**

Il testo deve includere:

- who you are
- where you are from / where you live
- what you do
- your typical day
- a short description of your room or town
- what you can and can't do
- one personal preference or opinion

### Suggested structure

**Paragraph 1 – Personal profile**  
Name, age, country, city, job/studies

**Paragraph 2 – Daily routine**  
Morning, transport, work/study, evening

**Paragraph 3 – Description + ability**  
Room/town, places or objects, abilities

### Useful connectors

- and
- but
- then
- after that
- usually
- also

## 🛠️ Error Clinic – Typical Italian Learner Problems

### 1. Age

❌ I have 28 years.  
✅ I am 28 years old.

### 2. Third person singular

❌ She work in a bank.  
✅ She works in a bank.

### 3. Questions without auxiliary

❌ Where you live?  
✅ Where do you live?

❌ What he does?  
✅ What does he do?

### 4. Incorrect modal structure

❌ I can to swim.  
✅ I can swim.

❌ She doesn't can drive.  
✅ She can't drive.

### 5. Singular/plural confusion with there is / there are

❌ There is two parks near my house.  
✅ There are two parks near my house.

### 6. Overuse of "is" before main verbs

❌ He is work in an office.  
✅ He works in an office.

👉 Se questi errori compaiono ancora spesso, il passaggio ad A2 è possibile solo dopo un breve consolidamento.

## 🔊 Pronunciation and Fluency Review

Ripeti in gruppi di senso, non parola per parola:

- I'm from Italy and I live in Turin.
- I work in a café near the station.
- There are two chairs next to the desk.
- Can you speak English quite well?
- I can cook, but I can't drive yet.

### Mini fluency drill

Leggi tre volte questa micro-presentazione, ogni volta un po’ più fluentemente:

> Hi, I'm Sara. I'm 26 years old and I'm from Italy. I work in a bookshop. Every day I wake up at 7, have breakfast and go to work by bus. In my room there is a desk and there are a lot of books. I can speak English a little and I can cook quite well, but I can't drive.

## 🚀 Bridge to A2

Nel livello A2 inizierai a fare qualcosa di nuovo: non solo descrivere il presente, ma anche **parlare del passato**, organizzare meglio il discorso e gestire scambi più naturali.

Per arrivarci bene, alla fine di A1 devi avere tre basi forti:

1. frasi corrette
2. domande corrette
3. mini-produzioni continue di 1–2 minuti

Se queste basi ci sono, A2 diventa molto più fluido e molto meno frustrante.

> A1 ends when basic language becomes usable, not when rules are simply memorised.
