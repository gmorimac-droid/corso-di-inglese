# Unità 25 – Giving Opinions

---

## 🎯 Lesson Goals

Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

- esprimere opinioni in modo più sfumato e naturale, non solo con il semplice **I think**
- mostrare accordo, disaccordo, riserva, dubbio e parziale concessione con formule utili nel parlato reale
- sostenere un’opinione con ragioni, esempi e contrasti
- partecipare a conversazioni su temi quotidiani, studio, lavoro, tecnologia e società in modo più maturo
- evitare errori tipici degli italofoni, soprattutto **I am agree**, **I think yes**, uso eccessivo di **I think** e mancanza di coesione nel discorso

A livello **B1** non basta “dire la propria idea”: bisogna saperla formulare in modo chiaro, credibile e conversazionale.

---

## 🧠 Core idea: opinions are not only grammar, but interaction

Esprimere un’opinione in inglese significa saper fare almeno quattro cose:

1. **dire cosa pensi**
2. **mostrare il grado di certezza**
3. **reagire all’opinione degli altri**
4. **spiegare perché**

Per questo motivo, a livello B1 è utile andare oltre il trio base:

- I think...
- I agree.
- I don’t agree.

Queste formule funzionano, ma risultano limitate se usate sempre uguali. In conversazione reale servono varietà, tono e collegamenti logici.

---

## 1. Degrees of opinion: strong, neutral, cautious

### A. Neutral opinion

- I think this solution is practical.
- I think the course is well organised.
- In my opinion, this option is safer.

Queste forme sono dirette ma non aggressive.

### B. Stronger opinion

- I strongly believe that communication is more important than perfect grammar.
- I’m convinced that remote work will remain common.
- There’s no doubt in my mind that practice matters more than theory.

Queste strutture mostrano maggiore sicurezza.

### C. More cautious / diplomatic opinion

- I suppose it depends on the situation.
- I’m not entirely sure, but I think this could work.
- From my point of view, this seems a bit unrealistic.
- I’m not convinced that this is the best approach.

Queste formule sono molto utili quando vuoi sembrare più equilibrato o professionale.

---

## 2. The most useful opinion structures at B1

### Core forms

- I think...
- I don’t think...
- I believe...
- In my opinion...
- From my point of view...
- As far as I’m concerned...

### Examples

- I think this app is useful for beginners.
- I don’t think that policy is fair.
- I believe people learn faster when they practise regularly.
- From my point of view, working from home is efficient but not ideal for everyone.
- As far as I’m concerned, public transport should be cheaper and more reliable.

### Register note

- **I think...** = neutro, comune, molto frequente
- **I believe...** = leggermente più formale o più forte
- **In my opinion...** = chiaro e organizzato, utile anche nello scritto
- **From my point of view...** = naturale in contesti più discussivi

---

## 3. Agreeing and disagreeing naturally

A livello B1 è importante non sembrare troppo bruschi.

### Direct agreement

- I agree.
- I agree with you.
- Exactly.
- That’s true.
- I think so too.

### Softer disagreement

- I’m not sure I agree.
- I see your point, but...
- That may be true, but...
- I’m not convinced.
- I don’t really see it that way.

### Stronger disagreement

- I don’t agree.
- I don’t think that’s right.
- I think that view is too simplistic.

Per una conversazione civile e naturale, di solito è meglio iniziare con una formula più morbida prima di contraddire.

Esempio:

- I see your point, but I think cost is still a major problem.

---

## 4. Giving reasons and support

Un’opinione da sola spesso suona debole. A B1 devi imparare ad aggiungere supporto.

### Connectors that help

- because
- so
- that’s why
- for example
- however
- on the other hand

### Examples

- I think online learning is effective because it gives people more flexibility.
- I don’t think this plan will succeed, because it ignores the cost.
- Public transport is useful. That’s why cities should invest in it.
- I like remote work. However, I think teams still need some in-person meetings.
- For example, many employees are more productive when they can work from home.

---

## 5. Opinion + contrast = more mature speaking

Una delle abilità più importanti a livello B1 è riconoscere che due idee possono essere vere contemporaneamente.

Esempi:

- I think social media can be useful, but it can also be distracting.
- In my opinion, city life is exciting. On the other hand, it can be stressful.
- I agree that technology saves time; however, it sometimes reduces real interaction.

Questa struttura ti fa sembrare subito più naturale e meno “scolastico”.

---

## 6. Common conversation patterns

### A. Asking for opinions

- What do you think?
- What’s your opinion on this?
- How do you feel about working online?
- Do you agree?

### B. Giving opinions briefly

- I think it’s useful.
- I’m not sure it’s a good idea.
- In my opinion, it depends.

### C. Extending the opinion

- I think it’s useful because it saves time.
- I’m not sure it’s a good idea, especially for small teams.
- In my opinion, it depends on the context and on the people involved.

### D. Reacting to another speaker

- That’s a good point.
- I see what you mean.
- I understand your point, but...
- I hadn’t thought of it that way.

---

## 7. Extended examples in context

### Technology

- I think AI can be extremely helpful, especially when it saves people time on routine tasks.
- However, I don’t think it should replace human judgement in every situation.

### Work

- From my point of view, remote work improves concentration for many people.
- On the other hand, it can make communication slower if the team is not organised.

### Education

- I believe exams are useful, but I don’t think they should be the only way to evaluate students.

### Travel

- As far as I’m concerned, travelling by train is more relaxing than flying, even if it sometimes takes longer.

## 🎧 Reading: a discussion with real interaction

**Topic: Should companies allow remote work every day?**

Sara: In my opinion, remote work should remain available because it helps people manage their time better.

David: I agree to some extent, but I don’t think it works equally well for every job.

Sara: That’s fair. Still, I think many companies are too rigid.

David: Maybe, but some teams need regular face-to-face contact. Otherwise communication becomes weaker.

Sara: I see your point, but technology can solve part of that problem.

David: Yes, up to a point. However, I still believe in-person collaboration matters.

### Why this dialogue works

Qui vedi:

- opinioni espresse con intensità diverse
- accordo parziale
- disaccordo educato
- motivazioni e contrasti
- uso di connettori che rendono il discorso più credibile

## 🗣️ Speaking Drills

### Drill 1 – Basic opinion + reason

Completa a voce:

- I think learning English is important because ...
- I don’t think social media is always useful because ...
- In my opinion, a good teacher should ...
- From my point of view, travelling helps people ...

### Drill 2 – Agree and qualify

Rispondi usando accordo parziale:

1. “Online meetings are better than face-to-face meetings.”
2. “Big cities are the best places to live.”
3. “University is necessary for everyone.”

Usa strutture come:

- I agree to some extent, but ...
- That may be true, but ...
- I see your point, however ...

### Drill 3 – Mini debate responses

Dai una risposta di 2–3 frasi:

- Is technology making life better?
- Should people work fewer hours?
- Is travelling essential for personal growth?
- Are exams a fair way to evaluate students?

## ✍️ Writing Task

Scrivi un testo di **140–180 parole** su questo tema:

**“Do you think technology improves people’s lives?”**

Nel testo includi:

- la tua opinione principale
- almeno 2 ragioni
- 1 contrasto o limite
- 2 connettori diversi
- una conclusione chiara

Schema possibile:

1. Introduzione della tua opinione
2. Primo motivo
3. Secondo motivo
4. Punto opposto o limite
5. Conclusione

---

## 🔊 Pronunciation & Fluency

Per sembrare più naturale, lavora sul ritmo delle frasi frequenti:

- I think it’s a good idea.
- I don’t really agree.
- I see your point, but...
- In my opinion, it depends.

Nel parlato reale:

- **I think it’s** spesso si collega in un unico blocco ritmico
- **I agree with you** va pronunciato in modo fluido, non parola per parola
- **I don’t think so** è una chunk molto comune e va automatizzata

Ripeti più volte, con intonazione diversa:

- accordo sincero
- dubbio
- disaccordo educato
- opinione forte

---

## ⚠️ Typical Italian Mistakes

❌ I am agree  
✅ I agree

❌ I think yes  
✅ I think so

❌ I am not agree with this  
✅ I don’t agree with this

❌ In my opinion is wrong  
✅ In my opinion, it is wrong

❌ I think this is better because is easier  
✅ I think this is better because it is easier

❌ I think always the same structure is enough  
✅ It’s better to vary your opinion language in real conversation

---

## 🧩 Upgrade Your Language

Sostituisci il solito **I think** con alternative più ricche quando opportuno:

- I believe...
- From my point of view...
- As far as I’m concerned...
- I’m not convinced that...
- I suppose it depends...

Esempi:

- I believe language learning should be practical.
- From my point of view, motivation matters more than talent.
- I’m not convinced that working longer means working better.

## 🧠 Final Speaking Task

Parla per **3 minuti** su uno di questi temi:

- Is technology making communication better or worse?
- Should people live in big cities or small towns?
- Is remote work better than office work?
- Is travelling a luxury or a necessity?

Obiettivo:

- dare una posizione chiara
- supportarla con 2–3 ragioni
- introdurre almeno 1 contrasto
- usare almeno 4 formule di opinione diverse

---

## 🚀 Final Challenge

Simula un mini dibattito.

1. Dai la tua opinione.
2. Immagina un’opinione opposta.
3. Rispondi in modo educato ma deciso.
4. Concludi con la tua posizione finale.

Pattern utile:

- In my opinion, ...
- I understand that view, but ...
- For example, ...
- So overall, I would say that ...

---

> Fluent speakers don’t only know grammar. They know how to position an idea, support it, and respond to another point of view.
