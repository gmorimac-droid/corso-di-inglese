# Unità 26 – Work and Study

---

## 🎯 Lesson Goals

Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

- presentarti in modo credibile in un contesto professionale o accademico
- descrivere ruolo, responsabilità, routine, risultati e obiettivi con un inglese più maturo
- parlare di esperienza, competenze e sviluppo professionale senza suonare scolastico
- rispondere a domande tipiche di networking, job interview, onboarding o presentazione personale
- evitare gli errori più comuni degli italofoni, soprattutto con **work / job / career**, **experience**, **responsible for**, **for / since**, e costruzioni troppo letterali dall’italiano

Questa unità segna un passaggio importante del livello **B1**: non basta più sapere la regola; bisogna saper **parlare di sé in modo professionale, chiaro e naturale**.

---

## 🧠 Core idea: professional English is built through patterns

Quando parli di lavoro o studio in inglese, non usi una sola struttura. Devi combinare:

- **Present Simple** per ruolo e routine
- **Present Continuous** per progetti temporanei o attività in corso
- **Present Perfect** per esperienza e durata
- **modals and future forms** per obiettivi, intenzioni e sviluppo
- **collocations professionali** per suonare naturale

Confronta:

- I work in an office. *(corretto ma molto basico)*
- I work in operations and I’m mainly responsible for coordinating logistics. *(molto più professionale)*

L’obiettivo della lezione è proprio questo: passare da frasi scolastiche a **professional self-description**.

## 1. The four essential frames for talking about work

### A. Role

- I work as a customer support specialist.
- I’m a junior project manager.
- I’m currently working as an English teacher.

### B. Context

- I work for a medium-sized company.
- I work in the healthcare sector.
- I work with international clients.
- I’m part of the marketing team.

### C. Responsibilities

- I’m responsible for onboarding new staff.
- I deal with supplier communication.
- I manage schedules and internal reporting.
- I support the sales team.

### D. Experience / development

- I’ve been in this role for two years.
- I’ve worked on several international projects.
- I’m currently developing my data analysis skills.
- I’d like to move into a more strategic role.

Questi quattro blocchi sono la base di quasi ogni presentazione professionale efficace.

---

## 2. Work, job, career: do not treat them as synonyms

Molti italofoni usano queste parole come se fossero intercambiabili, ma non lo sono.

### Work

È il concetto generale di lavoro o l’attività lavorativa.

- I work in finance.
- Work can be stressful.
- I’m looking for work.

### Job

È il posto di lavoro, il ruolo o l’impiego specifico.

- I have a full-time job.
- My job involves dealing with customers.
- I’m applying for a new job.

### Career

È il percorso professionale nel lungo periodo.

- I want to build a career in tech.
- She changed career after ten years in law.

### Very common mistakes

❌ My work is teacher  
✅ My job is teaching / I work as a teacher

❌ I changed my work  
✅ I changed my job

❌ I want a good work  
✅ I want a good job

## 3. Talking about responsibilities naturally

Uno degli aspetti che distingue il livello B1 vero da quello scolastico è la capacità di descrivere responsabilità con **collocations naturali**.

### High-frequency professional verbs

- manage
- coordinate
- handle
- monitor
- support
- deliver
- report
- supervise
- train
- organise
- analyse
- solve

### Useful combinations

- manage a team / a project / a budget
- coordinate activities / schedules / communication
- handle customer requests / complaints / invoices
- monitor performance / progress / deadlines
- support clients / colleagues / internal teams
- report to a manager / report on results

### Examples

- I manage a small customer support team.
- I coordinate weekly meetings and deadlines.
- I handle client communication and follow-up.
- I report directly to the operations manager.

Queste combinazioni valgono molto di più di frasi vaghe come **I do many things in my job**.

## 4. Study language that sounds real, not translated

Per parlare dei tuoi studi in modo credibile, è utile andare oltre **I study English**.

### Better ways to talk about studies

- I’m studying economics at university.
- I’m doing a professional course in UX design.
- I’m in my final year.
- I’m working on my dissertation.
- I have a deadline next Friday.
- I’m preparing for an important exam.

### Useful academic verbs

- attend lectures
- submit assignments
- revise for exams
- carry out research
- improve academic writing
- develop practical skills

### Example

- I’m currently studying business administration, and I’m especially interested in organisational behaviour and HR.

Questo tipo di frase è già molto più naturale di una semplice lista di materie.

## 5. Experience: Present Perfect in real professional talk

Per parlare della tua esperienza, il **Present Perfect** è essenziale.

### Typical patterns

- I’ve worked in sales for five years.
- I’ve been with the company since 2021.
- I’ve gained experience in customer relations.
- I’ve worked on several cross-functional projects.
- I’ve recently started a new role.

### For / Since

- **for** + duration → for three years, for six months
- **since** + starting point → since 2022, since January, since I graduated

### Experience vs simple past

- I worked in retail for two years. *(periodo concluso)*
- I’ve worked in retail for two years. *(continuo o rilevante ora)*

Per chi parla italiano, questa distinzione è fondamentale perché il passato prossimo in italiano copre funzioni diverse.

## 6. Talking about strengths, skills and development

A livello B1 serve saper dire non solo cosa fai, ma anche **in cosa sei bravo** e **cosa vuoi migliorare**.

### Useful patterns

- I’m good at organising tasks and priorities.
- One of my strengths is communication.
- I’m developing my presentation skills.
- I’d like to improve my English for professional communication.
- I’m trying to become more confident in meetings.

### Natural examples

- One of my main strengths is solving problems under pressure.
- I’m currently improving my Excel skills because I use data a lot in my job.
- I’d like to move into a role with more responsibility.

## 7. Professional introductions: a simple but strong structure

Quando devi presentarti in inglese, puoi usare questa sequenza:

1. **who you are**
2. **what you do / study**
3. **what your responsibilities are**
4. **what experience you have**
5. **what you’re aiming for**

### Example 1 – Work

> I work as an operations assistant for a logistics company. I’m mainly responsible for coordinating deliveries, updating internal systems and communicating with suppliers. I’ve been in this role for around two years, and during this time I’ve developed strong organisational skills. At the moment, I’m also improving my English because I’d like to work on more international projects.

### Example 2 – Study

> I’m currently studying engineering at university. I’m in my third year, and I’m particularly interested in renewable energy. I also work part-time at the weekends, so I’ve learned how to manage my time quite efficiently. In the future, I’d like to specialise in sustainable systems.

## 🎧 Reading: networking conversation

**At a professional event**

Laura: So, tell me a bit about what you do.

Daniel: Sure. I work for a software company, and I’m part of the customer success team.

Laura: Interesting. What does your role involve?

Daniel: I mainly deal with onboarding new clients, monitoring account performance and helping customers use the platform effectively.

Laura: How long have you been doing that?

Daniel: I’ve been in this position for about eighteen months, but I’ve worked in customer-facing roles for much longer.

Laura: And what are you focusing on at the moment?

Daniel: Right now I’m trying to improve my data analysis skills because I’d like to move into a more strategic role in the future.

### Why this works

Questo dialogo funziona perché mostra:

- introduzione professionale naturale
- domande e risposte realistiche
- dettagli concreti, non vaghi
- esperienza + obiettivo futuro
- lessico professionale riutilizzabile

## 8. Job interview mini-language

### Common questions

- What do you do?
- Can you tell me about your current role?
- What are your main responsibilities?
- What do you enjoy most about your work?
- What skills have you developed?
- Why are you interested in this position?

### Useful answer starters

- At the moment, I work as...
- My main responsibilities include...
- One of the things I enjoy most is...
- Over the last few years, I’ve developed...
- I’m interested in this role because...

### Stronger answer pattern

Instead of:

- I work in an office and I do many tasks.

Try:

- I work in the administrative department, and my main tasks include preparing documents, coordinating appointments and supporting internal communication.

## ✍️ Writing Task

Scrivi un testo di **140–180 parole** su uno di questi temi:

1. **My professional profile**
2. **My studies and career goals**
3. **My current role and what I want to improve**

Il testo deve includere:

- presentazione iniziale
- contesto lavorativo o accademico
- responsabilità o attività principali
- esperienza già acquisita
- competenze sviluppate
- un obiettivo concreto per il futuro

### Model opening

> I currently work as a customer support specialist for an international company. My role involves answering client requests, solving problems and coordinating with other departments when necessary. I’ve been in this position for nearly two years, and during this time I’ve developed stronger communication and time-management skills. At the moment, I’m also improving my English because I’d like to take on more international responsibilities in the future.

## 🔊 Pronunciation & rhythm

Parole da curare:

- **work** → /wɜːrk/
- **world** → /wɜːrld/
- **career** → /kəˈrɪr/
- **responsible** → /rɪˈspɒnsəbəl/
- **colleague** → /ˈkɒliːɡ/
- **experience** → /ɪkˈspɪəriəns/

### Rhythm tip

In inglese professionale, molte frasi diventano più naturali se pronunciate in gruppi di significato:

- I work in sales / and I’m responsible for key accounts.
- I’ve been in this role / for just over two years.
- At the moment / I’m focusing on process improvement.

## ⚠️ Error clinic for Italian speakers

### 1. Work / job confusion

❌ I have a good work  
✅ I have a good job

### 2. Experience as countable noun

❌ I made many experiences  
✅ I had many experiences / I gained a lot of experience

### 3. Responsible + wrong preposition

❌ I’m responsible to manage the team  
✅ I’m responsible for managing the team

### 4. Study prepositions

❌ I study to university  
✅ I study at university

### 5. Literal Italian transfer

❌ My work is very formative  
✅ My job has taught me a lot / It has been a valuable experience

### 6. Duration mistakes

❌ I work here since three years  
✅ I’ve worked here for three years
