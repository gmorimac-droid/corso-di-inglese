# Unità 22 – Present Perfect vs Past Simple

---

## 🎯 Lesson Goals

Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

- distinguere con sicurezza tra **Present Perfect** e **Past Simple** in base al significato comunicativo
- scegliere il tempo corretto in conversazioni reali, email, racconti personali e contesti professionali semplici
- usare in modo accurato **already, just, yet, ever, never, for, since, today, this week, yesterday, last year**

Questa unità è decisiva a livello B1, perché qui non basta conoscere due tempi: bisogna capire **che cosa vuoi comunicare**.

## 🧠 The real question: finished past or present connection?

La differenza non è soltanto grammaticale. È soprattutto una differenza di prospettiva.

### Usa il **Present Perfect** quando il passato è collegato al presente

Il fatto è accaduto prima di adesso, ma conta ancora adesso.

- I’ve lost my wallet. *(so I can’t pay now)*
- We’ve finished the presentation. *(it is ready now)*
- She has changed jobs. *(this is her current situation)*
- I’ve been to Dublin twice. *(life experience)*

### Usa il **Past Simple** quando il fatto appartiene a un passato chiuso

Il tempo è definito, concluso o narrativamente chiuso.

- I lost my wallet yesterday.
- We finished the presentation at 6 p.m.
- She changed jobs in January.
- I went to Dublin in 2022.

👉 In breve:

- **Present Perfect** = past + present relevance
- **Past Simple** = completed past

## 1. The four main contrasts

### A. Experience vs specific occasion

- I’ve been to Berlin. *(experience in life)*
- I went to Berlin last summer. *(specific trip)*

### B. Result now vs finished action then

- She has broken her glasses. *(they are broken now)*
- She broke her glasses last week. *(past event)*

### C. Unfinished time vs finished time

- I’ve had three calls today. *(today is not finished)*
- I had three calls yesterday. *(yesterday is finished)*

- We’ve made good progress this month.
- We made good progress last month.

### D. General question vs detail question

- Have you ever worked with foreign clients?
- Yes, I have.
- When did you start?
- I started in 2021.

Questo passaggio da **general experience** a **specific past detail** è una struttura chiave del parlato naturale.

## 2. Time markers that change everything

### Usually with Present Perfect

- already
- just
- yet
- ever
- never
- so far
- recently
- today
- this week
- this month
- this year
- for
- since

### Usually with Past Simple

- yesterday
- last night
- last week
- last month
- in 2019
- three days ago
- when I was at university
- on Monday

### Watch out: today / this week / this month

Questi marcatori possono richiedere il **Present Perfect** perché il periodo è ancora aperto.

- I’ve had a busy day today.
- She hasn’t replied this week.

Ma se il contesto rende il periodo chiuso, puoi trovare anche il **Past Simple** in un racconto narrativo.

## 3. Italian interference: why this is difficult

In italiano diciamo spesso:

- *Ho visto quel film ieri.*
- *Ho finito due ore fa.*
- *Hai mai visitato Londra?*

In inglese, però, il sistema è diverso.

### English choices

- I **saw** that film yesterday.
- I **finished** two hours ago.
- Have you **ever visited** London?

L’errore nasce perché il passato prossimo italiano copre usi che in inglese si dividono tra **Present Perfect** e **Past Simple**.

---

## 4. for / since and the idea of duration

Quando una situazione è iniziata nel passato e continua ancora adesso, spesso userai il Present Perfect.

- I’ve worked here **for** six years.
- She has lived in Milan **since** 2020.
- We’ve known each other **for** a long time.

### Difference

- **for** = duration
- **since** = starting point

Non dire:

❌ I live here since 2020.  
✅ I’ve lived here since 2020.

## 5. Dialogue in context

**Sara:** Have you sent the client email yet?  
**Luca:** Yes, I’ve just sent it.

**Sara:** Great. Did you attach the updated price list?  
**Luca:** Yes, I did. I added it a few minutes ago.

**Sara:** Perfect. Have you ever dealt with this client before?  
**Luca:** Yes, I have. I worked with them last year on a smaller project.

**Sara:** And how has the collaboration been so far?  
**Luca:** It has been very positive. They’ve already approved two of our proposals.

### Comprehension

1. Which sentence shows a result relevant now?
2. Which sentence refers to a specific finished moment?
3. Why is **so far** compatible with the Present Perfect?
4. Why does Luca say **I worked with them last year**?

## 6. Typical sentence patterns you need

### Present Perfect patterns

- I’ve already finished ...
- I haven’t ... yet.
- Have you ever ...?
- We’ve just received ...
- She has never tried ...
- I’ve worked here for ...
- He has lived here since ...

### Past Simple patterns

- I did it yesterday.
- We met in 2022.
- She called me an hour ago.
- They moved house last month.
- I started learning English when I was ...

## 🗣️ Guided Speaking

### A. Life experience + follow-up

Ask and answer:

- Have you ever taken an important exam in English?
- Have you ever had a difficult interview?
- Have you ever travelled for work or study?
- Have you ever changed your routine because of a project or deadline?

After each **yes** answer, ask:

- When did it happen?
- Where did it happen?
- Who were you with?
- How did it go?

### B. Present result vs past event

Complete both versions:

- I’ve lost ... / I lost ...
- We’ve finished ... / We finished ...
- She’s changed ... / She changed ...

Explain the difference in meaning.

### C. Today vs yesterday

Speak for one minute comparing:

- what you’ve done **today**
- what you did **yesterday**

## ✍️ Writing Task

Scrivi un testo di **160–190 parole** sul tema:

**A recent experience and what it means for you now**

Nel testo usa:

- almeno **4 Present Perfect**
- almeno **4 Past Simple**
- almeno **2 esempi** con **already / just / yet / ever / never / so far**
- almeno **1 esempio** con **for** o **since**

### Suggested structure

1. Introduci l’esperienza in generale
2. Spiega che cosa è successo concretamente e quando
3. Descrivi il risultato o l’effetto nel presente
4. Concludi con una riflessione personale

## 🔊 Pronunciation and rhythm

Confronta il ritmo di queste coppie:

- I’ve finished it. / I finished it yesterday.
- She’s called me. / She called me this morning.
- We’ve seen it before. / We saw it last weekend.

Nel parlato reale, le contrazioni rendono il Present Perfect più rapido e compatto.

### Drill

Ripeti a voce alta:

- I’ve just sent it.
- We haven’t decided yet.
- Have you ever worked abroad?
- I saw him two days ago.
- She’s already done it.

## ⚠️ Error Clinic

### Error 1

❌ I have seen him yesterday.  
✅ I saw him yesterday.

### Error 2

❌ I have finished two hours ago.  
✅ I finished two hours ago.

### Error 3

❌ Did you ever go to New York?  
✅ Have you ever been to New York?

### Error 4

❌ I am here since 2021.  
✅ I’ve been here since 2021.

### Error 5

❌ I went to London many times.  
✅ I’ve been to London many times.

### Error 6

❌ Have you finished the report yesterday?  
✅ Did you finish the report yesterday?
