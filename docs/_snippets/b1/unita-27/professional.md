# Unità 27 – Modals: must, should, have to

---

## 🎯 Lesson Goals

Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

- dare consigli in modo più maturo, preciso e pragmatico
- distinguere chiaramente tra **advice**, **internal obligation**, **external obligation**, **prohibition** e **lack of necessity**
- usare **should**, **must**, **have to**, **mustn’t** e **don’t have to** senza tradurre meccanicamente dall’italiano
- formulare regole, raccomandazioni, linee guida e priorità in contesti reali: lavoro, salute, studio, sicurezza, servizio al cliente
- evitare gli errori tipici degli italofoni, soprattutto con **mustn’t vs don’t have to**, l’uso scorretto di **to** dopo i modali, e la tendenza a usare **must** dove l’inglese reale preferisce **have to**

Questa unità è importante perché porta l’uso dei modali dal livello “regola scolastica” a un livello **operativo e comunicativo**: non si tratta solo di costruire la frase corretta, ma di scegliere la forma che esprime il **grado giusto di forza**.

---

## 🧠 Core idea: modals express stance, not just grammar

Molti studenti imparano:

- **should = consiglio**
- **must = obbligo**
- **have to = dovere**

Ma nel parlato reale il punto non è solo il significato base. Il punto è **chi impone l’obbligo**, **quanto è forte**, e **come suona** la frase.

Confronta:

- You should check the figures again. *(consiglio professionale)*
- You must send this today. *(urgenza / obbligo forte, spesso deciso da chi parla)*
- We have to submit the report by 5 p.m. *(scadenza esterna / requisito pratico)*

La differenza è sottile ma decisiva.

---

## 1. Should: recommendation, expectation, strategic advice

**Should** non significa solo “dovresti”. A livello B1 serve per:

- dare consigli
- fare raccomandazioni professionali
- suggerire una strategia
- indicare ciò che è ragionevole o opportuno
- parlare di aspettative moderate

### Examples

- You should speak to your manager before making a decision.
- We should review the contract more carefully.
- You shouldn’t reply when you’re angry.
- The meeting should end by 4 p.m.

### Why it matters

**Should** è molto utile perché è abbastanza forte da guidare una persona, ma non aggressivo come **must**. In molti contesti professionali è la scelta più naturale.

### Useful professional patterns

- You should probably...
- We should consider...
- I think you should...
- You shouldn’t ignore...
- The best thing to do is...

### Example in context

- If the client is unhappy, you should acknowledge the problem first and then offer a solution.

---

## 2. Must: strong necessity, rule, urgency, speaker authority

**Must** esprime un obbligo forte. Spesso comunica che la regola è percepita come importante, urgente o non negoziabile.

### Examples

- You must wear protective equipment in this area.
- We must act quickly.
- I must remember to call her.
- You must read this before the meeting.

### Important nuance

Nel parlato quotidiano, soprattutto quando l’obbligo viene da circostanze esterne, l’inglese usa spesso **have to** più di **must**. **Must** resta molto comune per:

- istruzioni forti
- safety rules
- formal notices
- decisioni personali forti
- enfasi

### Internal obligation

- I must remember to back up my files.
- I must stop wasting time.

Qui l’obbligo parte dal parlante, non da una regola esterna.

---

## 3. Have to: external obligation, requirement, practical necessity

A livello B1, **have to** è spesso la forma più naturale per parlare di doveri quotidiani, scadenze, regole organizzative e necessità pratiche.

### Examples

- I have to attend a training session tomorrow.
- We have to wear badges in the office.
- She has to take medication twice a day.
- Do you have to work this weekend?

### Why learners overuse must

Molti italofoni traducono “devo” sempre con **must**, ma in inglese reale **have to** è spesso più naturale quando l’obbligo è legato a:

- orari
- procedure
- regolamenti
- scadenze
- condizioni esterne

Confronta:

- I must finish this. *(forte, personale, urgente)*
- I have to finish this by noon. *(scadenza concreta)*

---

## 4. The crucial contrast: mustn’t vs don’t have to

Questa è una delle distinzioni più importanti di tutta l’unità.

### Mustn’t = prohibition

Significa: **non devi farlo perché è vietato / proibito / non consentito**.

- You mustn’t enter this area.
- Staff mustn’t share passwords.
- You mustn’t use your phone during the exam.

### Don’t have to = no necessity

Significa: **non è necessario**.

- You don’t have to come if you’re busy.
- We don’t have to print the document.
- She doesn’t have to wear formal clothes on Fridays.

### Compare

- You mustn’t be late. = È proibito / inaccettabile fare tardi.
- You don’t have to be early. = Non serve arrivare in anticipo.

Questa distinzione va interiorizzata, non solo memorizzata.

---

## 5. Choosing the right modal by communicative force

### A. Gentle advice

- You should take a break.
- You should update the client.

### B. Strong recommendation bordering on obligation

- You really should check the figures again.

### C. Strong obligation / urgency

- You must send this now.

### D. External requirement

- We have to follow company policy.

### E. Prohibition

- You mustn’t disclose confidential information.

### F. Lack of necessity

- You don’t have to answer immediately.

Un buon parlante B1 sceglie il modal in base alla **forza pragmatica**, non solo alla traduzione italiana.

---

## 6. Workplace English: advice, rules and compliance

Questa unità è molto utile in ambito lavorativo.

### Advice to a colleague

- You should summarise the key points more clearly.
- You should confirm the deadline in writing.
- You shouldn’t promise something you can’t deliver.

### Company rules / procedures

- Employees have to complete the form before access is granted.
- Visitors must wear a pass at all times.
- Staff mustn’t leave confidential material unattended.

### Practical necessity

- I have to finish this before the weekly meeting.
- We have to update the database by Friday.

---

## 7. Health, study and everyday life

### Health advice

- You should cut down on sugar.
- You should get more sleep.
- You shouldn’t ignore these symptoms.

### Study advice

- You should revise a little every day.
- You shouldn’t leave everything until the night before the exam.
- You have to practise regularly if you want to improve.

### Everyday obligations

- I have to renew my passport.
- We have to leave early to avoid traffic.
- You mustn’t park in front of the gate.

---

## 8. Form and structure: keep it clean

### Positive

- should + base verb
- must + base verb
- have to + base verb

### Negative

- shouldn’t + base verb
- mustn’t + base verb
- don’t / doesn’t have to + base verb

### Questions

- Should I send it now?
- Must we sign this today?
- Do you have to attend the meeting?

### Past / future note

**Must** non ha tutte le forme verbali in uso quotidiano come **have to**.
Per passato e futuro si usa spesso **had to** / **will have to**.

- I had to cancel the appointment.
- We’ll have to leave earlier next time.

Questo è utile per rendere l’inglese più realistico e meno scolastico.

## 🎧 Reading / Dialogue

**Dialogue 1 – Team leader giving advice**

A: I’m not sure how to handle the client’s complaint.
B: You should reply quickly, but you shouldn’t sound defensive.
A: Should I call them or send an email?
B: You should probably email first and then offer a call.

**Dialogue 2 – Office policy**

A: Do we have to wear badges even on Fridays?
B: Yes, we have to wear them every day.
A: And can we share login details if someone is absent?
B: No, you mustn’t share passwords under any circumstances.

**Dialogue 3 – Personal obligation vs external rule**

A: Why are you still at the office?
B: I have to finish the monthly report.
A: You look exhausted.
B: I know. I should manage my time better.

## ✍️ Writing

Scrivi un testo di **140–180 parole** su uno di questi temi:

1. **Advice for new employees in my company**
2. **Rules and expectations in a professional environment**
3. **What people should do to work or study more effectively**

Cerca di includere:

- almeno 3 frasi con **should / shouldn’t**
- almeno 3 con **have to / don’t have to**
- almeno 2 con **must / mustn’t**
- almeno una distinzione chiara tra **prohibition** e **lack of necessity**

### Model opening

> New employees should ask questions when they are unsure, and they shouldn’t try to solve every problem alone. In our company, staff have to follow security procedures and update their tasks regularly. They mustn’t share confidential information, but they don’t have to work late every day. In my opinion, people should organise their priorities better and communicate clearly when deadlines are unrealistic.

## 🔊 Pronunciation and delivery

### Focus points

- **should** → vocale breve /ʃʊd/
- **mustn’t** → spesso parlato rapidamente, attenzione alla **t**
- **have to** → in connected speech spesso suona come **/hæftə/**
- **should have / must have / have to** non si leggono parola per parola come in italiano

### Practice lines

- You should tell them as soon as possible.
- We have to finish this by five.
- You mustn’t discuss this outside the team.

L’obiettivo non è la perfezione fonetica, ma una delivery **chiara, sicura e fluida**.

## ⚠️ Error Clinic for Italian Speakers

### 1. Adding “to” after modals

❌ You should to call him.  
✅ You should call him.

❌ I must to leave now.  
✅ I must leave now.

### 2. Confusing prohibition with no necessity

❌ You don’t have to smoke here.  
✅ You mustn’t smoke here.

❌ You mustn’t come tomorrow if you’re busy.  
✅ You don’t have to come tomorrow if you’re busy.

### 3. Overusing must for everything

❌ I must go to work at 8 every day. *(possible, but often too strong)*  
✅ I have to go to work at 8 every day.

### 4. Direct translation from Italian “devo”

In inglese non tutti i “devo” sono uguali. Chiediti sempre:

- è un consiglio?
- è una regola?
- è un obbligo esterno?
- è vietato?
- non è necessario?

Questo ti aiuta a scegliere la forma giusta.
