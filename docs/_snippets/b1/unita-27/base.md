# Unità 27 – Modals: must, should, have to

---

## 🎯 Obiettivo

Alla fine di questa unità saprai:

- dare consigli in modo naturale con **should / shouldn’t**
- esprimere obblighi, necessità e regole con **must**, **mustn’t** e **have to**
- distinguere tra un obbligo personale e una regola esterna
- parlare di ciò che è obbligatorio, consigliato, vietato o non necessario
- evitare gli errori più comuni con i verbi modali

---

## 🧠 Grammar in context

I verbi modali aiutano a esprimere **atteggiamento, obbligo, consiglio, divieto o necessità**. In questa unità ci concentriamo su tre strutture molto utili nel parlato reale:

- **should** → consiglio
- **must** → obbligo forte / regola percepita come importante
- **have to** → necessità pratica o regola esterna

### Forma base

- You **should** rest.
- You **must** wear a seatbelt.
- I **have to** leave early tomorrow.

Dopo **should** e **must** usiamo sempre il **verbo base**, senza **to**.

---

## 1. Should = advice

Usiamo **should** per dire che qualcosa è una buona idea, un suggerimento o un consiglio.

### Esempi

- You should drink more water.
- You should talk to your manager.
- You should go to bed earlier.
- We should leave now.

### Negativa

- You shouldn’t stay up so late.
- He shouldn’t worry too much.

### Domande

- Should I call her?
- What should we do?

---

## 2. Must = strong obligation or rule

Usiamo **must** quando l’obbligo è sentito come forte, importante o categorico.

### Esempi

- You must wear protective equipment.
- I must finish this report today.
- You must stop when the light is red.

### Negativa: mustn’t

**Mustn’t** significa **proibizione**.

- You mustn’t smoke here.
- Students mustn’t use their phones during the exam.

---

## 3. Have to = external obligation / practical necessity

Usiamo **have to** quando c’è una regola esterna, una necessità concreta o un obbligo pratico.

### Esempi

- I have to work on Saturday.
- We have to wear ID badges at the office.
- She has to take two buses to get to work.

### Negativa

- I don’t have to work tomorrow.
- You don’t have to pay now.

Attenzione: **don’t have to** non significa proibizione. Significa **non è necessario**.

---

## 4. Key difference

| Struttura | Significato principale | Esempio |
|---|---|---|
| **should** | consiglio | You should rest. |
| **must** | obbligo forte / regola importante | You must wear a helmet. |
| **have to** | necessità pratica / regola esterna | I have to be at work at 8. |
| **mustn’t** | proibizione | You mustn’t park here. |
| **don’t have to** | non necessario | You don’t have to come early. |

## 🎧 Reading / Dialogue

**Dialogue 1 – Advice**

A: I’m really tired these days.
B: You should take a short break.
A: I know. I also sleep very little.
B: Then you should go to bed earlier.

**Dialogue 2 – Rules at work**

A: Do we need our badges every day?
B: Yes, we have to wear them at all times.
A: Can we eat in the meeting room?
B: No, we mustn’t eat there.

**Dialogue 3 – Practical necessity**

A: Why are you leaving so early?
B: I have to catch a train.
A: Do you have to go now?
B: Yes, otherwise I’ll miss it.

## ✍️ Writing

Scrivi un testo di **90–110 parole** su uno di questi temi:

1. **Advice for a healthy routine**
2. **Rules in my workplace / school**
3. **Things I have to do during the week**

Cerca di includere:

- almeno 2 frasi con **should**
- almeno 2 frasi con **have to**
- almeno 1 regola con **must** o **mustn’t**

### Example

> During the week I have to wake up early and go to work by bus. I also have to prepare reports for my team. I think I should sleep more and do more exercise because I often feel tired. At work, we must wear our ID badges, and we mustn’t leave confidential documents on our desks. I don’t have to work on Sundays, so I usually relax and spend time with my family.

## 🔊 Pronunciation

Attenzione a queste forme:

- **should** → /ʃʊd/
- **must** → /mʌst/
- **have to** → spesso nel parlato suona vicino a **/hæftə/**
- **shouldn’t** → la **l** non si sente

Ripeti:

- You should call her.
- We have to leave now.
- You mustn’t park here.

---

## ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

❌ You should to study more.  
✅ You should study more.

❌ I must to go now.  
✅ I must go now.

❌ I no have to work tomorrow.  
✅ I don’t have to work tomorrow.

❌ You don’t must do that.  
✅ You mustn’t do that. / You don’t have to do that. *(dipende dal significato)*

Ricorda:

- dopo **should** e **must** → verbo base
- **mustn’t** = vietato
- **don’t have to** = non necessario

---

## 🧠 Pattern fondamentali

- Subject + **should** + base verb
- Subject + **must** + base verb
- Subject + **have to / has to** + base verb
- Subject + **don’t / doesn’t have to** + base verb

### Examples

- You should speak to him.
- She must be here by nine.
- I have to finish this task.
- He doesn’t have to come tomorrow.
