# Unità 21 – Present Perfect (Real Use)

---

## 🎯 Obiettivo

Alla fine di questa unità saprai:

- usare il **Present Perfect** in modo semplice ma corretto
- collegare un’azione passata al presente
- parlare di esperienze, risultati e cambiamenti
- distinguere tra **Present Perfect** e **Past Simple** nei casi più comuni

---

## 🧠 Grammar in context

### Struttura

👉 **subject + have / has + past participle**

- I have worked
- She has studied
- They have travelled

Forme brevi molto comuni:

- I’ve finished
- She’s gone out
- We’ve seen it

---

## 🔑 Usi principali

### 1. Esperienze di vita

Usa il Present Perfect quando parli di un’esperienza e **non dici quando**.

- I have been to Spain.
- She has eaten sushi.
- We have seen that film.

### 2. Risultati nel presente

L’azione è passata, ma il risultato è importante **adesso**.

- I have lost my keys.
- He has broken his glasses.
- We have finished our homework.

### 3. Cambiamenti nel tempo

- My English has improved.
- The city has changed a lot.
- She has become more confident.

---

## ⚖️ Present Perfect vs Past Simple

| Present Perfect | Past Simple |
|---|---|
| esperienza o risultato | azione finita nel passato |
| non dici quando | dici quando |
| legame con il presente | tempo passato concluso |

**Examples**

- I have been to Rome. ✅
- I went to Rome last year. ✅
- I have been to Rome last year. ❌

---

## 📚 Core vocabulary

### Past participles frequenti

- be → been
- go → gone
- do → done
- eat → eaten
- see → seen
- write → written
- make → made
- take → taken
- speak → spoken
- buy → bought

### Signal words

- **already** = già
- **just** = appena
- **yet** = ancora / già (in domande e negative)
- **ever** = mai
- **never** = mai

**Examples**

- I have already finished.
- She has just arrived.
- We haven’t started yet.
- Have you ever tried Indian food?
- I have never flown business class.

---

## 🎧 Reading / Dialogue

**A:** Have you finished your report yet?
**B:** Yes, I’ve just sent it.

**A:** Have you ever worked with international clients?
**B:** Yes, I have. I’ve worked with clients from Germany and Spain.

**A:** Really? When did you start?
**B:** I started two years ago.

---

## 🗣️ Speaking

Ripeti:

- I’ve just finished.
- We haven’t decided yet.
- She has already called.
- Have you ever tried it?

Ora completa con idee tue:

- I have already ...
- I have just ...
- I haven’t ... yet.
- I have never ...

Fai domande:

- Have you ever travelled alone?
- Have you finished your work yet?
- Have you ever met a famous person?

---

## ✍️ Writing

Scrivi 8 frasi usando il Present Perfect.

Usa almeno queste parole:

- already
- just
- yet
- ever
- never

Esempio:

> I’ve already done my homework.  
> I haven’t called my friend yet.  
> Have you ever visited London?

---

## 🔊 Pronunciation

Nel parlato naturale spesso si usano le contrazioni:

- I have → **I’ve**
- you have → **you’ve**
- she has → **she’s**
- we have → **we’ve**

Attenzione anche a:

- finished → /ˈfɪnɪʃt/
- worked → /wɜːrkt/
- lived → /lɪvd/

---

## ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

❌ I have went to London.  
✅ I have gone to London.

❌ I have done it yesterday.  
✅ I did it yesterday.

❌ I am finished.  
✅ I have finished.

❌ Have you ever been in New York?  
✅ Have you ever been to New York?

## ✅ Review & Practice

Completa con il participio corretto:

1. I have ___ (eat) lunch.
2. She has ___ (go) home.
3. We have ___ (see) that film.
4. He hasn’t ___ (finish) yet.
5. They have ___ (write) the email.

Scegli il tempo corretto:

1. I have seen / saw him yesterday.
2. Have you ever been / did you go to Ireland?
3. She has just arrived / just arrived five minutes ago.
4. We didn’t finish / haven’t finished yet.
