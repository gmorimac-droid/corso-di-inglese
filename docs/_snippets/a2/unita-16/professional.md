## 🧭 Language focus – food, quantity and real-life interaction

### 1. Countable vs uncountable: non è solo grammatica, è significato

I **countable nouns** si possono contare come unità separate:

- an apple
- two bananas
- three bottles
- a sandwich
- several customers

Gli **uncountable nouns** indicano spesso sostanze, cibi “non separati”, bevande o concetti visti come massa:

- water
- milk
- bread
- rice
- cheese
- sugar
- coffee

Questa distinzione influenza:

- articoli (**a / an**)
- plurale
- scelta tra **much** e **many**
- scelta tra **How much** e **How many**

Confronta:

- an apple / two apples
- some rice / some cheese
- many apples
- much rice

👉 In contesto reale, il problema non è solo sapere la regola, ma **vedere il nome come unità o come sostanza**.

---

### 2. Quando un nome uncountable può diventare countable

In contesti quotidiani, alcuni uncountable nouns diventano countable quando parliamo di **porzioni**, **contenitori** o **tipi**.

- two coffees = two cups of coffee
- a water = molto colloquiale in alcuni contesti, ma meglio per studenti: **a bottle of water**
- three cheeses = three types of cheese

Per un corso ben controllato, preferiamo forme più chiare:

- a cup of coffee
- a glass of water
- a bottle of milk
- a loaf of bread
- a piece of cake

---

### 3. Some, any, much, many, a lot of

#### Some

Usato soprattutto in frasi affermative e offerte/richieste educate.

- We need some eggs.
- I'd like some water.
- Would you like some coffee?

#### Any

Usato soprattutto in domande e negazioni.

- Do you have any fresh bread?
- We don't have any rice left.
- Is there any milk in the fridge?

#### Much / many

- **many** + countable plural
- **much** + uncountable

- How many oranges do we need?
- How much pasta is there?

Nel parlato quotidiano, in frasi affermative si preferisce spesso **a lot of**:

- We bought a lot of vegetables.
- She drinks a lot of coffee.

👉 **much** in frase affermativa semplice spesso suona più formale o meno naturale:

- We drink much coffee. ❌/strano
- We drink a lot of coffee. ✅

---

### 4. Containers and quantity expressions

Queste strutture rendono il linguaggio molto più naturale:

- a bottle of water
- a carton of milk
- a loaf of bread
- a packet of rice
- a kilo of apples
- a jar of jam
- a cup of tea
- a slice of pizza
- a piece of cake

Esempi:

- We need a carton of milk and a loaf of bread.
- Can I have a slice of cake?
- I'd like two bottles of water.

---

### 5. Functional language: ordering and shopping politely

In situazioni reali, la grammatica deve andare insieme alla **cortesia**.

Formule molto utili:

- I'd like ...
- Can I have ... , please?
- Could I get ...?
- Do you have any ...?
- How much is this?
- How much are these?
- That's all, thank you.
- Can I pay by card?
- Can I have the bill, please?

Confronta:

- I want a coffee. → corretto ma diretto
- I'd like a coffee, please. → più naturale e cortese
- Can I have a coffee, please? → molto comune

## 🎧 Reading – two realistic situations

### Situation 1 – supermarket

> We need to do a quick shop before dinner. There isn't any pasta at home and we only have a little rice left. Let's get some vegetables, a loaf of bread, two bottles of water and a carton of milk. We should also buy some fruit because there isn't much in the fridge.

### Situation 2 – restaurant

> Good evening. We'd like a table for two, please. Could we also have the menu? For starters, we'll have soup and a salad. For the main course, I'd like the grilled chicken with vegetables, and my friend would like the pasta. Can we also get a bottle of still water?

---

## 🔍 Notice the language

Osserva queste scelte:

- **there isn't any pasta** → negazione con uncountable noun
- **a little rice** → piccola quantità positiva
- **some vegetables** → quantità non specificata, ma presente
- **there isn't much in the fridge** → quantità ridotta
- **we'd like** → forma più morbida e naturale di **we want**

## 🗣️ Speaking drills

### Drill 1 – Say it naturally

Trasforma le idee in inglese naturale:

1. Vorrei un caffè e una fetta di torta.
2. Non abbiamo latte a casa.
3. Quante uova ci servono?
4. Posso pagare con la carta?
5. Abbiamo bisogno di una bottiglia d'acqua.

### Drill 2 – Expand the sentence

Parti da una frase breve e rendila più naturale.

- I want pasta.  
  → I'd like some pasta, please.

- We need milk.  
  → We need a carton of milk.

- Buy apples.  
  → Could you buy a kilo of apples?

### Drill 3 – Mini role-play

**At the market**

A: Can I help you?
B: Yes, I'd like ...
A: How much / how many ...?
B: ... please.
A: Anything else?
B: That's all, thank you.

## 🔊 Pronunciation and fluency

Parole da curare:

- vegetables → /ˈvedʒtəblz/
- chocolate → /ˈtʃɒklət/
- water → /ˈwɔːtə(r)/
- menu → /ˈmenjuː/
- sugar → /ˈʃʊɡə(r)/
- receipt → /rɪˈsiːt/

### Rhythm tip

Nelle richieste cortesi, il ritmo è importante:

- **I'd like** a coffee, please.
- **Can I have** the bill, please?
- **Could we get** some water?

Dire bene il ritmo rende l'inglese più naturale anche se il livello grammaticale è ancora intermedio basso.

## ⚠️ Error clinic – Italian learners

### Errore 1: articolo con uncountable nouns

❌ a bread  
✅ some bread / a loaf of bread

❌ a rice  
✅ some rice / a packet of rice

❌ a cheese  
✅ some cheese / a piece of cheese

### Errore 2: much / many

❌ much apples  
✅ many apples

❌ many water  
✅ much water / a lot of water

### Errore 3: richiesta troppo diretta

❌ I want a table.  
✅ I'd like a table, please.

❌ Give me water.  
✅ Can I have some water, please?

### Errore 4: quantità senza contenitore

❌ two breads  
✅ two loaves of bread / two pieces of bread

❌ three waters  
✅ three bottles of water
