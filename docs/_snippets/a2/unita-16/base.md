## 🧠 Grammar in context

### Countable nouns

I nomi **countable** si possono contare.

- an apple
- two apples
- three sandwiches
- a bottle
- four eggs

Possiamo usare:

- **a / an**
- numeri
- **many**

Esempi:

- I want **an apple**.
- We need **three tomatoes**.
- There are **many shops** in this street.

---

### Uncountable nouns

I nomi **uncountable** non si contano normalmente uno per uno.

- water
- milk
- bread
- rice
- sugar
- coffee
- cheese

Di solito non usiamo **a / an**.

- I want **water**.
- We need **some rice**.
- There isn't **any milk**.

---

### Some / Any

Usiamo **some** soprattutto nelle frasi affermative.

- I need **some bread**.
- She bought **some apples**.

Usiamo **any** soprattutto nelle frasi negative e interrogative.

- We don't have **any coffee**.
- Do you have **any eggs**?

---

### A lot of

**A lot of** si usa sia con countable sia con uncountable.

- We bought **a lot of fruit**.
- There are **a lot of customers** today.

---

### Useful question patterns

- Can I have a coffee, please?
- How much is it?
- How many apples do you want?
- Do you have any water?
- Would you like anything else?

## 🎧 Listening / Reading

**Dialogue 1 – at a café**

A: Good afternoon. What would you like?
B: Can I have a sandwich and a bottle of water, please?
A: Of course. Anything else?
B: Yes, a coffee, please.
A: Sure. That's £8.

**Dialogue 2 – at the supermarket**

A: Do we have any milk at home?
B: No, we don't. We also need some bread and eggs.
A: How many eggs do we need?
B: A dozen, please.

## ✍️ Writing

Scrivi 8–10 frasi su una di queste situazioni:

1. la tua spesa settimanale
2. un ordine al bar o al ristorante
3. cosa c'è e cosa manca nella tua cucina

Puoi usare questo modello:

> I usually buy some fruit, bread and milk.  
> I don't buy much meat.  
> At the café, I usually order a coffee and a sandwich.  
> At home, we don't have any rice today.

## 🔊 Pronunciation

Attenzione a queste parole:

- bread → /bred/
- fruit → /fruːt/
- juice → /dʒuːs/
- rice → /raɪs/
- sugar → /ˈʃʊɡə(r)/

👉 Non leggere tutto come in italiano. In inglese molte parole comuni hanno suoni molto diversi dalla grafia italiana.

## ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

❌ I want **a bread**  
✅ I want **some bread** / I want **bread**

❌ We need **a milk**  
✅ We need **some milk**

❌ How much apples?  
✅ **How many** apples?

❌ I buyed some food  
✅ I **bought** some food

❌ I want a water  
✅ I want **some water** / Can I have **a bottle of water**?

## 🧠 Pattern fondamentali

- **some + plural / uncountable**
- **any + plural / uncountable**
- **How much + uncountable?**
- **How many + plural countable?**
- **Can I have ... , please?**
- **I'd like ...**

Esempi:

- I'd like some soup.
- Do you have any cheese?
- How many bananas do you want?
- How much rice do we need?
