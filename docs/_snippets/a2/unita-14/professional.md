## 🧭 Language focus – talking about travel naturally

### 1. Raccontare fatti conclusi

Il **Past Simple** resta il tempo centrale per descrivere una vacanza finita.

- I went to Porto for a long weekend.
- We stayed in a small guesthouse near the river.
- I visited two museums and tried local food.
- We came back on Sunday evening.

### 2. Aggiungere ordine e coerenza

Per raccontare bene non basta una lista di frasi: serve una progressione.

- first
- then
- after that
- later
- in the evening
- on the last day
- finally

Confronta:

**Lista debole:**

> I went to Prague. I stayed in a hotel. I visited the centre. I ate a lot.

**Versione migliore:**

> First, I flew to Prague and checked into a small hotel near the centre. Then I walked around the Old Town and visited a museum. In the evening I tried local food in a traditional restaurant. On the last day I bought a few souvenirs and flew back home.

👉 La seconda versione non è più “difficile” grammaticalmente, ma è molto più professionale e naturale.

## 🧠 Travel vocabulary with real distinctions

### trip / travel / journey / holiday

- **trip** = viaggio singolo, spesso breve o definito
  - It was a business trip.
  - We went on a school trip.

- **travel** = viaggiare in generale, come attività o concetto
  - I love travel.
  - We travelled around Portugal.

- **journey** = spostamento da un luogo a un altro, attenzione al tragitto
  - The train journey was long.
  - We had a comfortable journey.

- **holiday** = vacanza
  - We went on holiday in June.
  - It was a relaxing holiday.

👉 In molti contesti italiani “viaggio” copre tutto, ma in inglese la scelta della parola cambia il significato.

## 🎧 Reading – A short city break

Leggi il testo.

> In October I went to Budapest with a friend for four days. We travelled by plane and stayed in a small hotel not far from the centre. On the first day we walked around the city and took a boat trip on the Danube. The weather was cold but sunny, so we spent a lot of time outdoors. On the second day we visited a museum and tried some local dishes in a traditional restaurant. Unfortunately, on the last day our flight was delayed, so we waited at the airport for three hours. Even so, we really enjoyed the trip and decided to go back one day.

### Comprensione

1. Where did the speakers go?
2. How long did they stay there?
3. Where did they stay?
4. What did they do on the first day?
5. What problem did they have on the last day?
6. Which sentences express opinion rather than only fact?

### Language focus

Il testo funziona bene perché contiene:

- **fatti**: went, travelled, stayed, visited
- **sequenza**: on the first day, on the second day, on the last day
- **valutazione**: the weather was cold but sunny, we really enjoyed the trip
- **piccolo imprevisto**: our flight was delayed

Questi elementi trasformano una lista di azioni in un vero racconto.

## 🗣️ Guided Speaking

### A. Short answers → full answers

Rispondi prima in modo breve, poi amplia.

1. Where did you go on your last holiday?
2. Who did you go with?
3. How did you travel?
4. Where did you stay?
5. What did you do during the day?
6. Did anything go wrong?
7. Would you like to go back?

Esempio:

- I went to Naples.  
- I went to Naples with two friends for a long weekend, and we stayed near the seafront.

### B. Build a travel story

Usa questa scaletta:

- destination
- transport
- accommodation
- activities
- food
- one positive point
- one problem or surprise
- final opinion

### C. 90-second task

Parla per **60–90 secondi** su uno di questi temi:

- My last holiday
- A trip I really enjoyed
- A trip that had a problem

## ✍️ Writing

### Task 1 – Guided paragraph

Scrivi un testo di **120–150 parole** su una vacanza passata.

Devi includere:

- almeno **5 verbi al Past Simple**
- almeno **4 parole del travel vocabulary**
- almeno **3 connettivi temporali**
- almeno **1 opinione personale**
- almeno **1 piccolo problema o imprevisto**

### Task 2 – Upgrade your writing

Controlla se il tuo testo risponde a queste domande:

- Il lettore capisce **dove** sei andato e **quando**?
- Hai spiegato **come** hai viaggiato?
- Hai incluso **due o tre attività** specifiche?
- Hai dato una valutazione personale, non solo fatti?
- Hai usato almeno un dettaglio concreto su hotel, cibo, luogo o trasporto?

### Final -ed

- stayed → /d/

- visited → /ɪd/

### Word stress utile

- **HO**tel
- **AIR**port
- **MU**seum
- souve**NIR**

### Rhythm tip

Le frasi di viaggio suonano più naturali se raggruppi le informazioni:

- We **stayed in a small hotel** near the centre.
- We **travelled by train** and arrived in the afternoon.
- On the **last day**, we bought a few souvenirs.

## ⚠️ Error clinic – errori tipici degli italiani

❌ I made a travel to London.  
✅ I took a trip to London. / I travelled to London.

❌ We did a holiday in Greece.  
✅ We went on holiday to Greece.

❌ I stayed in an hotel.  
✅ I stayed in a hotel.

❌ We didn't visited the museum.  
✅ We didn't visit the museum.

❌ Did you enjoyed the trip?  
✅ Did you enjoy the trip?

❌ I and my friend went to Paris.  
✅ My friend and I went to Paris.

👉 A questo livello la correttezza grammaticale conta, ma conta anche scegliere le espressioni davvero idiomatiche.
