## 🧭 Language focus – quantifiers in real English

### 1. Some e any: la regola base è utile, ma non basta

La regola iniziale è questa:

- **some** → soprattutto in frasi affermative
- **any** → soprattutto in negative e domande

Esempi base:

- We have **some** bread.
- We don't have **any** bread.
- Do we have **any** bread?

Questa regola è corretta, ma in inglese reale **some** compare anche in richieste e offerte quando il parlante pensa che la risposta sarà positiva o vuole essere cortese.

- Would you like **some** tea?
- Can I have **some** water, please?
- Could you give me **some** information?

👉 Quindi non pensare a **some** solo come “affermativa”: pensa anche a **quantità presente o desiderata in modo concreto e cortese**.

---

### 2. Much e many: corretto grammaticalmente, ma attenzione alla naturalezza

La regola formale:

- **many** + countable plural
- **much** + uncountable

Esempi:

- many people
- many emails
- much time
- much money

Ma nel parlato quotidiano, nelle frasi affermative, spesso preferiamo **a lot of**.

Confronta:

- I have **many friends** in London. ✅
- I have **a lot of friends** in London. ✅ più naturale nel parlato
- We drink **much coffee**. ⚠️ possibile, ma poco naturale
- We drink **a lot of coffee**. ✅ molto più naturale

👉 Usa **much** soprattutto:

- in frasi negative: We don't have much time.
- in domande: How much money do you need?
- in contesti più formali o scritti

---

### 3. Countable vs uncountable: la scelta del quantifier dipende dal nome

#### Countable nouns

Si possono contare singolarmente:

- a chair / two chairs
- a student / three students
- a sandwich / four sandwiches
- an idea / many ideas

Con questi usiamo:

- **many**
- **a few**
- numeri
- **How many...?**

#### Uncountable nouns

Non si contano normalmente come unità separate:

- water
- milk
- rice
- information
- advice
- furniture
- luggage
- homework

Con questi usiamo:

- **much**
- **a little**
- **some**
- **How much...?**

👉 Errori tipici da evitare:

- informations ❌ → information ✅
- advices ❌ → advice ✅
- furnitures ❌ → furniture ✅
- homeworks ❌ → homework ✅

### 4. Some, any, much, many in four common situations

#### At home

- We have some pasta, but we don't have any rice.
- There isn't much milk left.
- How many eggs are in the fridge?

#### At the supermarket

- Do we need any fruit?
- We need some tomatoes and a lot of water.
- How much cheese should I buy?

#### At work

- I don't have much time this morning.
- We don't have many people available today.
- Do you have any updates on the project?

#### In study situations

- I don't have much homework tonight.
- She has a lot of notes, but not many books.
- Do you have any questions about the exercise?

## 🎧 Reading – realistic contexts

### Text 1 – before a dinner party

> We need to check what we have at home before people arrive. There is some bread and there are some bottles of water, but there isn't much juice left. We don't have any ice cream, and there aren't many snacks in the cupboard. How many guests are coming? If there are ten people, we need a lot of food.

### Text 2 – at work

> I'm quite busy this week. I don't have much free time, and I still have a lot of work to finish. There are many emails in my inbox, but there isn't much useful information in some of them. Do you have any time this afternoon to help me?

---

## 🔍 Notice the language

Osserva queste scelte:

- **there is some bread / there are some bottles** → some con uncountable e con plural countable
- **there isn't much juice** → much con uncountable in negativa
- **there aren't many snacks** → many con plural countable in negativa
- **a lot of food / a lot of work** → molto naturale nel parlato
- **any time this afternoon** → any in domanda su disponibilità

## 🗣️ Speaking drills

### Drill 1 – Choose and justify

Completa e spiega la scelta:

1. We don't have ___ chairs for everyone.  
2. Is there ___ milk in the fridge?  
3. I don't have ___ time today.  
4. There are ___ good restaurants in this area.  
5. Could I have ___ information about the course?

### Drill 2 – Make it more natural

Rendi più naturale la frase.

- We have much work today.  
  → We have **a lot of work** today.

- I want some informations.  
  → I need **some information**.

- How much students are there?  
  → **How many students** are there?

- I don't have some money.  
  → I don't have **any money**.

### Drill 3 – Short role-plays

**Role-play A – office**

A: Do you have any time after lunch?  
B: I don't have much time, but I can stay for twenty minutes.

**Role-play B – shopping**

A: How many bottles of water do we need?  
B: Let's get four. We don't have any at home.

**Role-play C – study**

A: Do you have any questions about the homework?  
B: Yes, I have a few questions, but not many.

## 🔊 Pronunciation

Attenzione a queste parole:

- some → /sʌm/
- much → /mʌtʃ/
- many → /ˈmeni/
- money → /ˈmʌni/
- any → /ˈeni/

👉 Allenati a distinguere:

- **many** /ˈmeni/
- **money** /ˈmʌni/

Sono parole molto comuni e spesso gli italiani le confondono nella pronuncia.

## ⚠️ Error clinic – errori tipici degli italiani

❌ I don't have some time  
✅ I don't have any time

❌ There are much people here  
✅ There are many people here

❌ How much chairs do we need?  
✅ How many chairs do we need?

❌ I have many homeworks  
✅ I have a lot of homework / I have a lot of assignments

❌ Can you give me some informations?  
✅ Can you give me some information?

❌ We haven't many milk  
✅ We don't have much milk
