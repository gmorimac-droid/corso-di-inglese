## 🎯 Obiettivo

Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

- confrontare due persone, oggetti o luoghi usando il **comparative**
- dire qual è il massimo o il minimo usando il **superlative**
- distinguere tra aggettivi brevi e lunghi
- usare alcune forme irregolari molto comuni come **better** e **worse**
- esprimere preferenze semplici in frasi quotidiane

---

## 🧠 Grammar in context

### Comparatives: confronto tra due cose

Usiamo il **comparative** per confrontare due elementi.

#### Aggettivi brevi

Struttura:

**adjective + -er + than**

- Rome is bigger than Milan.
- This bag is cheaper than that one.
- My brother is taller than me.

#### Aggettivi lunghi

Con molti aggettivi di due o più sillabe usiamo **more + adjective + than**.

- This exercise is more difficult than the last one.
- London is more expensive than Lisbon.
- Her job is more interesting than mine.

---

### Superlatives: il massimo in un gruppo

Usiamo il **superlative** per dire che una cosa è al top o al bottom di un gruppo.

#### Aggettivi brevi

Struttura:

**the + adjective + -est**

- Rome is the biggest city in Italy.
- This is the cheapest option.
- He is the tallest student in the class.

#### Aggettivi lunghi

Struttura:

**the most + adjective**

- This is the most interesting lesson.
- It is the most expensive hotel in the area.
- She is the most organised person in the team.

---

### Forme irregolari comuni

Alcuni aggettivi non seguono la regola standard.

- good → better → the best
- bad → worse → the worst
- far → farther / further → the farthest / the furthest

Esempi:

- This restaurant is better than that one.
- Today is worse than yesterday.
- Which station is farther from here?

## 🎧 Listening / Reading

**Text:**

> My town is smaller than Rome, but it is quieter and cleaner. The public transport is worse, but the streets are less crowded. There are two hotels in the centre. The Grand Hotel is more expensive than the City Hotel, but it is also more comfortable. In my opinion, spring is the best season to visit because the weather is warmer and the town is more beautiful.

### Comprensione

1. Is the town bigger or smaller than Rome?
2. What is worse in the town?
3. Which hotel is more expensive?
4. Which hotel is more comfortable?
5. Which season is the best to visit?

## 🔊 Pronunciation

Attenzione alla pronuncia di alcune forme:

- bigger → /ˈbɪɡər/
- cheaper → /ˈtʃiːpər/
- easier → /ˈiːziər/
- best → /best/
- worst → /wɜːst/

👉 Nella lingua parlata, la terminazione **-er** deve essere chiara ma naturale.

---

## ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

❌ more bigger  
✅ bigger

❌ more cheap  
✅ cheaper

❌ the more expensive hotel  
✅ the most expensive hotel

❌ Milan is more small than Rome.  
✅ Milan is smaller than Rome.

❌ This is the better restaurant in town.  
✅ This is the best restaurant in town.

👉 Con gli aggettivi corti in genere non usiamo **more**.
