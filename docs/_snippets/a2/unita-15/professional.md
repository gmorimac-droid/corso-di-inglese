## 🧭 Language focus – how comparison really works

### 1. Comparative: confronto tra due elementi

Il **comparative** serve a mettere in relazione due persone, oggetti, luoghi o situazioni.

- Rome is bigger than Bologna.
- This laptop is lighter than my old one.
- A hotel is usually more expensive than a hostel.
- Working from home is sometimes easier than commuting every day.

Il punto importante non è solo la forma, ma il **criterio del confronto**:

- size → bigger / smaller
- price → cheaper / more expensive
- quality → better / worse
- comfort → more comfortable
- speed → faster / slower

👉 Un buon confronto specifica sempre **che cosa** stai comparando e spesso **in base a quale criterio**.

---

### 2. Superlative: il massimo all'interno di un gruppo

Il **superlative** identifica un elemento come il più alto, il più basso, il migliore o il peggiore di un gruppo.

- This is the cheapest restaurant in the area.
- It was the most interesting talk of the day.
- She is the best player on the team.
- Winter is the worst season for me.

Spesso il superlativo è seguito da:

- **in** + place / group
  - the best café **in** town
- **of** + plural / period / set
  - the most useful lesson **of** the course

## 🧠 Formazione: short adjectives, long adjectives, irregular forms

### Short adjectives

Di solito usano:

- **-er than**
- **the -est**

Esempi:

- tall → taller → the tallest
- fast → faster → the fastest
- cheap → cheaper → the cheapest
- nice → nicer → the nicest

### Spelling changes to remember

#### Final -e

- nice → nicer → the nicest
- large → larger → the largest

#### Consonant + y → ier / iest

- easy → easier → the easiest
- busy → busier → the busiest
- happy → happier → the happiest

#### Double consonant in some short words

- big → bigger → the biggest
- hot → hotter → the hottest
- thin → thinner → the thinnest

### Long adjectives

Molti aggettivi più lunghi usano:

- **more + adjective + than**
- **the most + adjective**

Esempi:

- more useful
- more reliable
- more comfortable
- the most interesting
- the most convenient

### Irregular forms

- good → better → the best
- bad → worse → the worst
- far → farther / further → the farthest / the furthest

Esempi:

- This option is better than the other one.
- The weather today is worse than yesterday.
- Which stop is further from the station?

## 🔍 Beyond the basics: very useful comparison patterns

### 1. much / a lot / far + comparative

Usiamo queste parole per rafforzare il confronto.

- This exercise is **much easier** than the previous one.
- Travelling by train is **a lot more relaxing** than driving.
- My new phone is **far better** than my old one.

### 2. a bit / a little / slightly + comparative

Per un confronto più moderato.

- Today is **a bit colder** than yesterday.
- This neighbourhood is **slightly quieter** at night.
- The second option is **a little more expensive**.

### 3. as ... as

Per dire che due cose sono uguali o quasi uguali sotto un certo aspetto.

- My sister is as tall as me.
- This café is as expensive as the one near the station.
- The exam wasn't as difficult as I expected.

Forme utili:

- as good as
- as fast as
- not as easy as
- not as crowded as

👉 A2 richiede già di saper esprimere non solo “più di”, ma anche “uguale a” e “non così ... come”.

## 🎧 Reading – Choosing the best place to stay

Leggi il testo.

> We looked at three places for our weekend in Valencia. The first hotel was the cheapest, but it was also the farthest from the centre. The second one was smaller but much more comfortable, and the staff seemed friendlier. The third option was the most modern and the closest to the station, but it was also the most expensive. In the end, we chose the second hotel because it wasn't as cheap as the first one, but it was far better in terms of comfort and location. Sometimes the cheapest option isn't the best one.

### Comprensione

1. Which hotel was the cheapest?
2. Which one was the farthest from the centre?
3. Which hotel was the most modern?
4. Why did they choose the second hotel?
5. Find one sentence with **not as ... as**.
6. Find one sentence where price and quality are contrasted.

### Language focus

Nel testo trovi:

- **superlatives**: the cheapest, the farthest, the most modern, the most expensive
- **moderated comparison**: smaller but much more comfortable
- **equal/unequal comparison**: wasn't as cheap as
- **value judgement**: the cheapest option isn't the best one

Questo è il tipo di lingua che rende i confronti utili e credibili.

## 🗣️ Guided Speaking

### A. Controlled comparison

Rispondi con una frase completa.

1. Is your city bigger or smaller than Rome?
2. Is travelling by train better than travelling by bus for you?
3. Which is more useful: learning vocabulary or doing grammar exercises?
4. What is the best place to study in your house?
5. Which season is the worst for commuting?

### B. Expand the answer

Aggiungi almeno un motivo.

Esempio:

- Travelling by train is better than travelling by bus.  
- Travelling by train is usually better than travelling by bus because it is more comfortable and more reliable.

### C. One-minute task

Parla per **60–90 secondi** su uno di questi temi:

- Two cities you know
- Two ways of travelling
- Two restaurants or cafés
- Studying at home vs studying in a library

Obiettivo:

- usa almeno **3 comparatives**
- usa almeno **1 superlative**
- usa almeno **1 structure with as ... as**

## ✍️ Writing

### Task 1 – Comparison paragraph

Scrivi un testo di **120–150 parole** dal titolo:

**Which option is better?**

Scegli uno di questi argomenti:

- city A vs city B
- hotel A vs hotel B
- travelling by train vs travelling by car
- studying online vs studying in class

Devi includere:

- almeno **4 comparatives**
- almeno **2 superlatives**
- almeno **1 irregular form** (**better**, **worse**, **best**, **worst**)
- almeno **1 structure with as ... as** o **not as ... as**
- almeno **2 reasons** per giustificare la tua opinione

### Task 2 – Make it more natural

Rivedi il testo e controlla se hai inserito:

- criteri chiari di confronto (prezzo, qualità, tempo, comfort, distanza)
- connettivi semplici come **but**, **because**, **however**, **in my opinion**
- almeno una frase che non sia solo grammaticale, ma anche valutativa
  - *It is more expensive, but it is much more reliable.*

## 🔊 Pronunciation

Fai attenzione a queste forme:

- cheaper → /ˈtʃiːpər/
- easier → /ˈiːziər/
- quieter → /ˈkwaɪətər/

- worst → /wɜːst/

### Pronunciation note

Con alcune forme come **easier**, **busier**, **quieter**, il rischio è “mangiare” una sillaba o italianizzare il suono. Meglio dire la parola in due tempi e poi accelerare:

- ea-sier
- bu-sier
- qui-e-ter

## ⚠️ Error clinic – errori tipici degli italiani

❌ more easier  
✅ easier

❌ the better hotel in town  
✅ the best hotel in town

❌ This exercise is more easy than that one.  
✅ This exercise is easier than that one.

❌ Milan is more small than Rome.  
✅ Milan is smaller than Rome.

❌ My brother is more tall than me.  
✅ My brother is taller than me.

❌ This phone is as better as the other one.  
✅ This phone is as good as the other one.

### Perché succedono questi errori?

Per tre ragioni molto comuni:

1. trasferimento diretto dall'italiano (*più facile* → *more easy*)
2. incertezza su quando usare **-er** e quando usare **more**
3. confusione tra **comparative** e **superlative**

## 🔄 Transformation drill

Trasforma seguendo l'indicazione.

1. This hotel is cheap. → (comparative)
2. The blue car is fast. → (superlative in a group of three cars)
3. My town is quiet. → (comparative with Rome)
4. This exercise is difficult. → (negative equality: not as ... as)
5. That restaurant is good. → (comparative)
6. This is a bad option. → (superlative)

Poi controlla:

- hai scelto **-er** o **more** in modo corretto?
- hai usato **the** con il superlativo?
- hai evitato forme sbagliate come **more easier** o **the better**?
