## Chunk 2 — Social, Study, Work, and Communication Vocabulary

Questo secondo chunk raccoglie il **lessico funzionale e relazionale** che diventa centrale da **B1 a B2**, ma che parte da bisogni molto concreti già presenti ad **A2**.  
Qui il focus non è solo “sapere più parole”, ma imparare a gestire situazioni reali: **studiare, lavorare, collaborare, comunicare, spiegare problemi, prendere decisioni, discutere idee e muoversi in contesti contemporanei**.

Il lessico è organizzato per aree d’uso, con una progressione interna:
- **core vocabulary** per la base operativa
- **useful combinations** per evitare traduzioni letterali
- **typical sentence patterns** per lo speaking e il writing
- **common mistakes for Italian speakers**
- blocchi **Professional** per collocations, register, nuance, tone e lexical precision

In questa versione rigenerata, il **Core vocabulary** è stato reso più leggibile e più utile per lo studio attivo tramite tabelle con:
- **word / expression**
- **significato in italiano**
- **example**

L’obiettivo non è solo capire il lessico, ma usarlo meglio in contesti reali.

- **job** = specific employment

## Chunk 2 Review — From Functional Vocabulary to Social and Professional Use

This chunk is designed to help learners move from **general communication** to **functional, purposeful English**.  
At this stage, vocabulary growth is not just about learning more words; it is about learning:
- which words naturally go together
- how different situations require different tones
- how the same idea can sound casual, neutral, or professional
- how to avoid literal translations from Italian

### Core learning goals
By working through this chunk, the learner should become more confident in:
- talking about study and work
- describing roles and responsibilities
- managing meetings, tasks, and digital tools
- writing clearer emails and messages
- expressing opinions and disagreeing diplomatically
- discussing problems, decisions, and solutions
- talking about media, society, and current issues
- handling social interaction more naturally

### High-value vocabulary habits
To make this chunk truly useful, the learner should:
1. study words in **chunks**, not in isolation
2. review **collocations** regularly
3. rewrite simple sentences in more natural English
4. note down recurring **false friends**
5. practise moving from **basic** to **professional** versions of the same message

<div class="pro-only" markdown="1">

### Professional upgrade checklist
At the higher end of this chunk, learners should start noticing:
- **issue** vs **problem**
- **role** vs **job**
- **feedback** vs **correction**
- **follow up** vs simple **ask again**
- **current** vs false-friend **actual**
- **raise awareness** vs literal translations
- **meet a deadline** vs *respect a deadline*
- **gain experience** vs *make experience*

### Final upgrade challenge
Take one simple sentence from each area and upgrade it:
- study
- work
- project communication
- email writing
- discussion
- social behaviour

Example:  
Basic: **I work in an office and send emails.**  
Professional: **I work in an office-based role and regularly communicate with clients by email.**  
More advanced: **My role is mainly office-based and involves frequent written communication with clients and internal teams.**
