## 🎯 Obiettivo

Alla fine di questa unità saprai:

- costruire un'opinione complessa in inglese in modo chiaro e convincente
- distinguere tra **claim**, **reason**, **example** e **conclusion**
- esprimere accordo, disaccordo e accordo parziale in modo naturale
- usare un linguaggio più maturo per discutere temi astratti o controversi
- partecipare a una discussione senza perdere chiarezza o controllo

---

## 🧠 Idea chiave

A livello **C2**, non basta più dire cosa pensi.
Devi saper:

- strutturare il pensiero
- graduare la forza delle tue affermazioni
- anticipare obiezioni
- rispondere senza suonare aggressivo o rigido
- guidare l'ascoltatore attraverso il tuo ragionamento

Un'argomentazione efficace non è solo una serie di opinioni.
È una **linea logica**.

---

## 🧩 La struttura base di un'argomentazione

Un buon intervento segue spesso questa sequenza:

1. **Claim** – la tua tesi
2. **Reason** – la tua motivazione
3. **Example / Evidence** – un esempio, un dato, un caso
4. **Conclusion** – la conseguenza o il punto finale

### Esempio

- **Claim:** I believe remote work should remain an option.
- **Reason:** It often improves concentration and flexibility.
- **Example:** Many employees are more productive when they can avoid commuting.
- **Conclusion:** For that reason, a fully office-based model may not always be the most efficient solution.

## 🔧 Useful language for building arguments

### Stating your position
- I believe that...
- I would argue that...
- My view is that...
- It seems to me that...
- I tend to think that...

### Giving reasons
- One reason for this is that...
- This is largely because...
- The main point is that...
- What matters here is that...

### Giving examples
- For example...
- A clear example of this is...
- This can be seen in...
- A good case in point is...

### Drawing a conclusion
- Therefore...
- As a result...
- For this reason...
- This suggests that...
- Overall, it seems fair to say that...

## ⚖️ Agreeing, partially agreeing and disagreeing

### Agreeing
- I completely agree.
- That makes perfect sense.
- I think that's a very strong point.

### Partial agreement
- I see your point, but...
- I agree up to a point.
- There is some truth in that, although...

### Disagreeing diplomatically
- I'm not sure I would go that far.
- I can see why you say that, but...
- I'm not entirely convinced that...
- I would challenge that view for two reasons.

A livello alto, il disaccordo efficace è spesso **controllato**, non aggressivo.

---

## 🧠 Strong vs cautious argument language

Confronta:

### Too absolute
- This is wrong.
- Everyone knows this is true.
- There is only one solution.

### More mature / more defensible
- I'm not convinced this is the best approach.
- There is strong evidence to suggest that this is true.
- This may be one of the most effective solutions.

Le frasi più prudenti sono spesso più forti, perché sono più credibili.

## 🔍 Linking ideas in debate

Per far avanzare il discorso in modo chiaro, usa connettori logici.

### Addition
- Furthermore,
- In addition,
- Another point worth considering is that...

### Contrast
- However,
- That said,
- On the other hand,
- Even so,

### Concession
- Although that may be true,
- While I accept that,
- Even if we assume that,

### Result
- Consequently,
- As a result,
- This leads to...

## 🧪 Example of a short argument

**Topic:** Should universities focus more on practical skills than theory?

> I would argue that universities should place greater emphasis on practical skills. One reason for this is that students increasingly need to operate in fast-changing professional environments. For example, many graduates have strong theoretical knowledge but struggle when asked to solve real-world problems. This does not mean theory is unimportant; however, a more balanced approach would better prepare students for life after university.

## ⚠️ Errori tipici degli italofoni

### 1. Opinioni troppo dirette
❌ This is absurd.

✔️ I find that position difficult to support.

### 2. Ripetizione di **I think**
❌ I think this is good and I think this is useful and I think people know it.

✔️ I believe this is useful, especially because people already recognise its value.

### 3. Mancanza di struttura
❌ I disagree because yes there are problems and also another thing is money and it is not easy.

✔️ I disagree for two main reasons: cost and practicality. First, the policy would be expensive. Second, it would be difficult to implement consistently.

### 4. Tono troppo assoluto
❌ This always happens. / Nobody thinks that.

✔️ This often happens. / Very few people would argue that.

## 🗣️ Speaking practice

Rispondi oralmente in 60–90 secondi.

1. Should working from home remain common in the future?
2. Is technology making communication better or worse?
3. Should universities be more practical or more academic?
4. Is social media more useful or more harmful?

Schema consigliato:

- state your position
- give one strong reason
- add an example
- mention a limitation or alternative view
- close clearly

---

## ✍️ Writing task

Scrivi un testo di **180–220 parole** su uno dei temi seguenti:

- Should public transport be free in large cities?
- Is remote work better than office work?
- Should schools teach more life skills?

Cerca di includere:

- una tesi chiara
- almeno due motivazioni
- un esempio concreto
- una conclusione equilibrata

## ✅ Self-check

Controlla se sai:

- esprimere una tesi con chiarezza
- sviluppare un ragionamento con ordine
- usare esempi in modo pertinente
- mostrare accordo, contrasto o concessione
- disaccordare in modo diplomatico
- evitare frasi troppo assolute o troppo semplici

Se riesci a farlo con fluidità e controllo, stai usando l'inglese in modo molto vicino al livello **C2**.
