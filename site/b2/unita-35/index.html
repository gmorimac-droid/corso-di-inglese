    Unità 35 – Discussion Skills (Agree, Disagree, Argue)

    Unità 35 – Discussion Skills (Agree, Disagree, Argue)

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità saprai:

    • esprimere accordo e disaccordo in modo più naturale
    • reagire alle opinioni degli altri senza bloccare la conversazione
    • dare una ragione semplice ma chiara per sostenere la tua idea
    • partecipare a una discussione con frasi utili e strutture riutilizzabili
    • distinguere tra accordo totale, accordo parziale e disaccordo educato
    • difendere un punto di vista in modo più maturo rispetto a un livello B1

    A livello B2 non basta più dire solo I agree oppure I don’t agree. Devi saper:

    • mostrare sfumature
    • riconoscere il punto dell’altra persona
    • aggiungere una ragione
    • sviluppare la conversazione

    🧠 Perché questa unità è importante

    Nella vita reale, discutere non significa necessariamente litigare. Molto spesso significa:

    • confrontare idee
    • valutare pro e contro
    • reagire a una proposta
    • sostenere una posizione
    • modificare un’opinione dopo aver ascoltato gli altri

    Per esempio:

    👉 livello più semplice:

    • I think it’s good.
    • I agree.

    👉 livello più maturo:

    • I see your point, but I’m not sure it would work in practice.
    • I agree to some extent, although I think the cost would be a problem.

    Questo è il passaggio chiave verso un inglese più credibile e professionale.

    📚 Agreement: come mostrare accordo

    Accordo diretto

    • I agree.
    • I agree with you.
    • You’re right.
    • That’s true.
    • Exactly.
    • Absolutely.

    Esempi

    • I agree with you. It’s a practical solution.
    • You’re right. We need more time.
    • That’s true. The current system is too slow.

    Accordo più naturale

    Per suonare meno meccanico, prova a usare una piccola espansione:

    • I agree, especially because...
    • That’s true, particularly when...
    • Exactly, and that’s why...

    Esempi

    • I agree, especially because it saves time.
    • That’s true, particularly when people work remotely.
    • Exactly, and that’s why we need a clear plan.

    📚 Partial agreement: accordo parziale

    Molto spesso in una discussione non sei totalmente d’accordo o totalmente in disaccordo. In questi casi, l’accordo parziale è molto utile.

    Frasi utili

    • I agree to some extent.
    • I see your point.
    • You may be right, but...
    • That’s true up to a point, but...
    • I understand what you mean, but...

    Esempi

    • I see your point, but I think the price is too high.
    • I agree to some extent, but the idea still needs work.
    • You may be right, but we also need to think about the deadline.

    👉 Questa è una struttura molto importante a livello B2:

    recognition + contrast

    • riconosci il punto dell’altro
    • poi introduci la tua riserva

    📚 Disagreement: come dissentire senza suonare aggressivo

    Dire semplicemente You are wrong è quasi sempre troppo diretto. Nella maggior parte delle situazioni conviene usare un disaccordo più diplomatico.

    Disaccordo soft

    • I’m not sure.
    • I don’t think so.
    • I’m not convinced.
    • I’m not sure that would be the best option.
    • I see your point, but I disagree.

    Disaccordo più diretto

    • I don’t agree.
    • I disagree.
    • I don’t think that’s correct.

    Esempi

    • I’m not sure that would work.
    • I don’t think so, because the team is already overloaded.
    • I disagree. I think we need a different approach.

    👉 In generale, più la situazione è formale o professionale, più è utile scegliere forme morbide.

    📚 Come dare ragioni e sostenere la tua idea

    Una discussione buona non si basa solo su opinioni. Serve anche una ragione chiara.

    Strutture fondamentali

    • I think... because...
    • The main reason is that...
    • This would be better because...
    • One problem is that...
    • The advantage is that...
    • The disadvantage is that...

    Esempi

    • I think this is a better option because it is cheaper.
    • The main reason is that it would save time.
    • One problem is that not everyone has the same schedule.
    • The advantage is that it would be easier to manage.

    🧩 Pattern base per argomentare meglio

    Usa questo schema semplice:

    1. Opinion

    • I think we should change the system.

    2. Reason

    • because it is too slow and confusing.

    3. Example or consequence

    • For example, customers often wait too long.

    Esempio completo

    • I think we should change the system because it is too slow. For example, customers often wait more than ten minutes.

    Questo pattern ti aiuta a non fermarti dopo una frase sola.

    💬 Useful Discussion Language

    Per esprimere la tua opinione

    • I think...
    • In my opinion...
    • From my point of view...

    Per reagire a un’opinione

    • I agree.
    • I’m not sure.
    • I see your point, but...
    • That’s true, but...

    Per aggiungere una ragione

    • because...
    • The reason is that...
    • This matters because...

    Per aggiungere un esempio

    • For example,...
    • For instance,...
    • Take remote work, for example.

    🎧 Reading / Dialogue

    Discussion topic: Working from home

    A: I think working from home is better for most people.

    B: I see your point, but I don’t think it works for everyone.

    A: Why not?

    B: Because some people focus better in an office environment.

    A: That’s true, but working from home saves time and reduces stress.

    B: I agree to some extent, especially when the commute is long.

    Cosa notare nel dialogo

    • I see your point, but... = disaccordo diplomatico
    • That’s true, but... = accordo parziale + contrasto
    • especially when... = dettaglio utile che rende l’inglese più naturale

    🗣️ Speaking Drills

    Ripeti ad alta voce:

    • I agree with you.
    • That’s true, but I still think...
    • I see your point, but I’m not convinced.
    • The main reason is that...
    • One advantage is that...
    • One problem is that...

    Ora completa oralmente:

    • I agree that ____, but...
    • I don’t think ____ because...
    • In my opinion, the best solution is ____.
    • That’s true up to a point, but...

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi una mini discussione di 120–150 parole su uno di questi temi:

    • working from home vs working in an office
    • online learning vs classroom learning
    • public transport vs private cars
    • living in a city vs living in the countryside

    Usa almeno:

    • 2 espressioni di accordo
    • 2 espressioni di disaccordo o accordo parziale
    • 3 ragioni
    • 1 esempio concreto

    🔊 Pronunciation

    Fai attenzione a queste parole:

    • agree → /əˈɡriː/
    • argue → /ˈɑːɡjuː/
    • point → /pɔɪnt/
    • reason → /ˈriːzən/
    • although → /ɔːlˈðəʊ/

    Nota utile

    Nell’inglese parlato, la frase I see your point tende a essere pronunciata in modo fluido, quasi come un unico blocco. Allenati a dirla senza pause innaturali.

    ⚠️ Errori comuni degli italiani

    ❌ I am agree
    ✅ I agree

    ❌ I don’t agree with this because is expensive
    ✅ I don’t agree with this because it is expensive

    ❌ I think yes
    ✅ I think so

    ❌ I am not agree completely
    ✅ I don’t completely agree

    ❌ I explain my opinion
    ✅ I’ll explain my point of view

    👉 Attenzione anche a non ripetere sempre I think in ogni frase. Alterna con:

    • In my opinion
    • From my point of view
    • As I see it

    🔁 Review & Practice

    Completa

    1. I ______ with you.
    2. I see your ______, but...
    3. I don’t think that would ______.
    4. The main ______ is that...
    5. One advantage is ______...
    6. That’s true up ______ point.

    Trasforma

    1. It is a good idea.
      → I agree, especially because...

    2. It is not the best option.
      → I’m not sure that...

    3. It is useful, but expensive.
      → I agree to some extent, but...

    4. We need a new plan.
      → In my opinion,... because...

    🧠 Think & Speak

    Parla per 1–2 minuti su uno di questi temi:

    • social media
    • remote work
    • university education
    • tourism
    • artificial intelligence

    Schema consigliato

    • give your opinion
    • add a reason
    • mention a possible problem
    • give a short example
    • conclude

    🚀 Mini Task

    Simula una breve discussione con due voci:

    Topic

    Should companies allow employees to work from home three days a week?

    Voice A

    • agree
    • give two reasons

    Voice B

    • partially disagree
    • mention one risk
    • suggest a compromise

    ✅ Self-check

    Prima di chiudere la lezione, chiediti:

    • so esprimere accordo senza usare sempre la stessa formula?
    • so dissentire senza essere troppo aggressivo?
    • so aggiungere una ragione chiara?
    • so usare I see your point, but... in modo naturale?
    • riesco a sostenere una mini discussione per almeno un minuto?

    Se la risposta è “quasi sempre sì”, la base della skill è pronta.

    Unità 35 – Discussion Skills (Agree, Disagree, Argue)

    🎯 Lesson Goals

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • partecipare a una discussione B2 con un ventaglio più ampio di mosse discorsive
    • esprimere accordo, accordo parziale, riserva e disaccordo con il giusto grado di forza
    • sostenere una posizione con una struttura argomentativa più solida
    • reagire a idee altrui senza interrompere il flusso della conversazione
    • negoziare significato, concedere un punto e riformulare il tuo argomento
    • gestire il tono in modo più diplomatico in contesti professionali o accademici
    • distinguere tra stating a view, challenging a view, justifying a view e modifying a view

    A livello B2, “saper discutere” non significa solo conoscere frasi come I agree o I disagree. Significa saper fare almeno quattro cose bene:

    1. take a position
    2. support it with reasons
    3. respond to another position
    4. keep the conversation cooperative

    Questa è una competenza linguistica e pragmatica allo stesso tempo.

    🧠 What makes discussion skills truly B2

    Molti studenti a livello intermedio riescono a dire la propria opinione, ma fanno ancora fatica a:

    • articolare una posizione complessa
    • mostrare sfumature
    • riconoscere il valore di un argomento contrario
    • sostenere la propria idea senza suonare rigidi o aggressivi
    • mantenere un tono collaborativo in una conversazione difficile

    Per esempio:

    Too basic

    • I agree.
    • I disagree.
    • It is good.
    • I think it is wrong.

    More B2-like

    • I can see why you’d say that, but I’m not sure the benefits would outweigh the risks.
    • I agree with the general idea, although I think the proposal is too optimistic.
    • That may be true in some cases, but I don’t think it applies here.

    La differenza non è solo di vocabolario. È una differenza di stance management.

    🧭 The spectrum: agreement is not binary

    Nelle discussioni reali, raramente siamo al 100% d’accordo o al 100% in disaccordo. È molto più comune muoversi lungo uno spettro.

    1. Full agreement

    • I completely agree.
    • I couldn’t agree more.
    • That’s exactly how I see it.
    • You’re absolutely right.

    2. General agreement with nuance

    • I agree overall.
    • I largely agree.
    • I agree with the main point.
    • On the whole, I’d agree with that.

    3. Partial agreement

    • I agree up to a point.
    • I see where you’re coming from, but...
    • There’s definitely some truth in that, but...
    • I take your point, although...

    4. Soft disagreement

    • I’m not entirely convinced.
    • I’m not sure that follows.
    • I see the logic, but I’m not persuaded.
    • I’m not sure I’d put it that way.

    5. Stronger disagreement

    • I don’t agree with that.
    • I think that view ignores an important issue.
    • I think that’s too simplistic.
    • I’d strongly disagree with that position.

    👉 Il punto chiave non è memorizzare solo le frasi. È capire il grado di forza che comunicano.

    🎚️ Hedging and softening: disagree without sounding hostile

    Un tratto molto importante del B2 è la capacità di soften disagreement.

    Perché serve

    Se dici:

    • You’re wrong.
    • That makes no sense.
    • No, that’s not true.

    il tuo inglese può risultare troppo brusco, anche se grammaticalmente corretto.

    Tecniche di softening

    1. Use uncertainty markers

    • I’m not sure...
    • I’m not convinced...
    • I don’t know if...
    • I’m not certain that...

    2. Use perspective markers

    • From my perspective...
    • As I see it...
    • I’d say that...
    • Personally, I’d argue that...

    3. Use recognition first

    • I see your point, but...
    • I understand the argument, but...
    • That’s a fair point, although...

    Examples

    • I see your point, but I don’t think the long-term impact has been considered.
    • That’s a fair point, although I’d still be worried about the cost.
    • I’m not entirely convinced that this would solve the main problem.

    🧩 The building blocks of a strong argument

    Molti studenti dicono la loro opinione ma non riescono a svilupparla. Un argomento chiaro, invece, di solito segue un percorso riconoscibile.

    A practical B2 structure

    1. Claim

    La tua posizione.

    • I think companies should allow more flexible working hours.

    2. Reason

    Perché lo pensi.

    • The main reason is that flexibility improves productivity for many employees.

    3. Evidence or example

    Un esempio concreto, reale o plausibile.

    • For example, people often work better when they can organise their tasks around their most productive hours.

    4. Consequence or implication

    Che cosa produce questa scelta.

    • As a result, they may perform better and feel less stressed.

    Full model

    • I think companies should allow more flexible working hours. The main reason is that flexibility improves productivity for many employees. For example, people often work better when they can organise their tasks around their most productive hours. As a result, both performance and well-being can improve.

    👉 Questo schema ti aiuta a evitare risposte troppo brevi o troppo frammentate.

    ⚖️ Concession: one of the most important B2 moves

    Una mossa altamente naturale nel discorso argomentativo è la concession: riconosci un punto valido, ma non cambi completamente posizione.

    Useful patterns

    • I agree that..., but...
    • While it’s true that..., I still think...
    • Although I can see the benefits, ...
    • That may be the case, but...
    • Even if that’s true, ...

    Examples

    • While it’s true that remote work offers more flexibility, I still think teams need regular face-to-face contact.
    • Although I can see the benefits, I’m concerned about communication problems.
    • That may be the case, but I don’t think it applies to every industry.

    Questa struttura ti permette di apparire più ragionevole, più sofisticato e meno rigido.

    🔗 Discussion markers that help you sound organised

    Un discorso B2 non è solo una serie di idee. È un discorso organizzato.

    To state a view

    • In my view...
    • As far as I’m concerned...
    • The way I see it...

    To add a supporting point

    • What’s more...
    • Another point is that...
    • On top of that...

    To introduce contrast

    • However,...
    • That said,...
    • At the same time,...
    • Having said that,...

    To develop an argument

    • The main issue is that...
    • What matters here is...
    • The key point is...

    To conclude

    • Overall,...
    • So, on balance,...
    • Taking everything into account,...

    🗣️ Discussion management: keeping the exchange cooperative

    Discutere bene non significa solo dire cose intelligenti. Significa anche gestire il turno di parola e il tono.

    To invite others in

    • What do you think?
    • Would you agree with that?
    • How do you see it?
    • Do you think that’s a fair summary?

    To show you are listening

    • Right, I see.
    • That makes sense.
    • I get what you mean.
    • I know what you’re saying.

    To interrupt politely

    • Sorry to interrupt, but...
    • Could I just add something here?
    • If I could come in for a second...

    To challenge an idea without attacking the person

    • I’m not sure that follows.
    • I think that may be too broad a conclusion.
    • Isn’t there a risk that...?
    • Wouldn’t that create another problem?

    🧠 Casual discussion vs professional discussion

    Il tipo di lingua che usi cambia in base al contesto.

    More casual

    • Yeah, I get that, but...
    • I’m not sure about that.
    • I kind of agree, but...

    More professional / academic

    • I understand the argument, but I’m not convinced by the evidence.
    • I can see the rationale, although I think the proposal underestimates the risks.
    • That’s a valid concern, but I’d argue that the benefits are more significant.

    A livello B2 devi cominciare a controllare questa differenza di registro in modo intenzionale.

    📖 Model Discussion

    Topic: Should universities make attendance compulsory?

    A: In my view, attendance should be compulsory, at least for most classes. The main reason is that students learn more effectively when they engage directly with the teacher and the group.

    B: I agree up to a point, but I don’t think that approach would work for everyone. Some students have jobs or long commutes, so compulsory attendance could create unnecessary pressure.

    A: That’s a fair point. However, if attendance is optional, many students may stop participating consistently, and that could affect both motivation and results.

    B: Possibly, but I think the real issue is quality. If classes are genuinely useful, students usually want to attend. Forcing them to be there doesn’t necessarily improve learning.

    A: I can see that. Still, some minimum attendance requirement might help create structure and accountability.

    B: Yes, I’d accept that more moderate position. A limited requirement sounds more realistic than full compulsory attendance.

    Why this dialogue works

    Perché contiene:

    • accordo parziale
    • concessione
    • ragioni chiare
    • contro-argomento
    • modifica della posizione
    • tono cooperativo

    Questa è una dinamica molto più B2 di un semplice scambio “yes/no”.

    🧪 Useful patterns to internalise

    Recognise + challenge

    • I understand that argument, but I think it overlooks...
    • I can see why you’d say that, but...
    • That’s true to an extent, although...

    Argue + justify

    • I’d argue that... because...
    • The main reason I say that is...
    • What makes me think that is...

    Concede + maintain position

    • Even so, I still think...
    • That may be true, but I’d still argue that...
    • Despite that, I’m not persuaded that...

    Suggest nuance

    • It depends on the context.
    • That may be true in some cases, but not in all of them.
    • I think the situation is more complex than that.

    🔊 Pronunciation and delivery

    Discussion skills non dipendono solo dalle parole. Dipendono anche da come le dici.

    Focus points

    • usa sentence stress sulle parole importanti
    • fai una breve pausa prima del contrasto
    • abbassa leggermente il tono quando vuoi dissentire in modo diplomatico
    • evita un ritmo troppo veloce quando stai costruendo un argomento complesso

    Example

    • I see your point, / but I’m not entirely convinced.

    La piccola pausa prima di but aiuta molto la chiarezza e il controllo del tono.

    ⚠️ Error Clinic for Italian Speakers

    1. I am agree

    ❌ I am agree
    ✅ I agree

    2. I don’t agree because is wrong

    ❌ I don’t agree because is wrong
    ✅ I don’t agree because it is wrong

    3. Overusing I think

    Molti studenti ripetono I think in ogni frase.

    ❌ I think this is good. I think it is useful. I think it helps people.

    ✅ In my view, this is a useful approach. One reason is that it helps people work more efficiently.

    4. Too direct disagreement

    ❌ You are wrong.

    ✅ I’m not sure I’d agree with that. ✅ I see the point, but I think the issue is more complicated.

    5. Weak argument structure

    ❌ I disagree because no.

    ✅ I disagree because the proposal doesn’t address the main problem, which is cost.

    6. Translating Italian discourse directly

    Frasi italiane come “sono d’accordo ma fino a un certo punto” o “capisco cosa vuoi dire” vanno rese con strutture naturali, non letterali.

    Meglio usare:

    • I agree up to a point.
    • I see what you mean.
    • I understand where you’re coming from.

    🔁 Controlled Practice

    Complete the sentence

    1. I agree ______ a point, but...
    2. I can see why you’d say that, ______...
    3. The main reason I think this is ______...
    4. That may be true, ______ I still think...
    5. Overall, I’d argue ______...
    6. Another point worth considering ______...

    Rewrite in a more diplomatic way

    1. You are wrong.
    2. This idea is stupid.
    3. No, that is not true.
    4. I don’t agree. It is bad.

    Expand the argument

    Start from this sentence and add: - one reason - one example - one concession - one conclusion

    Online meetings are better than face-to-face meetings.

    🗣️ Speaking Tasks

    Task 1 – One-minute position

    Speak for 1 minute on one of these topics:

    • Should remote work remain common?
    • Is university still worth the cost?
    • Are social media platforms more harmful than useful?
    • Should cities restrict car use?

    Use this structure:

    1. state your view
    2. give two reasons
    3. mention one opposite point
    4. respond to that point
    5. conclude

    Task 2 – Partial agreement drill

    Respond to each opinion using: - recognition - contrast - reason

    Example prompt:

    Public transport should always be free.

    Target response shape:

    • I can see the attraction of that idea, but...

    Task 3 – Mini debate

    Role A: defend the statement
    Companies should monitor employee productivity closely.

    Role B: challenge it without sounding hostile.

    Goal: - disagree diplomatically - use concession - ask one challenging question - suggest a more balanced alternative

    ✍️ Writing Task

    Write a short argumentative response of 180–220 words on this topic:

    Do the advantages of remote work outweigh the disadvantages?

    Include:

    • a clear position
    • at least 3 argument phrases
    • at least 2 concessive structures
    • at least 2 discourse markers for contrast or conclusion
    • one counter-argument and your response to it

    Suggested structure

    • Introduction: state your view
    • Paragraph 1: main advantage + support
    • Paragraph 2: counterpoint + response
    • Conclusion: balanced final judgement

    ✅ Self-assessment

    Check if you can now do these things confidently:

    • esprimere accordo forte, accordo parziale e disaccordo soft
    • riconoscere un punto valido prima di contestarlo
    • sostenere la tua opinione con ragioni ed esempi
    • usare concessione e contrasto in modo naturale
    • gestire una mini discussione senza sembrare rigido o aggressivo
    • suonare più credibile in un contesto professionale o accademico

    Se riesci a fare tutto questo con una certa fluidità, la tua discussion competence sta entrando davvero in area B2 alta.
