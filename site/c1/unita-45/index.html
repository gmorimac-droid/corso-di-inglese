    Unità 45 – Reported Speech (Advanced Use)

    Unità 45 – Reported Speech (Advanced Use)

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità saprai:

    • trasformare il discorso diretto in indiretto con maggiore sicurezza
    • riportare affermazioni, domande, ordini e suggerimenti
    • adattare tempi, pronomi, riferimenti di tempo e luogo
    • usare diversi reporting verbs in modo più naturale
    • raccontare conversazioni, riunioni e scambi di informazioni in modo più fluido

    🧠 Il salto da B2 a C1

    A livello B2 spesso il reported speech viene trattato come un esercizio di trasformazione:

    • “I’m tired.” → He said that he was tired.

    A livello C1 il punto non è solo cambiare la forma.

    Il punto è saper:

    • riportare il contenuto essenziale
    • scegliere il verbo più adatto
    • decidere se fare o no il backshift
    • rendere il discorso più naturale, sintetico e informativo

    In altre parole, non stai più “spostando i tempi”. Stai trasmettendo significato.

    🧩 Direct Speech vs Reported Speech

    Direct Speech

    Riporta le parole esatte.

    • She said, “I need more time.”
    • He asked, “Are you ready?”

    Reported Speech

    Riporta il contenuto del messaggio.

    • She said that she needed more time.
    • He asked if I was ready.

    Perché usare il reported speech?

    Per:

    • raccontare conversazioni
    • riferire decisioni prese in una riunione
    • sintetizzare informazioni
    • scrivere report, email e aggiornamenti professionali

    🔁 Backshifting: la regola di base

    Quando il verbo introduttivo è al passato, i tempi spesso “scendono indietro”.

    Direct Reported
    Present Simple Past Simple
    Present Continuous Past Continuous
    Past Simple Past Perfect
    Present Perfect Past Perfect
    will would
    can could
    may might

    Esempi

    • “I work from home.” → He said that he worked from home.
    • “I am waiting.” → She said that she was waiting.
    • “I finished the report.” → He said that he had finished the report.
    • “I will call you.” → She said that she would call me.

    ⚠️ Ma il backshift non è automatico al 100%

    Non sempre bisogna cambiare il tempo.

    Quando spesso NON si fa backshift

    1. Quando il contenuto è ancora vero

    • “Rome is the capital of Italy.”
    • He said that Rome is the capital of Italy.

    2. Quando vuoi mantenere il fatto come valido adesso

    • She said the office opens at 9.

    3. Quando riporti notizie o affermazioni ancora attuali

    • The spokesperson said that the company is reviewing the proposal.

    A livello alto, la scelta dipende dal punto di vista del parlante.

    🧠 Cambiamenti di pronomi, tempo e luogo

    Quando riporti qualcosa, non cambiano solo i tempi verbali.

    Pronomi

    • I → he / she
    • we → they
    • my → his / her
    • our → their

    Espressioni di tempo

    • now → then
    • today → that day
    • yesterday → the day before
    • tomorrow → the next day / the following day
    • last week → the week before
    • next month → the following month

    Espressioni di luogo

    • here → there
    • this → that
    • these → those

    Esempio completo

    • “I’m working here today.”
    • He said that he was working there that day.

    🗣️ Reporting statements

    Con le affermazioni usiamo spesso:

    • say (that)
    • tell + object + that
    • explain (that)
    • mention (that)
    • admit (that)
    • promise (that)

    Esempi

    • She said that the plan was too expensive.
    • He told me that he needed help.
    • They explained that the delay was unavoidable.
    • She admitted that she had made a mistake.
    • He promised that he would call later.

    Say vs Tell

    • say → non richiede oggetto immediato
    • tell → richiede quasi sempre una persona

    ✅ He said that he was tired. ✅ He told me that he was tired.

    ❌ He said me that...

    ❓ Reporting questions

    Yes / No questions

    Usiamo if o whether.

    • “Are you coming?”

    • He asked if I was coming.

    • “Did you finish the task?”

    • She asked whether I had finished the task.

    Wh- questions

    Manteniamo la parola interrogativa.

    • “Where do you live?”

    • He asked where I lived.

    • “Why did she leave?”

    • They asked why she had left.

    Attenzione all’ordine delle parole

    Nel reported speech non usiamo l’ordine interrogativo.

    ❌ He asked where did I live. ✅ He asked where I lived.

    🧾 Reporting commands, requests and warnings

    Per ordini, richieste e avvertimenti si usa spesso:

    • tell + object + to + verb
    • ask + object + to + verb
    • warn + object + to / not to + verb

    Esempi

    • “Close the door.” → He told me to close the door.
    • “Please send the file.” → She asked me to send the file.
    • “Don’t be late.” → He told me not to be late.
    • “Don’t touch that wire.” → She warned us not to touch that wire.

    💡 Reporting suggestions and offers

    Non tutto si riporta con say.

    Suggest

    • He suggested waiting a few more days.
    • She suggested that we postpone the meeting.

    Offer

    • He offered to help.
    • She offered to drive us to the station.

    Promise

    • They promised to send an update.
    • He promised that he would be more careful.

    🧩 Reporting verbs più utili

    Verbo Uso tipico
    say dire
    tell dire a qualcuno
    explain spiegare
    mention menzionare
    admit ammettere
    deny negare
    suggest suggerire
    warn avvertire
    promise promettere
    offer offrire
    recommend raccomandare
    insist insistere

    Esempi

    • She denied that she had copied the work.
    • He recommended taking a break.
    • They insisted that the figures were correct.

    🧠 Riportare informazioni in modo naturale

    A livello alto non riporti ogni parola.

    Spesso selezioni solo ciò che conta.

    Direct speech

    “I know we wanted to finish everything today, but I’m still waiting for two documents, so I think we should move the deadline to next Monday.”

    Natural reported version

    She explained that she was still waiting for two documents and suggested moving the deadline to the following Monday.

    Questa è la vera abilità: sintetizzare senza perdere il senso.

    🎧 Reading

    During the meeting, the manager explained that sales had improved in the previous quarter but admitted that costs were still too high. She also warned the team that some deadlines might need to be revised and suggested focusing on the most urgent tasks first.

    Comprensione

    1. What had improved?
    2. What problem still existed?
    3. What did she warn the team about?
    4. What did she suggest?

    • un problema che ti è stato spiegato

    Modello utile

    • He told me that...
    • She explained that...

    ⚠️ Errori comuni

    Errore Corretto
    He said me that... He told me that...
    He asked where did I live He asked where I lived
    She suggested to wait She suggested waiting
    He told that he was tired He said that he was tired / He told me that...
    He said that he will come He said that he would come

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa

    1. “I am busy.” → She said that she ___ busy.
    2. “Do you agree?” → He asked if I ___ .
    3. “Send me the file.” → She told me ___ the file.
    4. “Let’s wait.” → He suggested ___ .
    5. “We will help you.” → They promised they ___ help me.

    Trasforma

    1. “I can’t stay long.”
    2. “Why are you late?”
    3. “Don’t open the window.”
    4. “We finished the project yesterday.”
    5. “I think the plan is too risky.”

    Obiettivo

    Devi includere:

    • 6 reporting verbs diversi
    • 1 question report

    • 1 esempio di informazione sintetizzata, non tradotta parola per parola

    🎯 Insight finale

    A livelli alti, il reported speech non serve solo a trasformare frasi.

    Serve a:

    • riportare idee
    • gestire informazioni
    • fare sintesi
    • dare il giusto tono al messaggio

    Advanced English is not about repeating words.
    It is about reporting meaning with precision.

    Unità 45 – Reported Speech (Advanced Use)

    🎯 Lesson Goals

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • usare il reported speech come strumento di information management, non come semplice trasformazione grammaticale
    • distinguere tra faithful reporting, selective reporting e interpretive reporting
    • gestire con precisione backshift, no backshift, cambi di riferimento e sfumature di prospettiva
    • scegliere reporting verbs che trasmettono non solo il contenuto, ma anche attitude, intention, stance e speech act
    • riportare affermazioni, domande, ordini, suggerimenti, accuse, promesse, richieste e valutazioni con registro adeguato
    • sintetizzare riunioni, conversazioni, interviste, briefing e scambi complessi in un inglese più maturo e professionale

    Questa unità non serve a “cambiare i tempi correttamente”. Serve a capire how reported speech reshapes meaning.

    🧠 Beyond transformation: reported speech as interpretation

    A scuola il reported speech viene spesso presentato così:

    • “I am tired.” → He said that he was tired.

    Ma a livello C1 la vera domanda non è:

    • Can I backshift the tense correctly?

    La vera domanda è:

    • What exactly am I choosing to transmit?
    • Am I reproducing the wording, or the meaning?
    • Am I remaining neutral, summarising, distancing, softening, or interpreting?
    • What does my choice of reporting verb imply about the original speaker?

    Quando riporti il discorso, non trasferisci solo parole. Trasferisci:

    1. content → cosa è stato detto
    2. stance → come tu presenti quell’informazione
    3. evaluation → se il messaggio suona neutro, dubbio, forte, emotivo, diplomatico
    4. focus → quali elementi decidi di mettere in primo piano
    5. speaker intention → spiegare, promettere, accusare, negare, proporre, avvertire, ammettere

    Il reported speech è quindi una forma di controlled reframing.

    🧩 Direct speech, reported speech, and reporting distance

    Direct speech

    • She said, “I won’t sign this version.”

    Qui hai:

    • wording esatto
    • maggiore vividness
    • effetto più drammatico o immediato

    Neutral reported speech

    • She said that she wouldn’t sign that version.

    Qui il contenuto rimane, ma il messaggio è meno teatrale e più integrato nel discorso.

    Interpretive reporting

    • She made it clear that she was unwilling to approve the current version.

    Questa non è una semplice trasformazione. È una reframing choice: il parlante interpreta il valore pragmatico del messaggio.

    🔁 Backshift: not a rule, but a viewpoint choice

    Traditional pattern

    Quando il verbo introduttivo è al passato, spesso abbiamo:

    Direct Reported
    Present Simple Past Simple
    Present Continuous Past Continuous
    Past Simple Past Perfect
    Present Perfect Past Perfect
    will would
    can could
    may might

    Examples

    • “I work remotely.” → He said that he worked remotely.
    • “I’m waiting for a reply.” → She said that she was waiting for a reply.
    • “I finished it yesterday.” → He said that he had finished it the day before.
    • “I will call later.” → She said that she would call later.

    But at C1, backshift is not mechanical

    Il backshift segnala spesso che stai guardando il contenuto from a past reporting point.

    No-backshift, invece, può segnalare che il contenuto è:

    • ancora valido
    • presentato come ancora rilevante
    • trattato come fact rather than past statement
    • mantenuto vicino al punto di vista attuale del reporter

    Compare

    • He said that the office was on the second floor.
    • He said that the office is on the second floor.

    La seconda frase suggerisce che l’informazione è ancora vera adesso. La scelta quindi è semantica, non solo grammaticale.

    🧠 Reference shift: pronouns, time, place, perspective

    Un buon reported speech richiede controllo dei riferimenti.

    Pronouns

    • I → he / she
    • we → they
    • my → his / her
    • our → their

    Time

    • now → then
    • today → that day
    • yesterday → the day before
    • tomorrow → the next / following day
    • last week → the previous week
    • next month → the following month

    Place / deixis

    • here → there
    • this → that
    • these → those

    Example

    • “We’re meeting here tomorrow.”
    • He said that they were meeting there the following day.

    But advanced users also know that natural English sometimes keeps certain expressions unchanged if the context is still shared.

    🗣️ Reporting verbs are never neutral

    Molti studenti usano quasi solo say e tell. A livello C1 questo limita moltissimo precisione e nuance.

    Confronta:

    • He said that the figures were wrong.
    • He pointed out that the figures were wrong.
    • He complained that the figures were wrong.
    • He admitted that the figures were wrong.
    • He insisted that the figures were wrong.
    • He claimed that the figures were wrong.

    Il contenuto di base è simile, ma cambia completamente la lettura pragmatica.

    Reporting verbs by function

    Neutral / informational

    • say
    • tell
    • state
    • mention
    • report
    • note
    • explain
    • add

    Stronger stance / evaluation

    • claim
    • argue
    • insist
    • maintain
    • deny
    • admit
    • concede
    • confirm
    • reveal
    • stress
    • warn
    • urge
    • announce
    • acknowledge

    Speech-act oriented

    • promise
    • offer
    • threaten
    • suggest
    • recommend
    • advise
    • request
    • order
    • invite
    • refuse

    A C1, saper scegliere il verbo giusto significa saper codificare speaker intention con precisione.

    🧩 Grammar patterns after reporting verbs

    1. Verb + that-clause

    • She explained that the system had failed.
    • He admitted that he had overlooked the issue.
    • They claimed that the figures were accurate.

    2. Verb + object + that-clause

    • He told me that the deadline had changed.
    • She assured us that everything was under control.

    3. Verb + to-infinitive

    • She agreed to revise the draft.
    • He promised to call later.
    • They refused to comment.
    • She offered to help.

    4. Verb + object + to-infinitive

    • He told me to wait.
    • She asked us to leave early.
    • They urged the team to act quickly.
    • The doctor advised him to rest.

    5. Verb + -ing

    • He suggested waiting.
    • She denied copying the report.
    • They admitted making a mistake.
    • He recommended taking a different approach.

    6. Verb + preposition + -ing / noun

    • She apologised for arriving late.
    • He complained about having too little time.
    • They accused him of leaking the document.

    Il controllo di questi pattern è cruciale, perché il vero livello alto si vede spesso nella combinazione corretta verbo + struttura, non nella sola idea generale.

    ❓ Reporting questions: form, not just meaning

    Yes / No questions

    Usiamo if o whether:

    • “Are you available tomorrow?”
    • She asked if / whether I was available the next day.

    Wh- questions

    Manteniamo la parola interrogativa:

    • “Why did the client reject it?”
    • He asked why the client had rejected it.

    The advanced point

    Nel reported speech la domanda non mantiene la sintassi interrogativa.

    ❌ He asked why did the client reject it. ✅ He asked why the client had rejected it.

    A livelli alti, però, la vera differenza è che puoi anche reframe the question function:

    • He wanted to know why the client had rejected it.
    • She wondered whether the delay could still be avoided.
    • They questioned whether the current strategy was sustainable.

    Queste forme suonano spesso più naturali e sofisticate di un semplice asked.

    🧾 Reporting commands, requests, warnings, and pressure

    Standard patterns

    • tell + object + to + verb
    • ask + object + to + verb
    • warn + object + not to + verb
    • advise + object + to + verb
    • urge + object + to + verb

    Examples

    • She told us to leave the draft unchanged.
    • He asked me to resend the attachment.
    • They warned us not to share the file externally.
    • The consultant advised the team to simplify the process.
    • She urged him to speak to the client directly.

    Tone matters

    Confronta:

    • He told us to stop.
    • He asked us to stop.
    • He urged us to stop.
    • He warned us to stop.

    La differenza non è solo grammaticale: è una differenza di force, urgency, and stance.

    💡 Suggesting, recommending, proposing

    Uno degli errori più comuni è trattare tutti i suggerimenti allo stesso modo.

    Useful structures

    • He suggested waiting a bit longer.
    • She suggested that we postpone the launch.
    • They recommended reducing the scope.
    • He proposed moving the discussion to Friday.

    Wrong vs right

    ❌ He suggested to wait. ✅ He suggested waiting. ✅ He suggested that we wait.

    A livello C1, puoi anche usare il reported speech per compress extended discussion into one interpretive summary:

    After listening to the concerns, she suggested scaling the project back and focusing on the most realistic deliverables.

    🧠 Selective reporting vs word-by-word reporting

    Questa è una delle competenze più importanti del livello C1.

    Word-by-word tendency

    Molti studenti provano a riportare quasi tutto in sequenza, mantenendo la struttura del parlato originale. Questo produce spesso testi pesanti, ripetitivi e poco eleganti.

    Selective reporting

    Uno studente avanzato sa:

    • eliminare ridondanze
    • comprimere dettagli secondari
    • scegliere il reporting verb più informativo
    • organizzare logicamente il contenuto

    Example

    Original speech

    “I know we wanted to finish this week, but we’re still missing two approvals, and unless we get them by Friday, I don’t think we should commit to the launch date.”

    Weak report

    She said that they wanted to finish that week but that they were still missing two approvals and that if they didn’t get them by Friday, she didn’t think they should commit to the launch date.

    Stronger report

    She explained that two approvals were still missing and argued that the team should avoid committing to the launch date unless they were received by Friday.

    La seconda versione è più sintetica, più precisa e più professionale.

    🧠 Reported speech and stance

    Quando riporti, stai anche prendendo posizione rispetto a ciò che è stato detto.

    Compare

    • He said that the plan would work.
    • He claimed that the plan would work.
    • He argued that the plan would work.
    • He maintained that the plan would work.
    • He admitted that the plan might not work.

    Ogni verbo orienta il lettore in modo diverso.

    Practical consequence

    Nel writing professionale, accademico o giornalistico, la scelta del reporting verb è spesso un modo per segnalare:

    • affidabilità
    • distanza critica
    • neutralità
    • scetticismo
    • allineamento o disallineamento

    🧩 Reported speech in professional and academic settings

    Meeting summary

    • The director stated that the budget would remain unchanged.
    • Several team members pointed out that the timeline was unrealistic.
    • The client requested that the revised proposal be submitted by Monday.

    Analytical / academic tone

    • The author argues that the current framework is insufficient.
    • Recent studies have shown that the effect may be overstated.
    • It has been suggested that the results were influenced by external factors.

    News-style reporting

    • Officials confirmed that an investigation was under way.
    • Witnesses reported seeing smoke shortly before the explosion.
    • The company denied having breached any regulations.

    A C1, reported speech è anche una competenza di genre awareness.

    🎧 Reading

    In the briefing, the operations manager acknowledged that the rollout had taken longer than expected but insisted that the delays had prevented more serious problems later on. She also pointed out that several assumptions had been made too early and advised the regional teams to review the implementation schedule before committing to any new deadlines.

    Analyse the discourse

    1. Which verb shows openness about a problem?
    2. Which verb shows a defensive or assertive stance?
    3. Which phrase suggests criticism without sounding aggressive?
    4. Which verb introduces practical advice?

    🗣️ Speaking

    Task 1 — Recount a difficult conversation

    Racconta una conversazione complessa avuta al lavoro, all’università o in un contesto personale.

    Devi includere:

    • almeno 6 reporting verbs diversi

    Task 2 — Meeting summary

    Immagina di dover spiegare a un collega assente cosa è successo in una riunione.

    Obiettivo:

    • non ripetere tutto
    • organizzare le informazioni
    • segnalare accordi, obiezioni, suggerimenti e decisioni

    Useful frames:

    • She made it clear that...
    • He pointed out that...
    • They questioned whether...
    • The team agreed that...
    • She warned us not to...
    • He denied that...
    • They proposed...

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi un testo di 180–220 parole.

    Prompt

    Write a formal summary of a meeting, interview, discussion, or disagreement.

    Your text should:

    • report information accurately
    • include a range of reporting verbs
    • show clear control of time reference
    • distinguish between facts, opinions, concerns, and recommendations
    • sound natural and professionally structured

    Success criteria

    Include at least:

    • 8 different reporting structures
    • 1 example of no backshift with a clear reason
    • 1 reported question
    • 1 reported request / order / suggestion
    • 1 example where you compress long direct speech into a concise summary

    ⚠️ Error Clinic

    Errore Correzione Nota
    He said me that... He told me that... tell richiede un oggetto
    She suggested to wait She suggested waiting suggest + -ing
    He asked where did I go He asked where I had gone niente inversione interrogativa
    He said that he will come He said that he would come backshift standard
    She told that the plan was risky She said that the plan was risky / She told us that... tell richiede un destinatario
    He explained me the problem He explained the problem to me pattern del verbo
    They insisted to leave early They insisted on leaving early / insisted that we should leave early struttura diversa

    Subtle C1 pitfalls

    1. usare un reporting verb troppo forte o troppo debole rispetto al contesto
    2. fare backshift anche quando il contenuto è chiaramente ancora vero
    3. non distinguere tra reporting meaning e reporting wording
    4. produrre sequenze troppo letterali, poco sintetiche e poco idiomatiche

    ✅ Review & Practice

    A. Choose the best reporting verb

    1. He ______ that the figures had been checked twice.
      (explained / complained / denied)
    2. She ______ that she had overlooked the attachment.
      (admitted / promised / ordered)
    3. They ______ us not to share the draft outside the team.
      (warned / suggested / mentioned)
    4. He ______ whether the deadline could still be changed.
      (wondered / insisted / announced)

    B. Rewrite naturally

    Transform without sounding literal.

    1. “I’m not saying the strategy is wrong, but I think we should reconsider the timing.”
    2. “Can you send me the revised version by the end of the day?”
    3. “Don’t mention this to the client yet.”
    4. “We’ve been trying to solve the issue for weeks, but we still don’t have enough data.”

    C. Compress the message

    Turn this into one strong reported sentence:

    “I know everyone is under pressure, and I appreciate the effort, but unless we clarify responsibilities now, we’re going to run into exactly the same problem again next month.”

    🚀 Final Challenge

    Scenario

    You attended a tense strategy meeting. Different people:

    • defended the current plan
    • questioned the timeline
    • admitted some errors
    • suggested alternatives
    • warned about risks
    • asked for more data
    • promised follow-up actions

    Your task

    Write or say a clear, high-level summary of what happened.

    To succeed, you must show:

    • precision in reporting verbs
    • control of time reference
    • good balance between detail and synthesis
    • awareness of stance and tone
    • language that sounds genuinely C1, not translated word by word

    🎯 Final Insight

    At lower levels, reported speech is mostly about grammar. At C1, it becomes a tool for:

    • reframing meaning
    • managing information
    • positioning yourself toward what others said
    • writing and speaking with analytical control

    Advanced users do not merely repeat what was said.
    They decide how it should be represented.
