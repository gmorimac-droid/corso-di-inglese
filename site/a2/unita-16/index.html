    Unità 16 – Food and Shopping

    Modalità Base attiva. Questa unità ti aiuta a parlare di cibo, bevande, spesa e ordini al ristorante in inglese. Imparerai a distinguere tra nomi countable e uncountable, a usare some / any, e a fare richieste semplici in modo naturale. Per la versione estesa usa il toggle BASE / PRO nella barra in alto.

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • parlare di cibo, bevande e acquisti quotidiani
    • distinguere tra nomi countable e uncountable
    • usare some, any e a lot of in frasi semplici
    • chiedere e dire quantità di base
    • ordinare al ristorante o comprare prodotti in un negozio o supermercato

    🧠 Grammar in context

    Countable nouns

    I nomi countable si possono contare.

    • an apple
    • two apples
    • three sandwiches
    • a bottle
    • four eggs

    Possiamo usare:

    • a / an
    • numeri
    • many

    Esempi:

    • I want an apple.
    • We need three tomatoes.
    • There are many shops in this street.

    Uncountable nouns

    I nomi uncountable non si contano normalmente uno per uno.

    • water
    • milk
    • bread
    • rice
    • sugar
    • coffee
    • cheese

    Di solito non usiamo a / an.

    • I want water.
    • We need some rice.
    • There isn't any milk.

    Some / Any

    Usiamo some soprattutto nelle frasi affermative.

    • I need some bread.
    • She bought some apples.

    Usiamo any soprattutto nelle frasi negative e interrogative.

    • We don't have any coffee.
    • Do you have any eggs?

    A lot of

    A lot of si usa sia con countable sia con uncountable.

    • We bought a lot of fruit.
    • There are a lot of customers today.

    Useful question patterns

    • Can I have a coffee, please?
    • How much is it?
    • How many apples do you want?
    • Do you have any water?
    • Would you like anything else?

    🎧 Listening / Reading

    Dialogue 1 – at a café

    A: Good afternoon. What would you like? B: Can I have a sandwich and a bottle of water, please? A: Of course. Anything else? B: Yes, a coffee, please. A: Sure. That's £8.

    Dialogue 2 – at the supermarket

    A: Do we have any milk at home? B: No, we don't. We also need some bread and eggs. A: How many eggs do we need? B: A dozen, please.

    🗣️ Speaking

    Ripeti:

    • I need some bread.
    • We don't have any milk.
    • Can I have a coffee, please?
    • How much is this?
    • I'd like two apples and a bottle of water.

    👉 Ora parla di te:

    • What food do you usually buy?
    • What do you drink in the morning?
    • What do you order in a café?
    • What do you need at home today?

    👉 Fai domande:

    • Do you have any rice?
    • How many tomatoes do we need?
    • Can I pay by card?

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi 8–10 frasi su una di queste situazioni:

    1. la tua spesa settimanale
    2. un ordine al bar o al ristorante
    3. cosa c'è e cosa manca nella tua cucina

    Puoi usare questo modello:

    I usually buy some fruit, bread and milk.
    I don't buy much meat.
    At the café, I usually order a coffee and a sandwich.
    At home, we don't have any rice today.

    🔊 Pronunciation

    Attenzione a queste parole:

    • bread → /bred/
    • fruit → /fruːt/
    • juice → /dʒuːs/
    • rice → /raɪs/
    • sugar → /ˈʃʊɡə(r)/

    👉 Non leggere tutto come in italiano. In inglese molte parole comuni hanno suoni molto diversi dalla grafia italiana.

    ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

    ❌ I want a bread
    ✅ I want some bread / I want bread

    ❌ We need a milk
    ✅ We need some milk

    ❌ How much apples?
    ✅ How many apples?

    ❌ I buyed some food
    ✅ I bought some food

    ❌ I want a water
    ✅ I want some water / Can I have a bottle of water?

    🧠 Pattern fondamentali

    • some + plural / uncountable
    • any + plural / uncountable
    • How much + uncountable?
    • How many + plural countable?
    • Can I have ... , please?
    • I'd like ...

    Esempi:

    • I'd like some soup.
    • Do you have any cheese?
    • How many bananas do you want?
    • How much rice do we need?

    ❓ Domande utili

    • What would you like?
    • Can I help you?
    • Do you need anything else?
    • How much is it?
    • How many do you want?
    • Can I pay by card?

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa con some / any / much / many:

    1. We need ______ bread.
    2. Do you have ______ eggs?
    3. How ______ milk do you drink?
    4. How ______ apples do you want?
    5. There aren't ______ customers today.

    Trasforma in domanda:

    1. You have some coffee. → ______
    2. They need three bottles of water. → ______
    3. She wants some cheese. → ______

    🧠 Think & Speak

    Parla per 1 minuto:

    • cosa compri di solito al supermercato
    • cosa c'è nel tuo frigorifero
    • cosa ordini quando esci
    • quali cibi ti piacciono e quali no

    🚀 Mini task

    Simula un dialogo al ristorante.

    Ruolo A: cliente
    Ruolo B: cameriere

    Usa almeno queste espressioni:

    • Can I have ...?
    • I'd like ...
    • Anything else?
    • That's all, thank you.
    • Can I have the bill, please?

    💬 Extra practice

    Rispondi in modo completo:

    • What do you usually buy at the supermarket?
    • Do you drink much coffee?
    • How many meals do you eat a day?
    • Is there any food you never eat?

    🎯 Challenge

    Scrivi un mini-dialogo di 10–12 battute tra un cliente e un commesso oppure tra un cliente e un cameriere. Usa:

    • almeno 4 parole di cibo o bevande
    • some / any
    • una domanda con How much o How many
    • una richiesta educata con Can I have ...? o I'd like ...

    Modalità Professional attiva. In questa unità non impari solo il lessico del cibo: impari a gestire situazioni reali in cui devi fare la spesa, ordinare al ristorante, chiedere quantità, chiarire preferenze e risolvere piccoli problemi. L'obiettivo è usare con naturalezza countable / uncountable nouns, some / any / much / many / a lot of, i containers più comuni e formule di richiesta educate e credibili.

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • distinguere con sicurezza tra countable e uncountable nouns
    • usare correttamente some, any, much, many, a lot of e diversi quantity expressions
    • ordinare cibo e bevande in modo naturale e cortese
    • fare acquisti in inglese chiedendo prezzi, quantità, confezioni e disponibilità
    • evitare errori molto comuni come a bread, two informations, much apples o I'd like a water

    🧭 Language focus – food, quantity and real-life interaction

    1. Countable vs uncountable: non è solo grammatica, è significato

    I countable nouns si possono contare come unità separate:

    • an apple
    • two bananas
    • three bottles
    • a sandwich
    • several customers

    Gli uncountable nouns indicano spesso sostanze, cibi “non separati”, bevande o concetti visti come massa:

    • water
    • milk
    • bread
    • rice
    • cheese
    • sugar
    • coffee

    Questa distinzione influenza:

    • articoli (a / an)
    • plurale
    • scelta tra much e many
    • scelta tra How much e How many

    Confronta:

    • an apple / two apples
    • some rice / some cheese
    • many apples
    • much rice

    👉 In contesto reale, il problema non è solo sapere la regola, ma vedere il nome come unità o come sostanza.

    2. Quando un nome uncountable può diventare countable

    In contesti quotidiani, alcuni uncountable nouns diventano countable quando parliamo di porzioni, contenitori o tipi.

    • two coffees = two cups of coffee
    • a water = molto colloquiale in alcuni contesti, ma meglio per studenti: a bottle of water
    • three cheeses = three types of cheese

    Per un corso ben controllato, preferiamo forme più chiare:

    • a cup of coffee
    • a glass of water
    • a bottle of milk
    • a loaf of bread
    • a piece of cake

    3. Some, any, much, many, a lot of

    Some

    Usato soprattutto in frasi affermative e offerte/richieste educate.

    • We need some eggs.
    • I'd like some water.
    • Would you like some coffee?

    Any

    Usato soprattutto in domande e negazioni.

    • Do you have any fresh bread?
    • We don't have any rice left.
    • Is there any milk in the fridge?

    Much / many

    • many + countable plural

    • much + uncountable

    • How many oranges do we need?

    • How much pasta is there?

    Nel parlato quotidiano, in frasi affermative si preferisce spesso a lot of:

    • We bought a lot of vegetables.
    • She drinks a lot of coffee.

    👉 much in frase affermativa semplice spesso suona più formale o meno naturale:

    • We drink much coffee. ❌/strano
    • We drink a lot of coffee. ✅

    4. Containers and quantity expressions

    Queste strutture rendono il linguaggio molto più naturale:

    • a bottle of water
    • a carton of milk
    • a loaf of bread
    • a packet of rice
    • a kilo of apples
    • a jar of jam
    • a cup of tea
    • a slice of pizza
    • a piece of cake

    Esempi:

    • We need a carton of milk and a loaf of bread.
    • Can I have a slice of cake?
    • I'd like two bottles of water.

    5. Functional language: ordering and shopping politely

    In situazioni reali, la grammatica deve andare insieme alla cortesia.

    Formule molto utili:

    • I'd like ...
    • Can I have ... , please?
    • Could I get ...?
    • Do you have any ...?
    • How much is this?
    • How much are these?
    • That's all, thank you.
    • Can I pay by card?
    • Can I have the bill, please?

    Confronta:

    • I want a coffee. → corretto ma diretto
    • I'd like a coffee, please. → più naturale e cortese
    • Can I have a coffee, please? → molto comune

    🧠 Forme e contrasti fondamentali

    Countable

    • a tomato
    • two tomatoes
    • many tomatoes
    • How many tomatoes do we need?

    Uncountable

    • some pasta
    • much pasta
    • a lot of pasta
    • How much pasta do we need?

    Mixed use with containers

    • a bottle of water
    • three cups of coffee
    • two pieces of cake
    • a bowl of soup

    🎧 Reading – two realistic situations

    Situation 1 – supermarket

    We need to do a quick shop before dinner. There isn't any pasta at home and we only have a little rice left. Let's get some vegetables, a loaf of bread, two bottles of water and a carton of milk. We should also buy some fruit because there isn't much in the fridge.

    Situation 2 – restaurant

    Good evening. We'd like a table for two, please. Could we also have the menu? For starters, we'll have soup and a salad. For the main course, I'd like the grilled chicken with vegetables, and my friend would like the pasta. Can we also get a bottle of still water?

    🔍 Notice the language

    Osserva queste scelte:

    • there isn't any pasta → negazione con uncountable noun
    • a little rice → piccola quantità positiva
    • some vegetables → quantità non specificata, ma presente
    • there isn't much in the fridge → quantità ridotta
    • we'd like → forma più morbida e naturale di we want

    🗣️ Speaking drills

    Drill 1 – Say it naturally

    Trasforma le idee in inglese naturale:

    1. Vorrei un caffè e una fetta di torta.
    2. Non abbiamo latte a casa.
    3. Quante uova ci servono?
    4. Posso pagare con la carta?
    5. Abbiamo bisogno di una bottiglia d'acqua.

    Drill 2 – Expand the sentence

    Parti da una frase breve e rendila più naturale.

    • I want pasta.
      → I'd like some pasta, please.

    • We need milk.
      → We need a carton of milk.

    • Buy apples.
      → Could you buy a kilo of apples?

    Drill 3 – Mini role-play

    At the market

    A: Can I help you? B: Yes, I'd like ... A: How much / how many ...? B: ... please. A: Anything else? B: That's all, thank you.

    ✍️ Writing

    Task 1 – My shopping list

    Scrivi un breve testo di 120–150 parole in cui descrivi:

    • cosa devi comprare
    • cosa manca a casa
    • quali quantità ti servono
    • dove vai a fare la spesa
    • cosa compri più spesso e cosa raramente

    Cerca di usare:

    • almeno 3 uncountable nouns
    • almeno 3 countable nouns
    • some / any / a lot of
    • una domanda indiretta o una richiesta cortese

    Task 2 – At the restaurant

    Scrivi un mini-dialogo di 10–12 battute tra cliente e cameriere. Inserisci:

    • una richiesta iniziale
    • un ordine completo
    • una domanda su prezzo o disponibilità
    • una chiusura educata

    🔊 Pronunciation and fluency

    Parole da curare:

    • vegetables → /ˈvedʒtəblz/
    • chocolate → /ˈtʃɒklət/
    • water → /ˈwɔːtə(r)/
    • menu → /ˈmenjuː/
    • sugar → /ˈʃʊɡə(r)/
    • receipt → /rɪˈsiːt/

    Rhythm tip

    Nelle richieste cortesi, il ritmo è importante:

    • I'd like a coffee, please.
    • Can I have the bill, please?
    • Could we get some water?

    Dire bene il ritmo rende l'inglese più naturale anche se il livello grammaticale è ancora intermedio basso.

    ⚠️ Error clinic – Italian learners

    Errore 1: articolo con uncountable nouns

    ❌ a bread
    ✅ some bread / a loaf of bread

    ❌ a rice
    ✅ some rice / a packet of rice

    ❌ a cheese
    ✅ some cheese / a piece of cheese

    Errore 2: much / many

    ❌ much apples
    ✅ many apples

    ❌ many water
    ✅ much water / a lot of water

    Errore 3: richiesta troppo diretta

    ❌ I want a table.
    ✅ I'd like a table, please.

    ❌ Give me water.
    ✅ Can I have some water, please?

    Errore 4: quantità senza contenitore

    ❌ two breads
    ✅ two loaves of bread / two pieces of bread

    ❌ three waters
    ✅ three bottles of water

    ✅ Review & Practice

    A. Choose the correct option

    1. We don't have much / many eggs.
    2. I'd like some / any soup, please.
    3. How much / many bottles do you need?
    4. There isn't some / any milk left.
    5. She bought a lot of / much bananas.

    B. Complete the sentence

    1. Can I have ______ bottle of water, please?
    2. We need __ bread and ____ eggs.
    3. How ______ coffee do you drink?
    4. Do you have ______ vegetarian options?
    5. I'd like ______ slice of cake.

    C. Rewrite naturally

    1. I want a water.
    2. Give me the menu.
    3. We need much apples.
    4. There are not some eggs.
    5. I buyed bread yesterday.

    🧠 Self-check

    Riesci a fare tutto questo senza guardare gli appunti?

    • ordinare in un bar o ristorante in modo educato
    • distinguere How much e How many
    • usare some e any correttamente
    • parlare della spesa settimanale in 8–10 frasi
    • correggere da solo errori come a bread o much apples

    Se sì, la base A2 di questa area comunicativa è davvero solida.

    🚀 Final task

    Preparati a parlare per 90 secondi su questo tema:

    "What do you usually buy, eat and order during a normal week?"

    Nel tuo intervento prova a includere:

    • almeno 8 parole di lessico sul cibo
    • 2 countable nouns e 2 uncountable nouns
    • una frase negativa con any
    • una domanda con How much / How many
    • una richiesta educata con I'd like o Can I have

    Obiettivo: non solo usare la grammatica giusta, ma parlare in modo più fluido, realistico e credibile.
