    A1-A2

    Chunk 1 — Core Everyday Vocabulary

    Questo primo chunk raccoglie il lessico quotidiano fondamentale del corso, con una progressione che parte da A1–A2 e si estende gradualmente verso B1–B2.
    L'obiettivo non è solo imparare più parole, ma imparare a usare parole naturali in combinazioni naturali, con più controllo su significato, collocazioni, errori tipici e sfumature d’uso.

    In questa versione rigenerata: - il core vocabulary è presentato in tabelle più leggibili; - il lessico base è accompagnato da significato ed esempio breve; - restano presenti useful combinations, common mistakes e i blocchi Professional; - la struttura è pensata per essere più utile in revisione, speaking e writing.

    1. Personal Identity and Basic Personal Information

    Core vocabulary

    Word / Expression Significato Example
    name nome My name is Anna.
    surname / last name cognome My surname is Rossi.
    first name nome di battesimo Laura is my first name.
    age età What's your age?
    date of birth data di nascita Please write your date of birth.
    nationality nazionalità His nationality is Italian.
    country paese Italy is my country.
    city città I live in a big city.
    address indirizzo What's your address?
    phone number numero di telefono Can I have your phone number?
    email address indirizzo email Please give me your email address.
    job lavoro / impiego My job is quite demanding.
    student studente / studentessa She is a university student.
    language lingua English is an international language.
    beginner principiante I'm a beginner in German.
    intermediate intermedio I'm at an intermediate level.
    advanced avanzato She has an advanced level of English.

    Useful combinations

    • introduce yourself
    • spell your name
    • say your surname
    • give your email address
    • tell someone your age
    • mention your nationality
    • speak English
    • study languages
    • work as a teacher
    • live in Rome

    Typical sentence patterns

    • My name is ...
    • I'm from ...
    • I live in ...
    • I'm ... years old.
    • I work as a ...
    • I study ...
    • I speak Italian and English.

    Common mistakes for Italian speakers

    • I am 25 years → I am 25 years old
    • I live at Rome → I live in Rome
    • I work like engineer → I work as an engineer
    • I am married with → I am married to
    • I know to speak English → I can speak English / I know English

    Mini examples

    • Hi, my name is Laura and I'm from Milan.
    • I work as a project manager and I speak English every day.
    • Could you spell your surname, please?

    Professional upgrade

    At higher levels, personal information becomes more nuanced: - background - profile - professional background - area of expertise - field - sector - role - position - responsibilities - qualifications

    Higher-value expressions

    • I work in the education sector.
    • My role involves customer communication and project coordination.
    • I have a background in design and digital marketing.
    • My main area of expertise is language training.

    Nuance and register

    • job = common, everyday
    • role = more professional
    • position = often formal / HR-like
    • career = long-term professional path
    • background = education + experience + profile

    Upgrade lab

    Basic: I am a teacher.
    More natural: I work as a teacher.
    More professional: I teach English to adult learners.
    More developed: I work as an English teacher, mainly with adult learners and professionals.

    2. Family, Relationships, and Life Stages

    Core vocabulary

    Word / Expression Significato Example
    family famiglia My family is very close.
    parents genitori My parents live nearby.
    mother / mum madre / mamma My mum works at home.
    father / dad padre / papà My dad loves cooking.
    brother fratello I have one brother.
    sister sorella My sister is younger than me.
    husband marito Her husband is a doctor.
    wife moglie His wife works in finance.
    partner partner / compagno-a My partner speaks French.
    boyfriend ragazzo / fidanzato She has a new boyfriend.
    girlfriend ragazza / fidanzata His girlfriend is from Spain.
    son figlio They have a teenage son.
    daughter figlia Their daughter is ten.
    child / children bambino/a; figlio/a; bambini/e They have two children.
    relative parente We visited some relatives.
    cousin cugino/a My cousin lives abroad.
    uncle zio My uncle lives in Naples.
    aunt zia My aunt is very kind.
    nephew nipote (maschio) My nephew is five.
    niece nipote (femmina) My niece is at school.
    single single She's single at the moment.
    married sposato/a I'm married.
    divorced divorziato/a He's divorced.
    engaged fidanzato/a ufficialmente They're engaged.

    Useful combinations

    • have a brother / sister
    • get married
    • start a family
    • spend time with your family
    • be close to someone
    • get on well with someone
    • grow up in a small town
    • have children
    • raise children
    • support each other

    Typical sentence patterns

    • I have two sisters.
    • I'm married and I have one child.
    • We get on very well.
    • I grew up in a small village.
    • My parents live near me.

    Common mistakes for Italian speakers

    • I have 30 years → I am 30
    • I am married with Anna → I am married to Anna
    • I have a son of 10 years → I have a ten-year-old son / My son is ten
    • We are in four in family → There are four people in my family
    • I am an only child is correct, but do not say unique son for figlio unico

    Mini examples

    • I have a younger brother and an older sister.
    • We don't live together, but we're very close.
    • She got married last year and now lives abroad.

    Professional upgrade

    Useful vocabulary for more mature descriptions: - sibling - household - upbringing - childhood - adolescence - adulthood - elderly relatives - caregiving - family responsibilities - work-life balance

    Higher-value expressions

    • I come from a close-knit family.
    • My upbringing had a strong influence on my values.
    • Balancing work and family responsibilities can be challenging.
    • I help take care of an elderly relative.

    Nuance and lexical precision

    • childhood = the period when you were a child
    • upbringing = the way you were raised
    • household = the people living in one home
    • relative = general family member
    • sibling = brother or sister

    Common translation traps

    • education is not always educazione. In family contexts, educazione is often better translated as:
    • manners
    • the way someone was raised
    • upbringing

    3. Home, Rooms, Furniture, and Everyday Objects

    Core vocabulary

    Word / Expression Significato Example
    house casa We live in a small house.
    flat / apartment appartamento I rent a flat in the city centre.
    building edificio That building is very old.
    room stanza The room is quite bright.
    bedroom camera da letto My bedroom is upstairs.
    bathroom bagno The bathroom is next to the kitchen.
    kitchen cucina We usually eat in the kitchen.
    living room soggiorno The sofa is in the living room.
    dining room sala da pranzo We use the dining room on Sundays.
    hallway corridoio / ingresso Leave your shoes in the hallway.
    balcony balcone The flat has a small balcony.
    garden giardino They have a lovely garden.
    table tavolo The keys are on the table.
    chair sedia There are four chairs in the room.
    sofa divano I sat on the sofa.
    bed letto I need to make the bed.
    desk scrivania I work at my desk every day.
    wardrobe armadio My clothes are in the wardrobe.
    fridge frigorifero The milk is in the fridge.
    washing machine lavatrice The washing machine is in the bathroom.
    shower doccia I had a quick shower.
    keys chiavi I left my keys at home.
    charger caricatore My phone charger is missing.

    Useful combinations

    • live in a flat
    • rent a house
    • move house
    • clean the kitchen
    • make the bed
    • do the washing
    • hang clothes
    • charge your phone
    • leave your keys at home
    • sit on the sofa
    • put something on the shelf
    • tidy your room

    Typical sentence patterns

    • There is a desk in my bedroom.
    • There are two bathrooms in the house.
    • I left my keys on the table.
    • My flat is small but comfortable.
    • We moved house last summer.

    Common mistakes for Italian speakers

    • In my house there is... → There is ... in my house often sounds better
    • I have forgotten the keys at home → I left my keys at home
    • I have the phone in charge → My phone is charging / I'm charging my phone
    • I live in a home → I live in a house / flat
    • I do the bed → I make the bed

    Mini examples

    • My flat is on the third floor and it has a small balcony.
    • Could you put the bags in the kitchen?
    • I usually work at the desk in my room.

    Professional upgrade

    Vocabulary for more precise description: - spacious - cosy / cozy - bright - cluttered - tidy - messy - well-furnished - open-plan - storage space - neighbourhood / neighborhood - residential area - commute

    Useful higher-level combinations

    • a bright open-plan kitchen
    • a quiet residential area
    • limited storage space
    • a long commute to work
    • a well-connected neighbourhood

    Nuance and natural use

    • house = building / home type
    • home = emotional / personal concept
    • flat = British English
    • apartment = more common in American English
    • area is more neutral than zone in most everyday contexts

    Upgrade lab

    Basic: My house is big.
    Better: My flat is quite spacious.
    More natural: I live in a fairly spacious flat in a quiet area.
    More developed: I live in a fairly spacious flat in a quiet residential area, but the commute is quite long.

    4. Daily Routine, Time, and Frequency

    Core vocabulary

    Word / Expression Significato Example
    morning mattina I study in the morning.
    afternoon pomeriggio We have class in the afternoon.
    evening sera I usually read in the evening.
    night notte I don't go out at night.
    weekday giorno feriale On weekdays I'm very busy.
    weekend fine settimana We relax at the weekend.
    today oggi I'm busy today.
    tomorrow domani I'll call you tomorrow.
    yesterday ieri I stayed at home yesterday.
    early presto I woke up early.
    late tardi Sorry, I'm late.
    always sempre I always have breakfast.
    usually di solito I usually go by bus.
    often spesso I often study at night.
    sometimes a volte Sometimes I cook dinner.
    rarely raramente I rarely watch TV.
    hardly ever quasi mai I hardly ever go out on Mondays.
    never mai I never drink coffee at night.
    before prima I study before dinner.
    after dopo I relax after work.
    during durante I answer emails during the day.
    while mentre I listen to music while I cook.
    wake up svegliarsi I wake up at seven.
    get up alzarsi I get up quickly.
    have breakfast fare colazione I have breakfast at home.
    go to bed andare a letto I go to bed early.

    Useful combinations

    • wake up early
    • be in a hurry
    • run late
    • on weekdays
    • at the weekend
    • from Monday to Friday
    • after work
    • before dinner
    • during the day
    • once a week
    • twice a month

    Common mistakes for Italian speakers

    • I make breakfast → for eating, use have breakfast
    • I go to the bed → I go to bed
    • I do sport → I do sport / I exercise
    • At the morning → In the morning
    • At night vs in the evening: they are not interchangeable

    Mini examples

    • I usually wake up at 6:30 and go to work by bus.
    • On weekdays I don't have much free time.
    • I often study English in the evening.

    Professional upgrade

    Time and routine vocabulary for more developed speech: - routine - schedule - timetable - workload - shift - flexible hours - peak time - downtime - a packed day - a demanding schedule

    Natural higher-level combinations

    • stick to a routine
    • have a demanding schedule
    • fit something into your day
    • free up some time
    • manage your time better
    • have a fairly flexible schedule

    Nuance and precision

    • routine = repeated pattern
    • schedule = planned organization of time
    • timetable = especially transport / school / fixed schedule
    • workload = amount of work
    • downtime = time to relax / not work

    Upgrade lab

    Basic: I am very busy every day.
    Better: My days are usually quite busy.
    More natural: I have a fairly demanding schedule during the week.
    More professional: During the week I have a fairly demanding schedule, so I need to plan my time carefully.

    5. Food, Meals, and Eating Out

    Core vocabulary

    Word / Expression Significato Example
    food cibo Italian food is popular worldwide.
    meal pasto Breakfast is my smallest meal.
    breakfast colazione I have breakfast at 7:00.
    lunch pranzo We usually have lunch at work.
    dinner cena Dinner is ready.
    snack spuntino I had a snack in the afternoon.
    bread pane Can I have some bread?
    rice riso We had rice and vegetables.
    pasta pasta I like pasta very much.
    meat carne He doesn't eat much meat.
    fish pesce We had fish for dinner.
    vegetables verdure You should eat more vegetables.
    fruit frutta I usually eat fruit after lunch.
    dessert dolce The dessert was delicious.
    menu menù Could we see the menu?
    bill conto Can we have the bill, please?
    waiter / waitress / server cameriere/a The waiter was very kind.
    dish piatto This dish is quite spicy.
    starter antipasto We shared a starter.
    main course piatto principale The main course was excellent.
    side dish contorno I'd like a side dish of salad.

    Useful verbs and quantity language

    • eat
    • drink
    • cook
    • prepare
    • order
    • book a table
    • pay the bill
    • have dinner
    • try a dish
    • share a starter
    • a bottle of water
    • a glass of wine
    • a cup of tea
    • a slice of bread
    • a piece of cake
    • a bowl of soup
    • a plate of pasta

    Useful combinations

    • have lunch at work
    • cook from scratch
    • eat out
    • grab a quick bite
    • skip breakfast
    • order takeaway / takeout
    • pay separately
    • split the bill

    Common mistakes for Italian speakers

    • I take a coffee → I have a coffee / I'll have a coffee
    • I like very much pasta → I like pasta very much
    • I want water → I'd like some water is more polite
    • a bread → some bread / a loaf of bread / a piece of bread
    • I am agree with this dish → I like this dish / I'm happy with this dish

    Mini examples

    • I'd like a table for two, please.
    • Could we see the menu?
    • I usually have a light lunch and a bigger dinner.
    • We decided to split the bill.

    Professional upgrade

    More developed vocabulary: - ingredients - flavour / flavor - spicy - mild - filling - homemade - processed food - balanced diet - eating habits - dietary requirements - vegetarian / vegan / gluten-free

    Higher-level combinations

    • develop healthy eating habits
    • follow a balanced diet
    • be high in sugar
    • be low in fat
    • cater for dietary requirements
    • eat on the go
    • make healthier choices

    Nuance and lexical precision

    • dish = prepared food item
    • meal = breakfast / lunch / dinner event
    • course = part of a formal meal
    • flavour = taste quality
    • ingredient = what the food contains

    Translation traps

    • sensible in food or health contexts usually means reasonable, not sensibile
    • eventually does not mean eventualmente
    • actually does not mean attualmente

    6. Shopping, Money, and Everyday Transactions

    Core vocabulary

    Word / Expression Significato Example
    shop / store negozio There's a new shop near my house.
    supermarket supermercato I go to the supermarket every Saturday.
    market mercato We bought fruit at the market.
    pharmacy farmacia The pharmacy is on the corner.
    online shop negozio online I ordered it from an online shop.
    price prezzo The price is too high.
    cost costo / costare How much does it cost?
    money soldi / denaro I need to save money.
    cash contanti I don't have any cash.
    card carta Can I pay by card?
    receipt scontrino / ricevuta Could I have the receipt?
    discount sconto There's a discount today.
    sale saldo / offerta These shoes are on sale.
    size taglia / misura Do you have this in my size?
    brand marca I don't care about the brand.
    customer cliente The customer asked for help.
    delivery consegna Delivery is free over €50.
    return reso / restituire I'd like to return this item.
    refund rimborso Can I get a refund?

    Useful verbs

    • buy
    • pay
    • spend
    • save
    • order
    • return
    • exchange
    • try on
    • fit
    • cost
    • afford
    • compare prices
    • look for

    Useful combinations

    • pay by card
    • pay in cash
    • get a refund
    • ask for a receipt
    • try something on
    • be on sale
    • be out of stock
    • place an order
    • free delivery
    • compare products
    • save money

    Common mistakes for Italian speakers

    • How much costs it? → How much does it cost?
    • I pay with card → I pay by card / with a card
    • Can I change this? → in shops, Can I exchange this? is often better
    • I search a gift → I am looking for a gift
    • This shirt wears well → This shirt fits me well

    Mini examples

    • How much does this cost?
    • Do you have this in a larger size?
    • I'd like to return this because it doesn't fit.
    • We usually buy groceries online.

    Professional upgrade

    Extended vocabulary: - budget - affordable - overpriced - bargain - value for money - impulse purchase - subscription - purchase history - customer support - delivery window - return policy

    Useful combinations

    • stay within budget
    • offer good value for money
    • make an impulse purchase
    • contact customer support
    • check the return policy
    • track your order
    • miss the delivery window

    Nuance and register

    • cheap can sound negative
    • inexpensive / affordable are often safer
    • expensive = high price
    • overpriced = too expensive for what it is worth
    • good value = worth the money

    Upgrade lab

    Basic: It is cheap.
    Better: It's quite affordable.
    More natural: It's reasonably priced and good value for money.
    More developed: It's reasonably priced, good value for money, and probably a better option than the more expensive brands.
