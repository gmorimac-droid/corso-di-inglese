    Unità 23 – Future Forms (Will / Going to / Present Continuous)

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità saprai:

    • usare will, going to e Present Continuous per parlare del futuro
    • scegliere la forma corretta in base al contesto
    • esprimere piani, decisioni spontanee e appuntamenti organizzati
    • evitare gli errori più comuni legati alle forme future

    🧠 Grammar in context

    In inglese non esiste una sola forma del futuro. Le tre strutture più utili a questo livello sono:

    • will
    • be going to
    • Present Continuous con valore di futuro

    La scelta dipende dal significato.

    1. Will

    Usa will per:

    • decisioni prese nel momento in cui parli
    • offerte e promesse
    • previsioni personali

    Struttura

    subject + will + base form

    • I will call you later.
    • We will help you.
    • She will be here soon.

    Forma negativa

    • I will not call.
    • She won’t come.

    Domanda

    • Will you help me?
    • Will they arrive on time?

    2. Going to

    Usa be going to per:

    • piani o intenzioni già decise
    • previsioni basate su evidenze presenti

    Struttura

    subject + be + going to + base form

    • I am going to study tonight.
    • They are going to move house.
    • It is going to rain.

    Forma negativa

    • I am not going to stay.
    • We aren’t going to buy it.

    Domanda

    • Are you going to travel this summer?
    • Is she going to apply for the job?

    3. Present Continuous for future

    Usa il Present Continuous per appuntamenti e programmi già organizzati.

    Struttura

    subject + be + verb-ing

    • I am meeting Anna tomorrow.
    • We are flying to Madrid on Friday.
    • He is seeing the dentist this afternoon.

    Spesso trovi un riferimento di tempo:

    • tomorrow
    • tonight
    • next week
    • on Monday
    • this evening

    ⚖️ Differenza chiave

    Forma Uso principale
    will decisione spontanea, promessa, previsione personale
    going to piano già deciso, previsione con evidenza
    Present Continuous appuntamento o programma organizzato

    📚 Examples in context

    Decision now → will

    A: The phone is ringing. B: I’ll answer it.

    Prior plan → going to

    I’m going to start a new course next month.

    Arrangement → Present Continuous

    We’re meeting the client at 10 tomorrow.

    Prediction with evidence → going to

    Look at those clouds. It’s going to rain.

    Personal prediction → will

    I think our team will win.

    🎧 Reading / Dialogue

    A: What are you doing this weekend?
    B: I’m visiting my cousins on Saturday.

    A: Nice. Are you going to stay there all weekend?
    B: No, just one night.

    A: Oh, I forgot to book the restaurant.
    B: Don’t worry, I’ll do it now.

    A: Great. Do you think it will be busy?
    B: Yes, I think it will.

    🗣️ Speaking

    Ripeti:

    • I’ll call you later.
    • I’m going to study tonight.
    • We’re meeting at six.
    • It’s going to rain.

    Ora completa con idee tue:

    • I’ll ...
    • I’m going to ...
    • I’m ... tomorrow.
    • I think ... will ...

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi 9 frasi:

    • 3 con will
    • 3 con going to
    • 3 con Present Continuous per il futuro

    Usa almeno tre espressioni di tempo diverse.

    🔊 Pronunciation

    Nel parlato naturale si usano spesso le contrazioni:

    • I will → I’ll
    • we will → we’ll
    • I am going to → I’m going to
    • we are meeting → we’re meeting

    Nel parlato informale potresti sentire anche gonna, ma per ora è meglio saperla riconoscere prima di usarla attivamente.

    ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

    ❌ I will to call you.
    ✅ I will call you.

    ❌ I going to travel in August.
    ✅ I am going to travel in August.

    ❌ I am go tomorrow.
    ✅ I am going tomorrow.

    ❌ I will meeting my friend later.
    ✅ I am meeting my friend later.

    ❌ It will rain look at the sky.
    ✅ It’s going to rain. Look at the sky.

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Scegli la forma corretta:

    1. I think she will / is going to like the gift.
    2. We will / are going to move to a new flat next month. (già deciso)
    3. I will / am meeting Laura at 5 p.m. (appuntamento fissato)
    4. The bag looks heavy. I will / am going to help you.
    5. Look at the traffic. We will / are going to be late.

    Completa:

    1. Don’t worry. I ___ help you.
    2. We are ___ to start a new project.
    3. She is ___ the manager tomorrow morning.
    4. I think they ___ arrive late.

    🧠 Think & Speak

    Parla di:

    • tre cose che farai questa settimana
    • un piano già deciso
    • un appuntamento già organizzato
    • una decisione che puoi prendere adesso

    🚀 Mini task

    Parla per 2 minuti dei tuoi piani per i prossimi giorni.

    Cerca di usare:

    • will
    • going to
    • Present Continuous

    Real English uses different future forms for different meanings.
