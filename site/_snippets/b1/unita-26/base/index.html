    Unità 26 – Work and Study

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità saprai:

    • parlare del tuo lavoro o dei tuoi studi in modo chiaro e naturale
    • descrivere mansioni, responsabilità, compiti e obiettivi
    • rispondere a domande frequenti su esperienza, competenze e routine professionale
    • parlare di ciò che ti piace o non ti piace nel tuo lavoro o corso di studi
    • usare un inglese più credibile in contesti come networking, presentazioni personali e colloqui semplici

    🧠 Grammar in context

    In questa unità non studiamo una sola regola grammaticale isolata. L’obiettivo è combinare più strutture utili per parlare di lavoro e studio in modo realistico.

    Le strutture più utili sono:

    • Present Simple per routine e responsabilità abituali
    • Present Continuous per progetti temporanei o attività in corso
    • Present Perfect per esperienza e durata
    • Future forms per piani professionali o accademici

    Esempi

    • I work for an international company.
    • I’m currently working on a new project.
    • I have worked in customer service for three years.
    • I’m going to apply for a new position next month.

    1. Talking about your job

    Useful patterns

    • I work as a sales assistant.
    • I work in marketing.
    • I work for a small company.
    • I work with clients from different countries.
    • I’m responsible for customer support.

    Difference to remember

    • work as + professione → I work as a teacher.
    • work in + settore / area → I work in finance.
    • work for + azienda / organizzazione → I work for a multinational company.
    • work with + persone / strumenti / clienti → I work with children / data / international teams.

    2. Talking about your studies

    Useful patterns

    • I study economics at university.
    • I’m doing an online course in project management.
    • I’m in my second year.
    • I have an exam next week.
    • I’m trying to improve my English for work.

    Key vocabulary

    • degree
    • university
    • course
    • lecture
    • assignment
    • exam
    • deadline
    • research
    • subject
    • skills

    3. Responsibilities and daily tasks

    Per parlare del tuo ruolo in modo più naturale, usa verbi specifici.

    Common work verbs

    • manage
    • organise
    • analyse
    • support
    • prepare
    • design
    • teach
    • solve
    • check
    • report

    Examples

    • I manage a small team.
    • I prepare weekly reports.
    • I answer customer emails.
    • I analyse sales data.
    • I organise meetings and schedules.

    🎧 Reading / Dialogue

    Dialogue 1 – Professional introduction

    A: So, what do you do? B: I work as a project coordinator for a logistics company. A: What does your job involve? B: I organise schedules, deal with suppliers and support the operations team. A: How long have you been there? B: I’ve worked there for almost three years.

    Dialogue 2 – Talking about studies

    A: What are you studying at the moment? B: I’m studying business administration. A: Do you enjoy it? B: Yes, especially the management modules, although some subjects are quite demanding. A: What would you like to do after graduation? B: I’d like to work in human resources.

    🗣️ Speaking

    Ripeti:

    • I work as a receptionist.
    • I work in the sales department.
    • I’m responsible for customer communication.
    • I’m studying engineering.
    • I’m doing an online course in digital marketing.
    • I’d like to change job in the future.

    Ora completa a voce:

    • I work for ____.
    • I work with ____.
    • My main responsibilities are ____.
    • I’m currently studying ____.
    • The most interesting part of my job/course is ____.
    • In the future, I’d like to ____.

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi un testo di 100–120 parole su uno di questi temi:

    1. My current job
    2. My studies and future plans
    3. My work experience so far

    Cerca di includere:

    • il tuo ruolo o corso di studi
    • almeno 3 responsabilità o attività
    • qualcosa che ti piace o non ti piace
    • un piano futuro

    Example

    I work for a small company in the tourism sector. I mainly answer emails, help clients and organise bookings. I enjoy the job because it is dynamic and I speak to many people every day. However, it can be stressful during busy periods. I have worked there for two years, and now I’m doing an online English course because I’d like to take on more international tasks in the future.

    🔊 Pronunciation

    Attenzione a queste parole:

    • work → /wɜːrk/
    • world → /wɜːrld/
    • study → /ˈstʌdi/
    • career → /kəˈrɪr/
    • colleague → /ˈkɒliːɡ/
    • schedule → /ˈʃedjuːl/ o /ˈskedʒuːl/

    Allenati a dire:

    • I work in marketing.
    • I study at university.
    • I’m responsible for client communication.
    • I have worked here for three years.

    ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

    ❌ I do a work in an office
    ✅ I work in an office

    ❌ I make experience in this field
    ✅ I have experience in this field

    ❌ I study to university
    ✅ I study at university

    ❌ I am responsible to manage the team
    ✅ I am responsible for managing the team

    ❌ I have 28 years and I work from 5 years here
    ✅ I’m 28 and I have worked here for five years

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa:

    1. I work ___ a software company.
    2. I’m responsible ___ training new staff.
    3. I have worked here ___ 2022.
    4. I study law ___ university.
    5. I work ___ customers from different countries.

    Trasforma

    1. My job is in finance. → I work ____
    2. I started here three years ago and I still work here. → I have ____
    3. My role includes organising meetings. → I’m responsible ____
    4. I am a student of architecture. → I study ____

    🧠 Pattern fondamentali

    Memorizza queste strutture:

    • I work as...
    • I work in...
    • I work for...
    • I work with...
    • I’m responsible for...
    • I deal with...
    • I’m currently working on...
    • I’m studying...
    • I’d like to...

    🚀 Mini task finale

    Rispondi oralmente o per iscritto a queste domande:

    • What do you do?
    • What are your main responsibilities?
    • What do you enjoy about your work or studies?
    • What would you like to improve?
    • What are your future plans?

    Se riesci a rispondere in modo fluido e con esempi, stai già usando un inglese da vero B1 operativo.
