    Unità 45 – Reported Speech (Advanced Use)

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità saprai:

    • trasformare il discorso diretto in indiretto con maggiore sicurezza
    • riportare affermazioni, domande, ordini e suggerimenti
    • adattare tempi, pronomi, riferimenti di tempo e luogo
    • usare diversi reporting verbs in modo più naturale
    • raccontare conversazioni, riunioni e scambi di informazioni in modo più fluido

    🧠 Il salto da B2 a C1

    A livello B2 spesso il reported speech viene trattato come un esercizio di trasformazione:

    • “I’m tired.” → He said that he was tired.

    A livello C1 il punto non è solo cambiare la forma.

    Il punto è saper:

    • riportare il contenuto essenziale
    • scegliere il verbo più adatto
    • decidere se fare o no il backshift
    • rendere il discorso più naturale, sintetico e informativo

    In altre parole, non stai più “spostando i tempi”. Stai trasmettendo significato.

    🧩 Direct Speech vs Reported Speech

    Direct Speech

    Riporta le parole esatte.

    • She said, “I need more time.”
    • He asked, “Are you ready?”

    Reported Speech

    Riporta il contenuto del messaggio.

    • She said that she needed more time.
    • He asked if I was ready.

    Perché usare il reported speech?

    Per:

    • raccontare conversazioni
    • riferire decisioni prese in una riunione
    • sintetizzare informazioni
    • scrivere report, email e aggiornamenti professionali

    🔁 Backshifting: la regola di base

    Quando il verbo introduttivo è al passato, i tempi spesso “scendono indietro”.

    Direct Reported
    Present Simple Past Simple
    Present Continuous Past Continuous
    Past Simple Past Perfect
    Present Perfect Past Perfect
    will would
    can could
    may might

    Esempi

    • “I work from home.” → He said that he worked from home.
    • “I am waiting.” → She said that she was waiting.
    • “I finished the report.” → He said that he had finished the report.
    • “I will call you.” → She said that she would call me.

    ⚠️ Ma il backshift non è automatico al 100%

    Non sempre bisogna cambiare il tempo.

    Quando spesso NON si fa backshift

    1. Quando il contenuto è ancora vero

    • “Rome is the capital of Italy.”
    • He said that Rome is the capital of Italy.

    2. Quando vuoi mantenere il fatto come valido adesso

    • She said the office opens at 9.

    3. Quando riporti notizie o affermazioni ancora attuali

    • The spokesperson said that the company is reviewing the proposal.

    A livello alto, la scelta dipende dal punto di vista del parlante.

    🧠 Cambiamenti di pronomi, tempo e luogo

    Quando riporti qualcosa, non cambiano solo i tempi verbali.

    Pronomi

    • I → he / she
    • we → they
    • my → his / her
    • our → their

    Espressioni di tempo

    • now → then
    • today → that day
    • yesterday → the day before
    • tomorrow → the next day / the following day
    • last week → the week before
    • next month → the following month

    Espressioni di luogo

    • here → there
    • this → that
    • these → those

    Esempio completo

    • “I’m working here today.”
    • He said that he was working there that day.

    🗣️ Reporting statements

    Con le affermazioni usiamo spesso:

    • say (that)
    • tell + object + that
    • explain (that)
    • mention (that)
    • admit (that)
    • promise (that)

    Esempi

    • She said that the plan was too expensive.
    • He told me that he needed help.
    • They explained that the delay was unavoidable.
    • She admitted that she had made a mistake.
    • He promised that he would call later.

    Say vs Tell

    • say → non richiede oggetto immediato
    • tell → richiede quasi sempre una persona

    ✅ He said that he was tired. ✅ He told me that he was tired.

    ❌ He said me that...

    ❓ Reporting questions

    Yes / No questions

    Usiamo if o whether.

    • “Are you coming?”

    • He asked if I was coming.

    • “Did you finish the task?”

    • She asked whether I had finished the task.

    Wh- questions

    Manteniamo la parola interrogativa.

    • “Where do you live?”

    • He asked where I lived.

    • “Why did she leave?”

    • They asked why she had left.

    Attenzione all’ordine delle parole

    Nel reported speech non usiamo l’ordine interrogativo.

    ❌ He asked where did I live. ✅ He asked where I lived.

    🧾 Reporting commands, requests and warnings

    Per ordini, richieste e avvertimenti si usa spesso:

    • tell + object + to + verb
    • ask + object + to + verb
    • warn + object + to / not to + verb

    Esempi

    • “Close the door.” → He told me to close the door.
    • “Please send the file.” → She asked me to send the file.
    • “Don’t be late.” → He told me not to be late.
    • “Don’t touch that wire.” → She warned us not to touch that wire.

    💡 Reporting suggestions and offers

    Non tutto si riporta con say.

    Suggest

    • He suggested waiting a few more days.
    • She suggested that we postpone the meeting.

    Offer

    • He offered to help.
    • She offered to drive us to the station.

    Promise

    • They promised to send an update.
    • He promised that he would be more careful.

    🧩 Reporting verbs più utili

    Verbo Uso tipico
    say dire
    tell dire a qualcuno
    explain spiegare
    mention menzionare
    admit ammettere
    deny negare
    suggest suggerire
    warn avvertire
    promise promettere
    offer offrire
    recommend raccomandare
    insist insistere

    Esempi

    • She denied that she had copied the work.
    • He recommended taking a break.
    • They insisted that the figures were correct.

    🧠 Riportare informazioni in modo naturale

    A livello alto non riporti ogni parola.

    Spesso selezioni solo ciò che conta.

    Direct speech

    “I know we wanted to finish everything today, but I’m still waiting for two documents, so I think we should move the deadline to next Monday.”

    Natural reported version

    She explained that she was still waiting for two documents and suggested moving the deadline to the following Monday.

    Questa è la vera abilità: sintetizzare senza perdere il senso.

    🎧 Reading

    During the meeting, the manager explained that sales had improved in the previous quarter but admitted that costs were still too high. She also warned the team that some deadlines might need to be revised and suggested focusing on the most urgent tasks first.

    Comprensione

    1. What had improved?
    2. What problem still existed?
    3. What did she warn the team about?
    4. What did she suggest?

    🗣️ Speaking

    Racconta una conversazione o una riunione usando almeno 5 strutture di reported speech.

    Puoi parlare di:

    • una riunione di lavoro o di studio
    • una conversazione con un amico
    • un momento in cui qualcuno ti ha dato un consiglio
    • un problema che ti è stato spiegato

    Modello utile

    • He told me that...
    • She explained that...
    • They asked whether...
    • He warned us not to...
    • She suggested...

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi un testo di 140–170 parole.

    Traccia

    Racconta una riunione, una telefonata o una conversazione importante.

    Nel testo includi:

    • almeno 4 reporting verbs diversi
    • almeno 1 domanda riportata
    • almeno 1 ordine, richiesta o suggerimento riportato
    • almeno 1 esempio di scelta corretta tra backshift e non-backshift

    ⚠️ Errori comuni

    Errore Corretto
    He said me that... He told me that...
    He asked where did I live He asked where I lived
    She suggested to wait She suggested waiting
    He told that he was tired He said that he was tired / He told me that...
    He said that he will come He said that he would come

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa

    1. “I am busy.” → She said that she ___ busy.
    2. “Do you agree?” → He asked if I ___ .
    3. “Send me the file.” → She told me ___ the file.
    4. “Let’s wait.” → He suggested ___ .
    5. “We will help you.” → They promised they ___ help me.

    Trasforma

    1. “I can’t stay long.”
    2. “Why are you late?”
    3. “Don’t open the window.”
    4. “We finished the project yesterday.”
    5. “I think the plan is too risky.”

    🚀 Final Challenge

    Racconta un episodio complesso usando il reported speech.

    Obiettivo

    Devi includere:

    • 6 reporting verbs diversi
    • 1 question report
    • 1 command / request report
    • 1 suggestion
    • 1 esempio di informazione sintetizzata, non tradotta parola per parola

    🎯 Insight finale

    A livelli alti, il reported speech non serve solo a trasformare frasi.

    Serve a:

    • riportare idee
    • gestire informazioni
    • fare sintesi
    • dare il giusto tono al messaggio

    Advanced English is not about repeating words.
    It is about reporting meaning with precision.
