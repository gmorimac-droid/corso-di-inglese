    Base

    Modalità Base attiva. Questa unità ti aiuta a parlare di esperienze di vita usando il Present Perfect con have you ever...?, have / haven't e never. Imparerai a fare domande semplici, rispondere in modo naturale e distinguere tra esperienza generale e passato con data precisa. Per la versione estesa usa il toggle BASE / PRO nella barra in alto.

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • fare domande con Have you ever...?
    • parlare di esperienze personali con have / haven't
    • usare ever e never in modo corretto
    • distinguere Present Perfect e Past Simple in situazioni semplici
    • evitare errori comuni come Have you ever went?

    🧠 Grammar in context

    Present Perfect for experiences

    Usiamo il Present Perfect quando parliamo di un'esperienza nella vita, senza dire quando è successa.

    Schema:

    • have / has + past participle

    Esempi:

    • I have been to Spain.
    • She has tried Japanese food.
    • We have seen that film.
    • Have you ever visited London?

    👉 Qui l'idea importante è: esperienza nella vita, non il momento preciso.

    Questions with Have you ever...?

    Usiamo ever soprattutto nelle domande per chiedere se qualcuno ha fatto qualcosa almeno una volta nella vita.

    Schema:

    • Have you ever + past participle...?

    Esempi:

    • Have you ever been to Paris?
    • Have you ever eaten sushi?
    • Have you ever travelled alone?
    • Have you ever seen snow?

    Risposte brevi:

    • Yes, I have.
    • No, I haven't.

    Risposte più complete:

    • Yes, I have. I have been there twice.
    • No, I haven't, but I'd like to.

    Negative with never

    Usiamo never per dire che un'esperienza non è mai successa.

    • I have never tried Indian food.
    • He has never travelled by plane.
    • We have never been to Scotland.

    ⚠️ Never è già negativo, quindi non usiamo don't / didn't.

    ❌ I haven't never tried sushi. ✅ I have never tried sushi.

    Been and gone

    Per esperienze di viaggio, a livello base, la forma più utile è:

    • have been to = essere stato/a in un posto

    Esempi:

    • I have been to Rome.
    • She has been to Berlin.
    • Have you ever been to London?

    Present Perfect vs Past Simple

    Tempo Uso
    Present Perfect esperienza generale, senza tempo preciso
    Past Simple azione finita in un momento preciso

    Confronta:

    • I have been to Rome. → esperienza nella vita

    • I went to Rome last year. → dici quando

    • Have you ever eaten sushi? → esperienza generale

    • When did you eat sushi? → domanda sul momento preciso

    👉 Se dici yesterday, last year, in 2022, di solito usi il Past Simple.

    🎧 Reading / Listening

    Dialogue 1

    A: Have you ever been abroad?
    B: Yes, I have. I have been to Spain and Portugal.
    A: Really? When did you go to Portugal?
    B: I went there last summer.

    Dialogue 2

    A: Have you ever tried Korean food?
    B: No, I haven't. I have never tried it.
    A: Would you like to?
    B: Yes, definitely.

    🗣️ Speaking

    Ripeti ad alta voce:

    • Have you ever been to another country?
    • Have you ever eaten sushi?
    • Have you ever travelled alone?
    • I have never tried scuba diving.
    • Yes, I have.
    • No, I haven't.

    Ora prova con idee tue:

    • Have you ever ______?
    • I have ______.
    • I have never ______.

    Mini speaking task:

    Fai 5 domande a un compagno o rispondi da solo.

    Temi possibili:

    • food
    • travel
    • music
    • sport
    • cinema
    • study

    ✍️ Writing

    Task 1 – My experiences

    Scrivi 8 frasi:

    • 4 cose che hai fatto
    • 4 cose che non hai mai fatto

    Esempio:

    I have been to Florence.
    I have travelled by plane.
    I have seen the sea in winter.
    I have tried Japanese food.
    I have never visited New York.
    I have never driven a motorbike.
    I have never met a famous actor.
    I have never gone camping alone.

    Task 2 – Short interview

    Scrivi 5 domande con Have you ever...? e 5 possibili risposte.

    🔊 Pronunciation

    Attenzione a queste forme:

    • have → spesso debole /həv/
    • ever → /ˈevə(r)/
    • been → spesso /biːn/ oppure /bɪn/

    Chunk utili:

    • Have you ever...
    • I've never...
    • Have you been to...?

    Ripeti in modo fluido:

    • Have you ever been to London?
    • I've never tried that.
    • Have you ever spoken English abroad?

    ⚠️ Errori comuni (italiani)

    ❌ Have you ever went to Rome?
    ✅ Have you ever gone to Rome? / Have you ever been to Rome?

    ❌ I have see that film.
    ✅ I have seen that film.

    ❌ I have been in London.
    ✅ I have been to London.

    ❌ I didn't ever try sushi.
    ✅ I have never tried sushi.

    ❌ Have you ever did that?
    ✅ Have you ever done that?

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Complete the sentences

    1. Have you ever ___ (eat) sushi?
    2. I have never ___ (be) to Germany.
    3. She has ___ (see) that film.
    4. We have ___ (try) Thai food.
    5. Have they ever ___ (travel) abroad?

    Choose the correct form

    1. I have been / went to Rome.
    2. Have you ever see / seen snow?
    3. She has never try / tried Indian food.
    4. We went / have gone there last year.
    5. Have you ever been / gone to Paris?

    Transform

    1. I went to London last year. → I have ______ to London.
    2. I didn't try sushi in my life. → I have never ______ sushi.
    3. Did you ever visit Spain? → Have you ever ______ Spain?

    🧠 Think & Speak

    Parla di:

    • 3 posti che hai visitato
    • 3 esperienze che hai fatto
    • 3 cose che non hai mai fatto ma vuoi fare

    Modello utile:

    • I have been to...
    • I have tried...
    • I have never..., but I'd like to...

    🚀 Mini task

    Intervista una persona con 6 domande.

    Usa almeno questi temi:

    • travel
    • food
    • films
    • sport
    • study
    • free time

    Poi scrivi un mini report:

    Marta has been to Spain, but she has never travelled alone. She has tried sushi, but she hasn't tried Indian food.
