    Unità 05 – Present Simple

    Modalità Base attiva. Questa versione ti aiuta a usare il Present Simple per parlare di routine, abitudini e vita quotidiana. Per vedere la versione estesa usa il toggle BASE / PRO nella barra in alto.

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • parlare della tua routine quotidiana
    • descrivere abitudini semplici
    • usare il Present Simple in frasi affermative
    • usare correttamente la terza persona singolare

    🧠 Grammar in context

    Forma base

    Usiamo il Present Simple per parlare di:

    • abitudini
    • routine
    • fatti generali

    Schema:

    subject + verb

    • I work in an office.
    • You live in Rome.
    • They study English.

    Terza persona: he / she / it

    Con he / she / it normalmente aggiungiamo -s al verbo.

    • He works in a bank.
    • She lives in Milan.
    • It starts at 9.

    Esempi utili

    • I drink coffee in the morning.
    • We study English every day.
    • He works from home.
    • She eats lunch at 1.
    • My brother plays football on Saturday.

    📚 Core vocabulary

    Common verbs

    • work
    • live
    • study
    • eat
    • drink
    • go
    • like
    • play
    • start
    • finish

    Routine expressions

    • every day
    • in the morning
    • in the afternoon
    • at night
    • on Monday
    • after work
    • before dinner

    🎧 Listening / Reading

    Dialogue:

    A: What do you do? B: I work in a small office.

    A: Where do you live? B: I live in Naples.

    A: What does your sister do? B: She works in a hospital.

    A: Do you study English every day? B: Yes, I do.

    🗣️ Speaking

    Ripeti:

    • I work in an office.
    • I live in Italy.
    • I study English every evening.
    • He works in a shop.
    • She drinks tea in the morning.

    Ora completa con informazioni vere:

    • I work / study ______
    • I live in ______
    • I eat ______ for lunch.
    • I drink ______ in the morning.
    • He / She works ______

    ✍️ Writing

    Scrivi 6 frasi su di te o su una persona che conosci:

    1. I live ______
    2. I work / study ______
    3. I eat ______
    4. I drink ______
    5. I go to ______
    6. He / She works ______

    🔊 Pronunciation

    La -s finale

    Ascolta e ripeti:

    • works
    • lives
    • eats
    • plays

    👉 Non dimenticare la -s con he / she / it.

    ❌ He work in a bank.
    ✅ He works in a bank.

    ⚠️ Errori comuni

    ❌ He work every day.
    ✅ He works every day.

    ❌ She live in Rome.
    ✅ She lives in Rome.

    ❌ I am work in Milan.
    ✅ I work in Milan.

    👉 Con i verbi normali non usare “am / is / are”.

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Completa:

    1. He ___ (work) in an office.
    2. She ___ (live) in Turin.
    3. I ___ (drink) coffee.
    4. They ___ (study) English.
    5. My father ___ (play) tennis.

    Trasforma

    1. I work in a bank. → He ______
    2. I live in Rome. → She ______
    3. I drink tea. → He ______

    🚀 Mini task

    Descrivi la tua giornata in 4–5 frasi.

    Esempio:

    I wake up at 7.
    I drink coffee.
    I go to work at 8.
    I eat lunch at 1.
    I study English in the evening.

    Modalità Professional attiva. Questa versione amplia la lezione con un lavoro più solido sul Present Simple: forma affermativa, terza persona, routine reali, collocazioni frequenti, differenza tra abitudine e azione in corso, errori tipici degli italofoni, speaking drills, writing guidato e self-check finale.

    🎯 Obiettivo

    Alla fine di questa unità sarai in grado di:

    • usare il Present Simple per descrivere routine, abitudini e fatti generali
    • costruire frasi corrette con I / you / we / they e con he / she / it
    • usare in modo naturale espressioni di frequenza e tempo quotidiano
    • evitare gli errori più comuni, soprattutto sulla terza persona singolare
    • parlare in modo semplice ma credibile della tua vita quotidiana e di quella di altre persone

    🧭 Focus della lezione

    Il Present Simple è uno dei tempi più importanti dell’inglese. Non serve solo a “fare frasi facili”: serve a parlare di ciò che fai regolarmente, di come vivi, di cosa ti piace, di come lavora una persona o di come funziona qualcosa.

    In questa unità impari a:

    1. descrivere la tua routine
    2. parlare delle abitudini di un’altra persona
    3. usare la forma base del verbo senza l’aiuto di to be
    4. gestire correttamente la -s della terza persona
    5. combinare verbi e espressioni di tempo in modo naturale

    🧠 Grammar in context

    Quando usiamo il Present Simple

    Usiamo il Present Simple soprattutto per:

    1. Routine e abitudini

    • I wake up at 7.
    • She drinks coffee every morning.
    • We study English after work.

    2. Fatti generali o permanenti

    • I live in Italy.
    • He works in finance.
    • Water boils at 100°C.

    3. Gusti e preferenze

    • I like jazz.
    • She loves Italian food.
    • They prefer tea.

    Forma affermativa

    Con I / you / we / they

    Struttura:

    subject + base verb

    • I work in Milan.
    • You live near the station.
    • We study in the evening.
    • They play football on Friday.

    Con he / she / it

    Struttura:

    subject + verb + s

    • He works from home.
    • She lives in Bologna.
    • It starts at 9.

    Questa -s è obbligatoria nella lingua standard.

    Regole pratiche per la terza persona

    Nella maggior parte dei casi aggiungi -s:

    • work → works
    • live → lives
    • eat → eats
    • drink → drinks

    Con molti verbi che finiscono in -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, -o aggiungi -es:

    • watch → watches
    • finish → finishes
    • go → goes
    • do → does

    Con alcuni verbi che finiscono in consonante + y, la y cambia in -ies:

    • study → studies
    • try → tries

    Ma:

    • play → plays
    • enjoy → enjoys

    perché prima della y c’è una vocale.

    Present Simple vs Present Continuous

    A questo livello il punto chiave è capire l’idea generale:

    • Present Simple = abitudine / routine
    • Present Continuous = azione in corso adesso

    Confronto:

    • I work in a bank. → lavoro in banca / è il mio lavoro abituale

    • I’m working now. → sto lavorando adesso

    • She drinks tea every morning. → abitudine

    • She is drinking tea now. → azione in questo momento

    👉 In questa unità lavoriamo sul Present Simple, quindi su ciò che è regolare o abituale.

    📚 Core vocabulary

    Common routine verbs

    • wake up
    • get up
    • work
    • study
    • live
    • eat
    • drink
    • go
    • start
    • finish
    • read
    • watch
    • play
    • cook
    • drive

    Daily time expressions

    • every day
    • every morning
    • every evening
    • on weekdays
    • on Saturdays
    • at 7 o’clock
    • in the morning
    • in the afternoon
    • at night
    • after work
    • before dinner

    Useful collocations

    • drink coffee
    • go to work
    • go to school
    • have lunch
    • start work
    • finish work
    • study English
    • watch TV
    • play football

    🧩 Funzioni comunicative

    Con questa unità impari a:

    1. Parlare della tua routine

    • I wake up at 6:30.
    • I go to work by train.
    • I study English in the evening.

    2. Parlare delle abitudini di altre persone

    • My sister works in a hospital.
    • My father drives to work.
    • My colleague studies at night.

    3. Descrivere fatti semplici ma reali

    • We live near the city centre.
    • Our office starts at 9.
    • The lesson finishes at 6.

    4. Parlare di gusti e preferenze

    • I like tea.
    • He likes classical music.
    • She prefers small cafés.

    🎧 Listening / Reading

    Dialogue 1 – Talking about work

    A: What do you do?

    B: I work for a logistics company.

    A: Do you work in the office every day?

    B: No, I work from home two days a week.

    A: What time does your workday start?

    B: It usually starts at 8:30.

    Dialogue 2 – Talking about a relative

    A: What does your brother do?

    B: He works in a restaurant.

    A: Does he like it?

    B: Yes, he does. He likes the job, but he finishes late.

    A: Where does he live?

    B: He lives near the city centre.

    Dialogue 3 – Talking about routine

    A: Do you study English every day?

    B: Yes. I study for about thirty minutes in the evening.

    A: Do you watch videos or read books?

    B: I usually watch short videos and read simple articles.

    🗣️ Speaking

    Drill 1 – Say it about yourself

    Completa oralmente:

    • I wake up at ______.
    • I go to work / university at ______.
    • I usually eat lunch at ______.
    • I study English on ______.
    • I drink ______ in the morning.
    • I usually go to bed at ______.

    Drill 2 – Say it about another person

    Parla di una persona reale:

    • My mother works ______.
    • My friend lives ______.
    • My brother studies ______.
    • My colleague starts work at ______.
    • She likes ______.
    • He watches ______ in the evening.

    Drill 3 – Contrast I / he-she

    Trasforma oralmente:

    • I work in Rome. → He ______
    • I study in the evening. → She ______
    • I go by car. → He ______
    • I watch TV at night. → She ______

    ✍️ Writing

    Task 1 – My daily routine

    Scrivi 8 frasi sulla tua giornata tipica.

    Guida:

    1. what time you wake up
    2. what you drink or eat in the morning
    3. where you work or study
    4. what time you have lunch
    5. what you do in the afternoon
    6. what you do in the evening
    7. what you like doing after work
    8. what time you go to bed

    Task 2 – Another person’s routine

    Scrivi 6 frasi sulla routine di una persona che conosci.

    Usa almeno 4 verbi diversi e fai attenzione alla terza persona.

    🔊 Pronunciation

    The final -s matters

    La -s finale della terza persona non è un dettaglio “grafico”: fa parte della frase corretta.

    Ripeti:

    • works
    • lives
    • eats
    • drinks
    • plays
    • watches
    • finishes

    Mini contrast drill

    • I work / He works
    • I live / She lives
    • I play / He plays
    • I study / She studies

    👉 Allenati a sentire la differenza tra la forma base e quella con -s.

    ⚠️ Errori comuni degli italiani

    1. Dimenticare la -s

    ❌ He work in an office.
    ✅ He works in an office.

    2. Usare to be con un verbo normale

    ❌ I am work in Milan.
    ✅ I work in Milan.

    ❌ She is live in Turin.
    ✅ She lives in Turin.

    3. Confondere abitudine e adesso

    ❌ I am drinking coffee every morning.
    ✅ I drink coffee every morning.

    4. Dimenticare il soggetto

    ❌ Work in a bank.
    ✅ I work in a bank.

    In inglese il soggetto è normalmente espresso.

    ✅ Review & Practice

    Complete the sentences

    1. He ___ (work) in a hotel.
    2. She ___ (study) English online.
    3. My father ___ (go) to work by bus.
    4. We ___ (drink) coffee after lunch.
    5. My sister ___ (watch) TV in the evening.
    6. They ___ (live) near the station.
    7. The lesson ___ (start) at 10.
    8. Luca ___ (finish) work at 6.

    Rewrite with the third person

    1. I live in Rome. → Marta ______
    2. I study English at night. → My brother ______
    3. I go to work by car. → My father ______
    4. I watch TV after dinner. → Anna ______

    Choose the correct form

    1. He work / works in a bank.
    2. She study / studies every evening.
    3. They lives / live in Florence.
    4. My colleague go / goes to work at 8.
    5. I am drink / drink tea in the morning.

    🧠 Self-check

    Controlla se sai fare queste cose senza guardare la teoria:

    • parlare della tua routine in 6–8 frasi
    • descrivere la routine di un’altra persona
    • usare correttamente la -s con he / she / it
    • usare almeno 5 espressioni di tempo quotidiano
    • evitare frasi scorrette come I am work o He work

    Se riesci, la base del Present Simple è davvero tua.

    🚀 Mini task

    Parla o scrivi per 1 minuto sul tema:

    “My weekday routine”

    Prova a includere:

    • wake up
    • work / study
    • lunch
    • evening
    • like
    • go to bed

    Esempio breve:

    I wake up at 6:45 and I drink coffee.
    I go to work at 8.
    I have lunch at 1.
    In the evening I study English and watch videos.
    I usually go to bed at 11.
